## About

About からといって

English

Japanese

からといって is a grammar pattern in Japanese comprised of the conjunction particle から 'from', と, and the て-form of the う-Verb 言いう 'to say'. It is usually translated to 'just because (A)', and will indicate that the underlying reason of (A) does not necessarily mean that (B) is also true, or that (B) is not good.

As this grammar structure often includes a negative statement in the (B) part of the sentence, the more literal translation is closer to 'from saying that (A), doesn't mean (B)'. This simply focuses the nuance on that (A) does not equal (B), despite what one may think.

からといって may appear after verbs and い-Adjectives, or な-Adjectives and nouns that are followed by だ.

たくさん勉強べんきょうをしたからといって、テストに合格ごうかくするとは限かぎらない。

Just because you studied a lot doesn't mean that you will pass the test.

公園こうえんが広ひろいからと言いって、犬いぬの放はなし飼がいをしてもいいと言いうわけではない。

Just because the park is spacious doesn't mean that dogs are allowed off leash.

丈夫じょうぶだからといって、雑ざつに扱あつかえば必かならず壊こわれます。

Just because it is sturdy, it will definitely break if you handle it roughly.

日本人にほんじんだからといって、漢字かんじを書かけるとは限かぎらない。

Just because someone is Japanese doesn't mean that they can write kanji.

In most cases, からといって will be used for giving strong opinions, or for criticism of someone's actions or behavior.

Caution

It is not rare to see からといって abbreviated as either からとて, which is more literary, or からって, which is more colloquial.

日本にほんに２に年ねん間かん住すんでいたからとて、日本語にほんごが話はなせるとは限かぎらない。

Just because someone has lived in Japan for 2 years doesn't mean that they can speak Japanese.

暑あついからって、そんなに休憩きゅうけいばかりしていたら仕事しごとが進すすまないだろ。

If you keep taking so many breaks just because it's hot, you will never get your job done.

Please don't burst out in anger, just because you are in a bad mood.

Just because it is 'donburi', doesn't confine it to being cheap.

Just because I was comforted, doesn't mean that my mood will immediately improve.

懐なつかしいからといって、なんでもすぐに買かうのはやめてください。

Please, stop buying stuff on a whim just because you find it nostalgic.

憎にくいからといって、人ひとを殺ころしていいわけないでしょ。

Just because you hate the person, that isn't a reason for it to be okay to kill them.

トレーニングをしたからといって、必かならず試合しあいに勝かてるわけではない。

Just because you trained, it doesn't mean that you are certain to win the match.

日課にっかだからといって、機械きかい的てきにやっていては成長せいちょうしません。

Just because it is daily routine, doing it mechanically, you will not improve.

生意気なまいきだからといって、無下むげに扱あつかうわけにはいかない。お客きゃくさんだから。

You can't afford to treat him coldly just because he is impertinent. He is a customer after all...

貧まずしいからといって人ひとを差別さべつすることはこの学校がっこうで禁止きんしされている。

Discriminating against someone just because they are poor is against our school policy.

Just because you were able to get into the company doesn't mean you can relax. The real work is from here on.

お父とうさんはよく買かい物ものに行いき、安やすいからといって買かいすぎてしまい、買かった食たべ物ものを腐くさらせている。

Dad often goes shopping, buys too much just because it is cheap, and the food ends up going spoiled.

近頃ちかごろの人ひとは便利べんりだからといって不健康ふけんこうなファストフードを食たべてばかり、いいことはないと思おもいます。

Recently people are only eating unhealthy fast-food just because it is convenient. I think that is not a good thing.
