## About

About に合わせて・に合った

English

Japanese

When the る - Verb 合あわせる (sometimes 併あせる) is seen in the form 合あわせて, it conveys that something has been made to 'match (A)' or 'fit with (A)'. (A) being a noun that appears before に, followed by 合あわせて. This structure can also have the nuance of 'in accordance with (A)', highlighting that (A) is the governing factor.

It is also possible to see the う - Verb 合あう used in its past tense form, linking an (A) noun to a (B) noun. This has a similar meaning, but is closer to '(B) that has met (A)'.

In sentences that use either of these expressions, more information is usually given about why something is happening in accordance with (A), or what the purpose of (B) meeting (A) is/was.

音楽おんがくに合あわせてギターを弾ひく練習れんしゅうをしています。

I am practicing playing my guitar along with music.

相手あいてに合あわせて話はなし方かたを変かえるのは疲つかれる。

It is exhausting to change how you talk in accordance with who you are talking to.

このソファーにあったテーブルを買かおう。

Lets buy a table that matches this couch.

壁紙かべがみにあった家具かぐが欲ほしい。

I want furniture that matches the wallpaper.

Caution

With 合あわせて, sometimes the meaning is as simple as 'at the same time as (A)', or 'together with (A)'. However, (A) will still be perceived as being the primary cause/reason for whatever happens in the (B) part of the sentence.

リズムに合あわせて歌うたを歌うたう。

I sing while matching the rhythm. (I sing to the rhythm)

合図あいずに合あわせてスタートする。

To start in accordance with the signal.
