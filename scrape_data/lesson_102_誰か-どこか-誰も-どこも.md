## About

About 誰か・どこか・誰も・どこも

The constructions 誰だれか, and どこか are used when you want to ask, or answer questions about 'someone', or 'somewhere' (positive sentences). Alternatively, 'no one', or 'nowhere' based statements are made with 誰だれも, and どこも (negative sentences). Structures like this that use 誰だれ were briefly discussed in the 誰だれ grammar point.

誰だれかがいますか。

Is anyone here?

来月らいげつはどこかへ行いきますか？

Are you going somewhere next month?

俺おれの誕生日たんじょうびパーティーには誰だれも来こなかった。

No one came to my birthday party.

９時くじだからどこも開あいていない。

Since it is 9pm, nowhere is open.

Using 誰だれ, or どこ is not limited to the か and も particles. へ, に, or と may also be partnered with か or も, to give a more specific meaning.

休やすみの日ひはどこかへ行いきましたか？

Did you go anywhere during your break?

誰だれかに言いった？

Did you tell anyone? (Literally, 'did you tell it to anyone?')

誰だれかと電話でんわをしている。

He is on the phone with someone.

今日きょうはどこへも行いかない。

I won't go anywhere today. (Not even anywhere)

明日あしたは誰だれにも会あわない。

I will not meet anyone tomorrow. (Not even anyone)

私わたしは誰だれにも話はなす。

I talk to anyone. (Even anyone, but less common than でも)

As can be seen with these examples, か will always precede any other particle in a positive sentence. However, the opposite is true for も. も will always follow any other particles in negative sentences. This is because か will always attach directly to the noun (when altering the meaning of that noun), but も alters the whole phrase, not just the noun.

However, It should be noted that も is not limited to appearing in negative sentences, as can be seen with the final example.

## Examples

Examples

00:05

Sentence
Translation

「どこかに友達ともだちがいるのですか。」
「どこにもいません。」

'Do you have friends somewhere?'
'I don't have friends anywhere.'

「誰だれかにそのことを言いいましたか。」
「いいえ、誰だれにも言いっていません。」

'Did you tell someone about that?'
'No, I didn't tell anyone.'

「どこかにあるでしょう。」
「どこにもありませんよ。」

'It is probably somewhere.'
'It is not anywhere (I'm telling you).'

「紙かみを誰だれかに渡わたしましたか。」
「誰だれにも渡わたしていません。」

'Did you hand off the paper to someone?'
'I did not hand off the paper to anyone.'

「どこかへ出でかけますか。」
「どこへも行いきたくないよ。」

'Do you want to go somewhere?'
'I don't want to go anywhere, man.'

「彼かれは今いま誰だれかと話はなしていますか？」
「いいえ、誰だれとも話はなしていません。」

'Is he talking with someone now?'
'No, he is not talking with anyone.'

「どこかに行いきたい。」
「どこにも行いきたくないよ。」

'I want to go somewhere.'
'I don't want to go anywhere.'

誰だれかに言いいたいけど、まだ誰だれにも言いわない。

I want to tell someone, but I won't tell anyone yet.

「どこかへ食たべに行いきますか。」
「どこへも食たべに行いきたくありません。」

'Do you want to go eat somewhere with me?'
'I don't want to go out to eat anywhere.'

「誰だれかに言いう？」
「誰だれにも言いってはいけません。」

'Should we tell someone?'
'We must not tell anyone.'

「どこかにサッカーをしに行いこうよ。」
「雨あめが降ふっているから行いきたくない。」

'Let's go play soccer somewhere.'
'It's raining, so I don't want to go.'

「これ誰だれかにあげる？」
「誰だれにもあげるつもりないよ。」

'Will you give it to someone?'
'I don't intend to give it to anyone.'
