## About

About るところだ

The noun ところ (sometimes shortened to とこ) is often used to describe the 'place' or 'situation' in which something happens. The primary determining factor for the nuance of ところ, is the tense of the verb it follows. When it is in the non-past form, ところ means 'about to (A)', or 'on the verge of (A)'.

彼かれは手紙てがみを書かくところだ。

He is about to write a letter.

今いまから友達ともだちと会あうところです。

I'm about to meet my friend.

Caution

When the kanji form of 所ところ is used, it is often referring to an actual physical location, rather than a situation/standpoint. However, this is something that native speakers will sometimes mistake. Therefore, seeing the kanji form, and the hiragana form, are both relatively common.

いまの所ところ...

This current place…

いまのところ...

This current situation…

今いまから帰かえるところです。

I am just about to go home.

ちょうど出でかけるところだ。

I am about to leave right now.

髪かみの毛けを切きるところでした。

I was on the verge of cutting my hair. (just about to)

出でかけるところなので洗あらい物ものができない。

I am just about to go out, so I cannot do the dishes.

宿題しゅくだいをするところで、友達ともだちから電話でんわが入はいった。

Just when I was about to do my homework I got a call from a friend.

パソコンの電源でんげんを切きるところで、あの音おとを聞きいた。

Just as I was about to turn off my computer, I heard that sound.

食たべるところで、焦こげた匂においがした。

Just as I was about to eat, I smelled something burning.

番組ばんぐみが始はじまるところで赤あかちゃんが泣ないた。

Just as the show was about to start, the baby cried.

新あたらしい言語げんごを習ならい始はじめるところです。

I am about to start learning a new language.

富士山ふじさんがやっと見みえるところでまた天気てんきが悪わるくなって見みえなくなった。

Just as we were about to finally see Mt. Fuji, the weather got bad again and it disappeared from view.

友達ともだちが話はなし手てを待まっている時とき、電話でんわ：「家いえから出でるところだから、もうすぐ着つくよ。」

When a friend is waiting on the speaker, phone call: 'I am about to leave home, so I will be there soon.'

晩ばんご飯はんを食たべるところで、友達ともだちが家いえに来きた。

Just as I was about to eat dinner, my friend came to my house.
