## About

About 見える

In the same way that 聞きこえる is used to describe things that are 'audible', 見みえる can be used to describe things that are 'visible'. 見みえる itself is an intransitive verb, meaning that the object that is 'visible' will be marked with が.

この部屋へやからは富士山ふじさんが見みえます。

Mount Fuji can be seen from this room.

遠とおくに建物たてものが見みえるけどあれは何なに？

I can see a building in the distance, but what is it?

パンツが見みえるくらいズボンを下さげて、先生せんせいに怒おこられた。

My pants were sagging to the point where my underwear was visible, and I got scolded by my teacher.

When using 見みえる, (A) will always be followed by が. However, the 'way that something looks' will be marked adverbially. This means that an い-Adjective will be changed to its く form, and な-Adjectives/nouns will be followed by に.

ここからだと小ちいさく見みえるけど、あのビルは１３０ひゃくさんじゅうメートルもある。

From here, the building looks small, but it is 130 meters tall.

あの仕事しごとは大変たいへんに見みえるけど、簡単かんたんらしいよ。

That job looks difficult, but I heard that it is easy.

This usage of 見みえる will be covered more in our focused lesson on this particular grammar structure.

Fun Fact

The primary difference between 見みえる, and 見みられる (the potential form of 見みる), is that 見みえる is used to identify things that do not require the active concentration of the spectator to be seen. In other words, seeing it is unavoidable (if looking in its direction of course). However, 見みられる is used when the onlooker, is trying to concentrate on some specific object, and is referring to their 'ability' to see it.

部屋へやを暗くらくして外そとを見みたら、星ほしが見みえるよ。

If you make your room dark and look outside, you will see the stars. (Even without trying, you will see them)

部屋へやを暗くらくして外そとを見みたら、星ほしが見みられるよ。

If you make your room dark and look outside, you will see the stars. (If you focus past the clouds, and give your eyes some time to adjust)

Fun Fact

見みえる is also sometimes used to describe the 'sense' of sight, and refers to whether anything is visible at all for the speaker.

若わかい時ときに大おおきな怪我けがをしたからなにも見みえない。

Because of a big injury when I was young, I cannot see anything at all.

Sentence
Translation

「遠とおすぎるから書かかれていることが見みえないよ。」

'Because it is too far, I cannot see what is written there.'

「あの看板かんばんは大おおきいので、どこからでもよく見みえる。」

'Since that signboard is big, it can be seen from anywhere.'

「富士山ふじさんはとても大おおきいから遠とおくからでもよく見みえる！」

'Mount Fuji is very big, so it can be clearly seen even from afar!'

雨あめがやんで、今いまでははっきりと見みえるよ♪

I can see clearly now, the rain is gone♪

田舎いなかは都会とかいより明あかりが少すくないから星ほしがよく見みえる。

There is less light in the countryside than in cities, so stars are clearly visible.

目めが見みえない人ひとは聴覚ちょうかくが鋭するどい。

Blind people often have a sharp sense of hearing. (Literally - People who cannot see)

子犬こいぬは生うまれた直後ちょくごは目めが見みえない。

Puppies cannot see immediately after being born.

試験官しけんかん：「皆みなさん、黒板こくばんの文字もじがハッキリ見みえますか？」

Examiner: 'Can everybody see the letters on the blackboard well?'

「目めにごみが入はいって何なににも見みえないよ。」

'Dust got into my eye and I cannot see anything!'

「ベッドの下したなど、見みえない所ところもきれいに掃除そうじしましょう。」

'Let's also thoroughly clean places that cannot be seen, like under the bed.'

警察官けいさつかんは遠とおくからよく見みえるように反射はんしゃベストを着きている。

Police officers wear reflective vests so that they are visible from afar.

「東京とうきょうタワーの見みえる部屋へやを予約よやくしたいのですが。」

'I would like to book a room with a view of Tokyo Tower.'
