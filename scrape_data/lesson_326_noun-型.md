## About

About Noun＋型

English

Japanese

型がた, or sometimes 形がた, are two kanji that are often used as a suffix, to identify that something is 'shaped', or 'sized' according to (A). The primary reading of both of these kanji is かた, but becomes がた due to 連濁れんだく (sound changes due to conjugation). 型がた can be attached directly to the end of nouns, or to い-Adjectives. Occasionally, 型かた will be used after the case marking particle の. In these cases, because it is connected to の, and not directly to the noun, it will be read as かた, not がた.

この紙かみを星ほし形がたに切きってください。

Please cut this paper into the shape of a star.

この型かたのスマホはよく壊こわれます。

This type of smartphone breaks often.

九十きゅうじゅう年ねん代だいには犬いぬ型がたのロボットのおもちゃが人気にんきだった。

During the 90s, dog-type robots were popular.

新あたらしい型かたの自転車じてんしゃはいつ発売はつばいされますか？

When is the new model of this bike coming out?

Although there are two different kanji that can be used in this expression, 型がた will be used far more frequently than 形がた. This is primarily due to the base meanings that these kanji have. Those meanings are as follows:

型がた - Form (in regard to design, pattern, type, or model).

形がた - Shape (in regard to physical shapes only. Square, rectangle, triangle, etc).

Due to this, 型がた is used when describing any form/pattern that is abstract, while 形がた is primarily only used for describing very basic (visible) shapes.

次つぎはどんな髪かみ型がたにしよう。

What kind of hairstyle should I get next?

私わたしはハート形がたのアクセサリーが好すきです。

I like heart-shaped accessories.

Caution

Occasionally, 型がた may be read as 型けい, the 音おん読よみ (Chinese reading) of 型けい. This is primarily when it is paired with other words that also use their 音おん読よみ, or when it is not acting as a suffix, but part of a set word.

文ぶん型けいは基本きほんなのでしっかりと勉強べんきょうしておきましょう。
