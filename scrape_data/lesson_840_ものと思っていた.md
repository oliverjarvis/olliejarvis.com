## About

About ものと思っていた

English

Japanese

物ものと思おもう is made up of the noun 物もの 'thing', the case marking particle と, and the う-Verb 思おもう 'to think'. Regular translations include 'to believe that (A)', or 'to have confidence that (A)'. In a broader sense, this grammar pattern is just making use of one of the meanings of 物もの as being something abstract that the speaker can reliably determine has all of the qualities of something concrete.

When 思おもう appears in the past tense as ものと思おもっていた, the most common translation is 'as I had believed was true of (A)', or 'I was under the impression that (A)'.

As もの is a noun, this pattern may follow any word in its attributive form.

え...まだ晩御飯ばんごはん食たべてないの？てっきり帰かえりに食たべてくるものと思おもっていたから、あなたの分ぶんは作つくってないですよ。

What… You haven't eaten dinner yet? I was totally under the impression that you were going to grab something on the way back home, so I did not make anything for you.

私わたしの母ははは猫ねこが好すきではないものと思おもっていたが、聞きいてみたらただ猫ねこアレルギーがあるそうだ。

I was under the impression that my mother did not like cats, but after asking her, I found out that it's just that she is allergic to them.

ゲートボールは老人ろうじんしかやらないものと思おもっていたが、最近さいきんは若者わかものの間あいだで流行はやっているらしい。

I was under the impression that gateball was something only old people played, but it seems to be popular among young people these days.

As ものと思おもっていた highlights an impression of something that was true, contradictory statements will often appear in part (B) of the sentence following structures like が or けど.

## Examples

Examples

00:05

Sentence
Translation

てっきり漢字かんじを学まなぶのは退屈たいくつなものと思おもっていた。

I was under the impression that learning kanji would be boring. (But it is not)

犬いぬは怖こわいものと思おもっていたけど、飼かってみたら思おもったより優やさしかった。

I was under the impression that dogs were scary, but after I tried taking care of one, they turned out to be more gentle than I had thought.

お母かあさんに怒おこられるものと思おもっていたが怒おこられず、拍子抜ひょうしぬけした。

I was under the impression that my mum was angry, but she wasn’t angry, so it was an anticlimax.

子供こどもの頃ころ、森もりの中なかで迷まよった。てっきりこのまま死しぬものと思おもっていた。（が救すくわれた）

When I was a child, I lost my way in the forest. I was under the impression that I would die like this. (But I was saved)

面接めんせつに合格ごうかくするものと思おもっていたが不合格ふごうかくとなり、愕然がくぜんとしている。

I was under the impression that I had passed the interview, but since I failed I am astounded.

雷かみなりに打うたれて死しぬものと思おもっていたが、どうやら生いき残のこったようだ…。

I always used to think that if you got hit by lightning you would die, but it seems that they survived.

ワンピースは若わかい子こしか着きられないものと思おもっていたが、意外いがいと私わたしでも着きられる。

I always used to think that only young girls could wear dresses, but unexpectedly, even I can wear them.

強盗ごうとうに殺ころされるものと思おもっていたが、意外いがいにも強盗犯ごうとうはんは傷一きずひとつすらつけてこなかった。

I was under the impression that you’d be killed when getting robbed, but unexpectedly, the robber didn’t even lay a scratch on me.

雨あめの日ひはついていないものと思おもっていましたが、雨あめの日ひは雨あめの日ひなりに楽たのしいことがあります。

I always used to think that rainy days were unlucky, but rainy days are fun in their own way.

小説しょうせつ、勇者ゆうしゃ：「ドラゴンは伝説でんせつのものと思おもっていたが…｡」
小ちいさいドラゴン：「ガオー！」

Novel: 'I was under the impression that the dragons were things of legends.'
Small dragon: 'Rarrrr!'

私わたしは彼かれが拒否きょひするものと思おもっていたが、予想よそうとは逆ぎゃくに彼かれは賛成さんせいした。

I was under the impression that he would disagree, but contrary to expectations he agreed.

Ａエー：「彼女かのじょが負まけたなんて驚おどろいたな。きっと勝かつものと思おもっていたけど。」
Ｂビー：「わたしもそう思おもってた。」

A: 'Surprisingly, she lost. I was under the impression that she would win.'
B: 'I thought so too.'
