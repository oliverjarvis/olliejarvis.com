## About

About むず

むず is a combination and abbreviation of the classical auxiliary verb む, the case marking particle と, and す, from standard する verbs. む was used primarily for indicating conjecture or strong will in a similar way to the modern う or よう. Due to this, depending on the context, it may be translated as either 'I shall (A)', or 'Isn't it (A)?'

むず is almost exclusively seen in very old texts, so it is likely that the surrounding grammar will also be in forms primarily used in classical Japanese.

むず will attach to the negative stem of the verbs that they are modifying.

歩あるくとも逃にげれなむず。

You could run away by just walking.

迎むかへに人々ひとびとまうで来こむず。

People shall come to get me.

Occasionally, む will be replaced with ん, creating んず.

足あしの向むきたらむ方かたへ住いなんず。

I shall go wherever my feet take me.

日ひが沈しずむ前まえに帰かえらんず。

Let's go home before the sun sets.

むずる and むずれ are also possible to see. The former is the attributive form, while the latter is the realis, or indeterminate form.

宿やどは暗くらし、いかがせむずる。

The inn is dark, I wonder what I should do.

桜さくらは今週こんしゅうこそ咲さかむずれ。

The cherry blossoms will probably bloom this week.

Sentence
Translation

世よの流ながれに逆さからはず、静しずかに身みを引ひかむず。

Without defying the tide of the times, I shall retire quietly.

いかに世よの無常むじょうを知しるとも、誠まことの心こころだけは失うしなはじと誓ちかひ、歩あゆみ続つづけむず。

No matter how much I know the transience of this world, I vow that I will not lose my true heart, and I shall continue to journey onwards.

花はなの香かに誘さそはれしは、春はなの精せいならんず。

That which was enticed by the scent of flowers must be the spirit of spring.

この花はな、母ははに届とどけむず。

I shall deliver these flowers to my mother.

道険みちけわしくとも、志こころざしひとつにて進すすみゆかむず。

Even if the way is treacherous, I shall go on with resolve.

月つきの出でを待まちて、筆ふでを取とりて書かき記しるさむず。

Waiting for the moon to rise, I shall take up my brush and I shall make a record of it.

春はるの便たより待またれし頃ころ、そなたに文ふみを送おくらむずと思おもひ立たちぬ。

Around the time I was awaiting spring tidings, I resolved that I shall send a letter to you.

月明つきあかりのもとにて、再ふたたび会あわむずという契ちぎり、忘わすれはせぬ。

I shall not forget our vow that we shall be reunited under the light of the moon.

いかに哀かなしきことあらば、涙なみだは見みせじ。されど心こころのうちにては泣なかむず。

No matter how sorrowful things may be, I shall not show my tears. And yet within my heart I shall weep.

争あらそひを避さけ、和わをもて世よを治おさめむずは、賢かしこき者ものの道みちなり。

The one who shall avoid conflict and shall govern the world in harmony, that is the path of the wise.

たとひ明日あした命いのちつきるとも、今日けふの一歩いっぽを悔くいなく進すすまむず。

Even if it were so that my life ceased upon the morrow, I shall venture forth today’s step without remorse.

何なにを思おもひ、声こえ震ふるへたまふらんず。我われはただ、黙もだしてその背せを見送みおくりし。

I wonder what could it be that he is thinking, to make his voice tremble so? I remain merely in silence, watching his back as he left.

たとひ千ちの障さはりあらばとて、わが想おもひは止やまず、ただ君きみにこの胸むねの内うちを明あかさむず。

That there were even a thousand impediments, my affections would not cease, merely, I shall reveal the depths of my heart to you.

君きみがまなざし、かくも遠とほくを見みつめたまふは、いづこの景けいを想おもひたまふらんず。

Your gaze affixed on such a distant point, what view must it be that you are so deeply transfixed on, I wonder.
