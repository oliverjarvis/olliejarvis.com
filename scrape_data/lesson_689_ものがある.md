## About

About ものがある

English

Japanese

ものがある is an interesting grammar structure in Japanese that is used to declare that something has a particular trait of (A), according to the speaker's subjective opinion. Often, ものがある is translated as 'I feel strongly that (A)', or 'there is something (A) about (B)'. The latter meaning is very close to the literal translation here, as もの 'thing', がある 'there is' implies that the speaker strongly senses the presence of (A) within (B), even if they themselves cannot figure out why they feel that way.

ものがある is primarily seen following verbs in either their dictionary or negated forms, い-Adjectives, or な-Adjectives followed by な.

この映画えいがは人ひとの心こころを動うごかすものがある。

This film has something that moves people's hearts.

彼かれの話はなしには納得なっとくいかないものがある。

There is something about his story that I don't agree with.

自分じぶんが好すきなことを仕事しごとにできるのは、嬉うれしいものがある。

There is something delightful about being able to do what you love for a living.

子供こどもだけで川かわで遊あそばせるのは、危険きけんなものがある。

There is something dangerous about letting children play in the river alone.

As ものがある emphasizes the existence of (A) within (B), it is very common for the emphasized には to also be used in place of に when highlighting the topic.

Caution

Although 'I feel strongly that (A)' is a common interpretation of ものがある, it is important to remember that the actual thing that is generating that feeling within the speaker may either be very strong, or very weak. In other words, ものがある may be used even when something isn't obvious about (A), so long as the speaker themselves feel strongly about it.

Fun-fact

目めを見張みはるものがある is a set phrase that is a combination of 目めを見張みはる 'to be astonished', and ものがある. In most cases, this expression just means that (B) is extremely surprising or impressive.

彼かれの運動うんどう神経しんけいの良よさは目めを見張みはるものがある。

His athleticism is remarkable.

モナリザには、神秘的しんぴてきなものがあるよ。

I feel strongly that that (the) Mona Lisa is mysterious.

文房具ぶんぼうぐには魅力みりょく的てきなものがある。

I feel strongly that stationery is fascinating.

この絵えって、うまく説明せつめいできないけどすばらしいものがあるよ。

I can't explain well, but this painting gives me a strong feeling of amazement.

彼かれの遺のこした言葉ことばには考かんがえさせられるものがあった。

I felt strongly that his last words were inspiring.

売店ばいてんには全然ぜんぜん売うれないものがある。

For a shop, I feel strongly that it doesn't sell well at all.

あの店みせには、派手好はでずきにはたまらないものがある。

I can't help but feel strongly that that store is attention-seeking.

こちらのお店みせのメニューには、人ひとを引ひき付つけるものがある。

I feel strongly that this restaurant's menu will bring in people.

君きみの俳句はいくにはなんとも言いえないものがある。

(I feel strongly that) there is something about your haiku that words cannot describe.

あの商品しょうひんには、単たんなる流行りゅうこうとは言いえないものがある。

I feel strongly that those goods can't be called a simple fad.

方言ほうげんには同おなじ日本語にほんごとは思おもえないものがある。

For a dialect, I feel strongly that one could think that it is not even Japanese.

日本にほんの伝統でんとう芸能げいのうには、心こころを動うごかすものがある。

In Japanese traditional art, there is something that really moves your heart.

美味おいしいものを食たべるため、寒さむい中なか行列ぎょうれつに並ならぶのはつらいものがある。

It is very hard to stand in line in the bitter cold, just to eat something delicious. (strong judgment)
