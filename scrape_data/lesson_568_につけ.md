## About

About につけ

English

Japanese

In a similar way to につき, につけ implies that there is a something that is 'affixed' to the event or occasion of (A). However, unlike に就つき this structure is a combination of に and the conjunctive form of the る-Verb 付つける 'to affix', or 'to adhere'.

When using につけ, the most common translation will be that something is happening 'every (A)', or 'whenever (A)'. In the (B) part of the sentence, the thoughts or feelings of the speaker will often be expressed in relation to (A). Additionally, (B) will always be something that is thought to happen naturally due to (A).

This structure may appear with either verbs or nouns in their dictionary forms.

元もとカノが写うつっている写真しゃしんを見みるにつけ、胸むねが痛いたくなる。

Every time I see a picture with my ex-girlfriend, my heart aches.

この防波堤ぼうはていで釣つりをするにつけ、父親ちちおやのことを思おもい出だす。

Every time I fish on this breakwater, it reminds me of my father.

彼かれは何なにかにつけ文句もんくを言いうので、一緒いっしょにいるだけで疲つかれる。

Since he complains whenever anything happens, it's exhausting being around him.

妻つまは妊娠中にんしんちゅうなので悲かなしい思おもいをしたり嬉うれしい思おもいをしたりするにつけ、すぐに泣なき出だします。

Since my wife is pregnant, she immediately bursts out crying whenever she has sad or happy experiences.

It is also possible to see につけて, but the abbreviated form is far more common.

あの時とき先輩せんぱいに言いわれた事ことを思おもい出だすにつけて、怒いかりが込こみ上あがってきます。

Every time I remember what my senpai said to me, I get filled with anger.

彼かれと一緒いっしょに時間じかんを過すごすにつけて、なんでこんな人ひとと付つき合あっているんだろうと思おもう。

Every time I spend time with him, I think to myself, 'why am I still dating such a person'.

Caution

This is considered a fairly old-fashioned grammar structure, so will mostly only be seen in literature or shows that are emphasizing a specific time period.

Whenever we have a discussion, unfortunately we end up only fighting.

この絵えを見みるに付つけ、子供こどもの頃ころのことを思おもい出だす。

Every time I see this picture, I recall my childhood.

悪わるい噂うわさを聞きくに付つけて、だんだん不安ふあんになってきた。

Every time I hear a bad rumor, I gradually become anxious.

雨あめに付つけ雪ゆきに付つけ、どうせ仕事しごとには行いかなければならない。

Whenever it rains, whenever it snows, I somehow have to go to work.

隣となりの叔父おじさんは何なにかに付つけて文句もんくを言いう人ひとだ。

The old man next door is a person who complains whenever something (anything) happens.

何なん回かいかサークルに行いくに付つけ、緊張きんちょうしなくなってきた。

Whenever I go to the (soccer) circle, I start to lose the feeling of nervousness a little more each time.

彼かれと関かかわるに付つけて、彼かれの本当ほんとうの姿すがたが見みえてきた。

Whenever I associate with him, I start to be able to see his true appearance.

高校こうこうの時ときの制服せいふくを見みるに付つけ、あの時ときの楽たのしさを思おもい出だす。

Every time I see my uniform from my high school days, I suddenly recall how much fun I had at that time.

アグネスからの茶碗ちゃわんを見みるに付つけて、一緒いっしょに居いて、平和へいわな日々ひびを懐なつかしく感かんじる。

Whenever I see the teacup from Agness, I feel nostalgic for our peaceful days together.

息子むすこからの便たよりが届とどくに付つけ、日々ひび成長せいちょうしているのがよく分わかる。

Whenever news about my son arrives, I fully understand that he is growing day by day.

会社かいしゃの実情じつじょうを知しるに付つけ、入社にゅうしゃしたことを後悔こうかいする人ひとが続出ぞくしゅつしています。

Whenever the people who joined the company become aware of the company's circumstances, one after another they regret it.

私わたしは昔むかしから古ふるい写真しゃしんにとても興味きょうみがあった。写真しゃしんを見みるに付つけ、撮とられた人ひとは誰だれなのか、後のちにどんな生活せいかつを送おくっていたのかなど疑問ぎもんに思おもう。

Since long ago, I have been interested in old pictures. Whenever I see a photo, I wonder who the people are, what kind of life they led, etc.
