## About

About さぞ

English

Japanese

さぞ is a fairly formal adverb in Japanese that is used emphatically to highlight that the following statement is something that the speaker understands to be true. This is often in relation to the feelings or situation of another. Common translations include 'you must be very (A)', 'how (A) you must be', or 'I dare say that (A)'. 'I dare to say that (A)' is probably the closest to the literal meaning of さぞ, as the speaker is empathizing with someone else by trying to imagine the situation from their point of view.

As さぞ is an adverb, it will usually appear at the beginning of a sentence, modifying the whole phrase that follows.

あの一流いちりゅう企業きぎょうで働はたらいているなら、さぞいいところに住すんでいるんでしょう。

If you work for that prestigious company, you must live in a very nice place.

おばあさんが急きゅうに入院にゅういんされて、さぞご心配しんぱいでしょう。

Your grandmother's sudden hospitalization must be very worrisome.

あんなニコニコしている斎藤さいとうさんは初はじめて見にた。さぞ嬉うれしい出来事できごとがあったんでしょう。

I had never seen Saito-san smiling like that before. He must have had something good happen to him.

The phrase following さぞ will often finish with だろう or でしょう. This highlights the speaker's opinion about the situation as being limited by their own knowledge.

Caution

さぞ comes from the kanji 嘸さぞ, which has two alternative readings, 嘸さぞや, and 嘸さぞかし. Both 嘸さぞや, and 嘸さぞかし are occasionally used as even further emphasized versions of さぞ.

あんなに大切たいせつにしていた車くるまを盗ぬすまれたなんて、さぞや悔くやしかっただろう。

You must have been so frustrated to have such a cherished car stolen.

あの人が彼かれの父ちちだなんて、さぞかし驚おどろいただろう。

He must have been very surprised that that man was his father.

Despite being almost identical in meaning, さぞかし is used more often than さぞや.

## Examples

Sentence
Translation

「夜勤やきんの仕事しごともさぞ大変たいへんだろう。」

How tough the night shift work must be!

「長旅ながたびご苦労くろう様さまです。さぞお疲つかれでしょう。」

You went through such a long trip. You must be very tired. (How tired you must be!)

「初はじめての赤あかちゃんのお誕生たんじょうにさぞ嬉うれしいことでしょう。」

You must be very happy to have your first child. (How happy you must be to have your first child!)

「智子さとこは昇進しょうしんが見送みおくられて、さぞがっかりしたに違ちがいない。」

Not getting the promotion, Satoko must be very disappointed. (How disappointed Satoko must be after not getting the promotion!)

料理好りょうりずきの旦那だんなさんと結婚けっこんされて、さぞ幸しあわせなことでしょう。

Getting married to a husband who loves cooking, how happy you must be.

わざわざこの国くにまで来きて下くださってありがとうございます。さぞ、疲つかれたことでしょう。

Thank you for coming all the way to this country. I am certain that you must be tired.

硬かたい手紙てがみ：「ご子息しそく様さまのこと、さぞご心配しんぱいのことと思おもいます。」

Formal letter: 'You must be very worried about your son.' ('How worried you must be about your son!')

「家族かぞくが一生懸命いっしょうけんめい働はたらいているのに、あなただけ毎日まいにち遊あそんでいるだけ、さぞ楽たのしいでしょうね！」

'Even though your family is working so hard, you are just having fun, how fun it must be!'

彼かれはハンサムでお金持かねもちでジェントルマンだ。さぞ女性じょせいからも男性だんせいからもモテるだろう。

He is handsome, rich, and a gentleman. I am certain that he is popular with both men and women.

一年いちねん足たらずでＴＯＥＩＣトーイックの点数てんすうを二百点にひゃくてんも上あげるなんて、さぞ懸命けんめいに勉強べんきょうしたに違ちがいない。

Something like raising the score of your TOEIC by two hundred points in not even one year, I am certain that there is no mistake that you were studying with all your might.

看護師かんごしとして一ヶ月いっかげつに十日とおかも夜勤やきんをこなしていると聞ききました。さぞ疲つかれが溜たまっていることと存ぞんじます。

As a nurse, I heard that you have 10 night shifts in a month. I dare to say that I am aware that you must get worn out.

あなたの長女ちょうじょは以前いぜんに違法薬物いほうやくぶつや万引まんびきなどで刑務所けいむしょに入はいっていたそうですね。さぞ骨ほねが折おれたことと思おもいます。

I heard that your eldest daughter has been in jail in the past due to things like illegal drugs and shoplifting. I am certain it must have been incredibly hard.

詐欺さぎにあって無一文むいちもんになった姉あねを見みて助たすけを差さし伸のべないなんて、その弟おとうとはさぞ冷酷れいこくな人ひとに違ちがいない。

Something like not reaching out to help your sister despite seeing her penniless after being duped, I dare say that there is no mistake that that brother is a cruel person.
