## About

About ぬ

English

Japanese

ぬ is the attributive form of ず, a classical auxiliary verb that is used for negation like ない, and connects to verbs in the same way.

As ぬ is the attributive form of ず, it may be seen modifying nouns that directly follow it. However, this use is not common in modern times, and in many cases it will be used as part of set expressions that have not changed in the time since ない became the far more widely used structure for negation. Alternatively, it may just be used for artistic expression, or to make a character seem 'classical'.

こんなに狭せまい道みちは通とおれぬ。

I cannot pass a road this narrow.

なんだこの飯めしは！こんなまずそうなものは口くちに入いれぬ！

What is this food? I will not put this disgusting thing in my mouth!

貴様きさまみたいなものには、わしなど殺ころせぬ。

Someone like you cannot kill the likes of me.

In the case that the verb being negated is いる, the old fashion おる will be modified to おらぬ instead.

鈴木すずき殿どのはここにはおらぬ。

Suzuki-dono is not here.

Alternatively, する will be negated as せぬ, and くる will be negated as こぬ. This is consistent with the standard conjugation patterns seen with the use of ず.

あの方かたは人ひとを傷きずつけるようなことはせぬ。

That person would never do anything to hurt anyone.

婆ばあさんが川かわから帰かえってこぬ。

My wife won't come back from the river.

## Examples

Sentence

「愚おろか者もの、男おとこには殺ころせぬのだ。」

'Fool, no man can kill me.'

こちらでは性せいによる差別さべつは許可きょかされておらぬ。

Sex discrimination is not permitted here.

ペーネロペー：「オデュッセウスはまだ戻もどってこぬのか？」

Penelope: 'Odysseus has not returned yet?'

「隅すみに置おけぬ人ひと」というのは「侮あなどれない人ひと」ということなのだ。

'A person who (can't be placed) in a corner' means 'a person who cannot be taken lightly'.

上司じょうしは部下ぶかが向上こうじょうできるように、至いたらぬ点てんを教おしえてあげるべきだ。

A superior should tell his/her subordinates where they fall short so that they can improve.

海うみのデッドゾーンは魚さかななどの大量死たいりょうしの原因げんいんとなりうるかもしれぬ。

The ocean's dead zones might become the cause of large amounts of death among fish (and other sea life).

予期よきせぬ事態じたいが起おきた場合ばあいは、この番号ばんごうに電話でんわをください。

If something unexpected happens, please call this number. (not)

彼かれは、自伝じでんを書かき上あげて１いち年ねんも経たたぬうちに亡なくなった。

He died without even one year passing after finishing his autobiography.

日本語にほんごの「言いわぬが花はな 」はおおよそ英語えいごの「silence is golden」に当あたるようである。

It seems that the Japanese 'a flower does not speak', is roughly equivalent to 'silence is golden' in English.

魔王様まおうさま：「 じゃからアンタを殺ころすと悪わるい気きはせぬぞ。」

Demon King: 'That's why if I kill you I won't feel bad.'

言いうまでもないことだが、知人ちじんに対たいして素知そしらぬふりをするのは失礼しつれいである。

Needless to say, pretending not to know acquaintances is rude.

何なんでジュリエットはロメオを愛あいせなくなったかというと変かわらぬ愛あいなどは存在そんざいせぬためだ。

If I were to say why Juliet stopped loving Romeo, well it is because there is no such thing as eternal love (unchanging).
