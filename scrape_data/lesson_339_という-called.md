## About

About という

English

Japanese

という is a combination of the case marking particle と, and the う - Verb 言いう. It is a set expression that primarily uses only hiragana. という connects two nouns, and means 'the (B) that is called (A)', or 'the (B) known as (A)'.

ローラというモデルさんを知しっていますか？

Do you know the model called Rola?

本山もとやまという駅えきで降おりてください。

Please get off at a station named Motoyama.

タナカタロウという少年しょうねんを見みつけたら、ここにお電話でんわをください。

If you find a child named Tanaka Tarou, please call us.

Fun Fact

という (and the verb 言いう itself) is often quite misunderstood, as the closest translation in English is 'to say'. In Japanese, 言いう actually refers to the information about something that cannot be discovered by simply 'looking at' it. For example, if you look at a man, you cannot know that his name is Bob, or that he works in an office, but you can know whether he is 'short', 'tall', etc. Due to this, という should only be used to express information that requires intimate knowledge, and not simple observations.

ここにボブという名前なまえの男おとこの人ひとが来きませんでしたか。

Did a man called Bob come here? (Natural)

ここに小ちいさいという男おとこの人ひとが来きませんでしたか。

Did a man known as small come here? (Unnatural)

It is possible to see (A) という (B) in sentences where (A) is describing something that seems 'obvious'. However, in these cases, the speaker is usually implying that something about (A) is not exactly as it seems, or that their opinion/knowledge differs.

あの人ひとは強つよいというんだけど、ステロイドを使つかっているんじゃない？

That person is known as strong, but don't you think he uses steroids? (He looks strong, but isn't)

This particular nuance is not limited to nouns, and may be seen with almost any word type.

The story that you 'didn't see anything' is a lie. (a story called/known as)

先生せんせいと言いう職業しょくぎょうは、けっこう大変たいへんな仕事しごとだ。

The job (known as a) teacher is quite hard.

涙なみだと言いうものはどうしてこんなに綺麗きれいなんだろう。

Why are things known as tears so beautiful?

一人暮ひとりぐらしと言いう生活せいかつは結構けっこう寂さみしいです。

The lifestyle known as 'hitori gurashi' is quite sad.

Dreams don't come true easily. (Things known as dreams don't come true easily)

The words (known as) 'Let's meet each other again, if we have a chance' is mostly just a courteous saying.

Have you ever gone to a yakiniku restaurant named 'Rin?'
