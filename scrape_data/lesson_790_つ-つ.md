## About

About つ〜つ

English

Japanese

つ is a conjunction particle in Japanese that is used in constructions where it will appear twice, highlighting contrasting verbs. This つ～つ structure is often translated as 'to be doing (A) and (B) over and over', where the actions are happening repeatedly one after the other.

つ will connect to the conjunctive form of verbs, with (A) and (B) generally appearing in direct sequence.

新あたらしく発売はつばいされたゲーム機きを買かおうか悩なやみながら、お店みせの前まえを行いきつ戻もどりつした。

I walked back and forth in front of the store, wondering if I should buy the newly released game console.

川かわで脱ぬげた靴くつが浮うきつ沈しずみつ流ながれていった。

My shoe that came off in the river was bobbing up and down as it floated away.

Additionally, it is also quite common to see the same verb being repeated in both places. When this happens, the (A) verb will be in the standard form, while the (B) verb will be in the passive form. This just translates as 'to do (A) and to have (A) done to oneself'.

僕ぼくたちは持もちつ持もたれつの関係かんけいで、保育園ほいくえんの頃ころから今いまに至いたるまでお互おたがい助たすけ合あってきた。

We have always been in a give and take relationship, helping each other from the time we were in nursery school until now.

コンサート会場かいじょうには大勢おおぜいの人ひとが集あつまっていて、ゲートが開ひらかれても押おしつ押おされつで全然ぜんぜん会場かいじょうに入はいれなかった。

There were so many people gathered at the concert venue that even when the gate was opened, I could not enter the venue at all because of the pushing and shoving.

Caution

This grammar pattern is only used with a limited set of words in modern Japanese. Here is a list of some standard combinations.

行ゆきつ戻もどりつ - To go back and forth.

浮うきつ沈しずみつ - To surface and submerge (to bob).

見みえつ隠かくれつ - To go in and out of sight.

とつおいつ (from 取とりつ置おきつ) - To waver between.

矯ためつ眇すがめつ - To take a good look.

組くんず解ほどれつ - To intertwine and release (to grapple).

抜ぬきつ抜ぬかれつ - To pull away and be pulled away from (to be neck and neck).

持もちつ持もたれつ - To have and to be held (to give and take).

押おしつ押おされつ - To press and be pressed.

追おいつ追おわれつ - To overtake and be overtaken.

差さしつ差さされつ - To pour and be poured (to take turns exchanging drinks).

I wasn't really expecting much in the end because they are children, but the marathon event was neck and neck race, it was more intense than I had imagined.

As I watched the ito-konyaku repeatedly float and then sink in the nabe, I pondered over the events of the day.
