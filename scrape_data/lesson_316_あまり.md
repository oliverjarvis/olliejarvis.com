## About

About あまり

English

Japanese

As a noun, あまり means 'excess', and refers to an amount of something that is in surplus. This noun is quite often used after many different types of words, to indicate that something happened/exists to an excessive degree (which then resulted in something negative occurring). By itself, 余あまり is not negative. However, this grammar point can be thought of as a set phrase that is generally used in negative situations.

To use あまり, you will need to attach it to the end of the following structures. A verb, an い-Adjective (that has been turned into a noun through the use of さ or み) followed by の, a な-Adjective followed by な, or a noun followed by の. Then, after あまり, a (B) phrase will highlight what the negative result was.

彼かれは仕事しごとを終おわらせることに集中しゅうちゅうするあまり、終電しゅうでんを逃のがしました。

He was so concentrated on finishing his work that he missed the last train.

トムは暑あつさのあまり、気きを失うしなった。

It was so hot that Tom fainted.

彼女かのじょは彼かれと別わかれて、悲かなしみのあまりボーっとしている。

She broke up with her boyfriend and is so sad that she is spacing out a lot.

彼かれは音楽おんがくが好すきなあまり、仕事しごとを辞やめてバンドを作つくった。

He likes music so much that he quit his job and started a band.

ヤスエは初はじめてのライブで、緊張きんちょうのあまり上手うまく歌うたえなかった。

Yasue had her first concert, and she was so nervous that she couldn't sing well.

Caution

The tense of the overall sentence will be dependent on the main clause. Therefore, it is common to see the verb before あまり being used in several different tenses.

先輩せんぱいは飲のみ会かいでお酒さけを沢山たくさん飲のんだあまり、一人ひとりで帰かえることができなかった。

Senpai drank so much at the party that he couldn't get home by himself.

最近さいきん彼女かのじょと別わかれた友達ともだちを心配しんぱいするあまり、友達ともだちの話はなしを聞ききに行いった。

I was so worried about my friend, who has broken up with his girlfriend recently, that I went to his place to listen to him vent.

社長しゃちょうは社員しゃいん全員ぜんいんに恐おそれられているあまり、誰だれも社長しゃちょうの提案ていあんを否定ひていしない。

The CEO of our company is so feared by all of the workers that no one will criticize his decisions.
