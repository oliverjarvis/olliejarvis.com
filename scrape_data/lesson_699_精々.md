## About

About 精々

English

Japanese

精々せいぜい 'at the limit' is an adverb used to convey that something is 'at the most (A)', or 'at best (A)'. This just indicates that the point that (A) has reached cannot be progressed beyond, due to having already reached some physical or metaphorical barrier. This can also often come across as 'no more than (A)', especially in cases where no actual barrier exists, but the speaker cannot imagine that anything higher (or lower) than (A) is possible.

As 精々せいぜい is an adverb, it often appears at the beginning of sentence, or at the beginning of the second clause of a sentence, before the thing that is thought to be at its maximum is described.

このバッグはせいぜい千せん円えんくらいだろう。

This bag probably costs 1000 yen at most.

せいぜい３さんヶ月かげつは働はたらいてもらわないと困こまります。

It would be a problem if you could only work for 3 months at the most.

私わたしが作つくれる料理りょうりはせいぜいこんなもんです。

This is at best what I can cook.

私わたしは今いま働はたらいているから、勉強べんきょうできても一いち日にちに精々せいぜい一いち時間じかんぐらいしか勉強べんきょうができない。

Since I am working now, even if I could study, I can only study 1 hour a day at most.

Fun-fact

精々せいぜい is said to have come from the old-fashioned word 精誠せいせい, which meant 'to completely exhaust all of one's good faith'. In modern times, 精々せいぜい itself has kept this meaning of 'completely exhausting (A)'. However, this is no longer used in reference to 'good faith', but rather exhausting all of one's 'power', 'mental capacity', or 'power of assumption' in relation to (A).

凄すごく怒おこったファン：「ダーデンは精々せいぜい二流にりゅうのコーチだぞ！子供こどもでさえもっと上手うまくコーチングできるぞ!」

'I think that in order to buy that car he spent 30 grand at most.'

'Judging from this athlete's movements, he has been studying karate for 2-3 years at most.'

'I want to help, but because there are few first-aid personnel available, I can only send you five people at most.'

I want to dedicate as much time as possible to this project, but in a day I probably only have 3 hours of useable time at most.

昔むかしの日本人にほんじんが高たかい白米はくまいを食たべれたのは、精々せいぜい祭まつりや結婚けっこんといったイベントの時ときだけでした。

The Japanese of old used to only eat good quality white rice during events like weddings and festivals at most.

店員てんいん：「これ、１０００せん円えんです。」クライアント：「１０００せん円えんだって？この服ふくの品質ひんしつがそんなに高たかいとは思おもえないがね。払はらえるのは精々せいぜい５００ごひゃく円えんでしょ。」

Locks are mostly not used in the peaceful countryside, and even if we assume that the were, it would at most only be in times where the safety worsens.

As of today, an electric car called the Tesla S can only carry batteries that store enough energy for a maximum of a few hundred kilometers per charge, but, I think that in the near future, the capacity of batteries will dramatically increase because of the substance known as graphene.
