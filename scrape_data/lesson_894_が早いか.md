## About

About が早いか

English

Japanese

が早はやいか is an expression in Japanese that groups with verbs, for the purpose of indicating an action which is immediately followed by a second, often unexpected action. It is a combination of the case-marking particle が, the い-Adjective 早はやい 'soon', and the adverbial particle か. It is often translated as 'no sooner than (A), (B)', or 'the instant that (A), (B)'. The more literal translation, taking into account the use of か, is '(A), or possibly sooner, (B)'. This just illustrates the instantaneousness of the (B) action in that (A) was unable to be determined as actually completed before (B) started.

This grammar pattern is formal, and will follow verbs in their dictionary or past tense forms. The verb that comes in the (B) phrase will always be in the past tense.

彼女かのじょは彼かれが書かいた手紙てがみを読よむが早はやいか、悲かなしさのあまりで泣なき始はじめた。

As soon as she read the letter from her boyfriend, she started to cry out of sadness.

息子むすこは食卓しょくたくに座すわるが早はやいか、並ならべてあった夕食ゆうしょくを吸すい込こむように食くい上あげた。

As soon as my son sat at the dining table, he ate all the food that was out as if he sucked them up.

木村きむらくんはアラームが鳴なったが早はやいか、スヌーズボタンを押おした。

As soon as the alarm went off, Kimura-kun pressed the snooze button.

警察官けいさつかんは犯人はんにんを見みつけたが早はやいか、犯人はんにんに飛とびつき手錠てじょうをかけた。

As soon as the police officer saw the suspect, he jumped onto him and handcuffed him.

(A) will tend to be actions that happen momentarily, or having a clearly defined finish point. For actions that don't have a clearly defined finish point, 終おわる 'to finish' will often be linked to the stem form, in order to specify the end. Additionally, while the (B) action will often be unexpected, it will also usually be volitional, that is to say something done intentionally.

Caution

Due to が早はやいか always expressing something in the past, it cannot be used to express wishes or intentions for the future. Additionally, it will not highlight the speaker's own actions.

雨あめが止やむが早はやいか、出でかけるつもりだ。

As soon as the rain stops, I am planning to go out. (Unnatural Japanese)

俺おれは毎日まいにち家いえに荷物にもつを置おくが早はやいか、友達ともだちの家いえに行いく。

I go to my friends house as soon as I drop my stuff off at home, everyday. (Unnatural Japanese)

As soon as the man dressed completely in black noticed the detective, he dashed off like the wind.

At night, as soon as the young wizard opened the window, a white owl flew in.

自動じどうドアが開ひらくが早はやいか、まもなく人々ひとびとは電車でんしゃに乗のり込こんだ。

As soon as the automatic doors opened, people promptly entered the train.

As soon as the soldiers' silhouettes came into sight, Ayame ran away into a nearby field.

No sooner had my mother stepped from the shade into the sunlight than she put up her sun umbrella.

No sooner had the shop opened than, all at once in huge numbers, customers entered.

授業じゅぎょうの終了しゅうりょうを告つげるチャイムが鳴なるが早はやいか 、生徒せいとたちは教室きょうしつを足速あしばやに去さっていった。

No sooner had the chime rung signalling the end of class than the students fled the classroom with haste.

No sooner had my son returned home and placed his school bag in the entrance than he left the house again.

It is said that people that fall asleep as soon as they get into bed have accumulated too much fatigue.

私わたしは彼かれの事ことを一目ひとめ見みるが早はやいか恋こいに落おちてしまい、猛烈もうれつなアプローチを始はじめました。

No sooner had I laid eyes on him than I fell in love and approached him fervently.

その母親ははおやは娘むすめの裁判さいばんで娘むすめが無罪むざいだと言いい渡わたされるが早はやいか、大粒おおつぶの涙なみだを流ながし始はじめた。

No sooner had a verdict of not-guilty been passed down in her trial than that girl’s mother started weeping in huge tears.
