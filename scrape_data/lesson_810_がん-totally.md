## About

About がん～

English

Japanese

ガン見み is a noun that acts as a する-Verb in order to convey that someone is looking at something with fixation. Originally, it is a colloquial abbreviation of ガンガン見みる, with ガンガン being an onomatopoeic expression that can be interpreted as 'furiously', or 'vigorously' in certain contexts.

ガン can be attached to the stem form of certain verbs to create new する-Verbs, or attached to certain pre-existing する-Verbs.

電車でんしゃの中なかで転ころんで、周まわりの人ひとたちにガン見みされたときは恥はずかしさのあまり、泣ないてしまった。

When I fell while on the train and everyone majorly stared at me, I cried from embarrassment.

両親りょうしんの電話でんわをガン無視むししていたら家いえまで来こられた。

When I completely ignored my parents' phone calls, they came to my house.

今日きょうも先輩せんぱいにガン切ぎれされたよ。

I was majorly scolded by my senpai again today.

As you are exposed to Japanese more and more, seeing certain onomatopoeia structures shortened like this will be quite common, and many of them are only used in some specific phrases. In this case ガン is not usually attached to other words, and words like ガン見み are considered a standalone expressions. Due to this, it will be important to look up similar expressions on a case-by-case basis in order to determine if the onomatopoeic part is acting as a prefix, or is just a set phrase.
