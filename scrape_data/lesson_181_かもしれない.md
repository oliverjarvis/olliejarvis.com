## About

About かもしれない

かもしれない is an expression in Japanese that is often treated as a single word, but is actually the combination of the particle かも, and the potential form of the う-Verb 知しる (to know) with the auxiliary verb ない attached. かもしれない is regularly translated simply as 'might', or 'maybe', but the literal translation is much closer to 'even (A), we cannot know'.

To use かもしれない, attach it to the end of any verb, noun, な-Adjective or い-Adjective, in their non-polite (keigo) forms.

来年らいねんから観光客かんこうきゃくが増ふえるかもしれない。

From next year, the number of tourists might increase.

あの建物たてものは教会きょうかいかもしれない。

That building might be a church.

今日きょうは無理むりかもしれない。

It might not be possible today.

そこの池いけは深ふかいかもしれないから気きを付つけてね。

That lake over there might be deep, so please be careful.

As the う-Verb 知しる is part of this construction, it may also be altered to its polite-potential form, creating かもしれません.

明日あしたは休やすむかもしれません。

I might take the day off tomorrow.

Due to かもしれない being such a common grammar pattern in daily speech for expressing uncertainty about various things, several colloquial forms also exist. These are かも, かもしらん, and かもしれん.

明日あしたは休やすみだからドライブするかも。

I have a day off tomorrow, so I might go for a drive.

財布さいふを家いえに忘わすれてきたかもしらん。

I might have left my wallet at home.

明日あしたは夜よる遅おそくまで仕事しごとがあるから、行いかないかもしれん。

I have to work late tomorrow night, so I probably won’t go.

かも is by far the most common of these colloquial forms, with かもしらん potentially sounding a little bit rough/boyish.

It is maybe better to not say such things.

He might go to Japan without researching anything.

この時期じきは晴はれの日ひが続つづくから、富士山ふじさんを綺麗きれいに見みられるかも。

This time of year has a surplus of clear weather days, so you might be able to see Mt. Fuji clearly.

彼かれは専門家せんもんかだから、説明せつめいが分わかりやすいかもしれない。

He is an expert so his explanation might be easy to understand.

This sushi restaurant might be more delicious than the one close to my house.

あの鞄かばんは本当ほんとうにあの人ひとのものかもしれません。

That bag might actually be that person's bag.

I haven't been studying recently, so I might fail the test tomorrow.

That person has never seen something so beautiful before, so she might feel fascinated.

If I buy these nice clothes, I might become popular.
