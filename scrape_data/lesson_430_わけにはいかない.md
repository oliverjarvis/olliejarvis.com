## About

About わけにはいかない

English

Japanese

わけにはいかない is a statement that combines わけ 'to be so', 'reason', or 'cause', with the case marking particle に, the adverbial particle は, and the 'cannot go' meaning of 行いかない. It can be translated as 'cannot afford to (A)', or 'impossible to (A)'. However, the literal meaning is much closer to 'it cannot be so that (A)'.

This structure will be used directly after the non-past form of verbs, in order to highlight them as being either highly undesirable, or highly unattainable.

この料理りょうりは妻つまが頑張がんばって作つくってくれたやつだから、不味まずくても残のこすわけにはいかない。

This food is something that my wife tried her best to make for me, so even if it tastes bad, there's no way that I can leave it.

明日あしたは人手ひとでが足たりないから、休やすむわけにはいかない。

Since we are short-handed tomorrow, I cannot afford to take a day off.

Although わけにはいかない primarily highlights things that are undesirable, it may also highlight something that is desirable, but impossible for some reason. This will depend largely on the speaker's tone of voice, and context of the conversation.

このジャケットが欲ほしいけど、今月こんげつはお金かねを使つかい過すぎたから買かうわけにはいかない。

I want this Jacket, but because I spent too much money this month, it cannot be so that I buy it.

先輩せんぱいに誘さそわれたから、断ことわるわけにはいかない。

I was invited by my senpai, so there is no way that I can decline the offer.

In these types of sentences, the expression is quite similar to the 'I guess it's just not meant to be so' nuance that can be conveyed in English, highlighting regret.

## Examples

Examples

Sentence
Translation

僕ぼくはその問題もんだいを黙だまって見み過すごす訳わけには行いかない。

I cannot afford to silently overlook that problem.

まだやるべきことがあるから、死しぬ訳わけには行いかないんだ！

I can't afford to die, because there are still some things I have to do!

大統領だいとうりょうに頼たのまれたので断ことわる訳わけには行いかない。

Since I was asked by the president I can't afford to say no.

それは捨すてる訳わけには行いきません。大切たいせつな人ひとからもらったんです。

I can't afford to throw it away. I got it from my special someone.

まだ許ゆるす訳わけには行いきません。ちゃんと反省はんせいしてないでしょ。

I can't afford to forgive you yet. You haven't taken the time to properly reflect on what you have done.

ダイエット中ちゅうだから、こんな美味おいしいケーキ、食たべる訳わけには行いかない。

I am on a diet, so I cannot afford to eat this delicious cake.

３０さんじゅっ分ぷん遅刻ちこくしたから、これ以上いじょうお客きゃくさんを待またせる訳わけには行いきません。

Since I was 30 minutes late, I cannot afford to keep my customers waiting anymore.

彼かれはまだ初心者しょしんしゃだから、あの仕事しごとを一人ひとりでやらせる訳わけには行いかない。

I can't afford to let him do that job alone, because he is still a newbie.

引退いんたいするし、このチームの選手せんしゅとして最後さいごの勝負しょうぶだから、負まける訳わけには行いかない。

Because I am retiring and this is my last game as a team member, I can't afford to lose.

前まえに一度いちど、彼女かのじょとの約束やくそくを破やぶったから、今度こんどは破やぶる訳わけには行いかない。

I broke a promise with her once before, so this time I cannot afford to break another.

前回ぜんかいの日本語にほんご能力のうりょく試験しけんに不ふ合格ごうかくだったので、次つぎは失敗しっぱいする訳わけには行いかない。

I failed the JLPT last time, so I cannot afford to fail it a second time.

硬貨こうかを持もってないから、何なにも買かう訳わけには行いかない。

I don't have any hard currency on me, so I cannot afford to buy anything.
