## About

About 切る

English

Japanese

Another verb that is often seen used after the conjunctive form of other verbs is the う - Verb 切きる 'to cut'. 切きる will give the (A) verb the nuance of being done 'to completion'. However, the 'completion' that is being conveyed with 切きる is usually caused by the running out of some kind of resource, like energy. Due to this, 切きる is often translated as 'to do (A) to the point of being unable to continue' (or not needing to continue), or 'to totally and utterly do (A)'.

自分じぶんは全部ぜんぶ使つかい切きるまで、新あたらしいのを買かわない。

Personally, I don't buy new ones until I use everything up.

今日きょうはライトノベルを２に冊さつも読よみ切きった。

I finished reading 2 light novels today.

水筒すいとうの水みずを飲のみ切きったがまだのどが渇かわいている。

I finished drinking the water in my bottle, but am still thirsty.

Occasionally, 切きる may also be used to show that (A) is being done at 'full pelt', 'full steam', or 'full capacity'. In these cases, it is similar to 'to resolutely do (A)', and just means that 100% effort is being put into the action.

このカーブではスピードを落おとさずに走はしり切きるのが重要じゅうようだ。

At this curve, it is important to drive at full pelt without slowing down.

今回こんかいの大会たいかいで全力ぜんりょくを出だし切きることができて良よかったです。

I am happy that I was able to do the best that I can do at this tournament. (Full capacity of power)

Buy another jar of jam after you use one up completely.

Because of the cold today, I am completely frozen. (utterly)

I completely used up my entire paycheck the day I got it. (utterly)

たとえどんなことがあっても勇者ゆうしゃさまを信しんじ切きります。

No matter what will happen, I trust the hero completely.

先週末せんしゅうまつ、三冊さんさつのマンガを読よみ切きった。

I completely finished reading three manga last weekend.

これだけの薪まきがあれば冬ふゆを乗のり切きることが出来できるでしょう。

With this much firewood, we will surely be able to make it completely through winter.

彼かれはウィスキーを水みずのように一気いっきに一本いっぽん飲のみ切きった。

He completely drank an entire bottle of whiskey like it was water.

喉のどが渇かわき切きっていたから、水みずがものすごく美味おいしく感かんじた。

The water tasted amazing, because my throat was completely dry. (utterly)

妻つまは亡なき夫おっとの代かわりに本ほんを書かき切きった。

The wife finished writing the book in place of the late husband. (completely・resolutely)

彼女かのじょがお詫わびを言いい切きらないうちに、彼かれは「ドンマイ」と言いった。

Before she could finish her apology, he said, 'Don't worry about that.' (completely)

疲つかれ切きっていたから、私わたしの代かわりに兄あにをお母かあさんの迎むかえに行いかせた。

Because I was completely worn out, I had my brother go pick up my mother in my place. (utterly)

新製品しんせいひんは大だい成功せいこうでした！初はつロットを一週間いっしゅうかんで売うり切きりました。

The new product was a great success! We completely sold the first batch in one week.
