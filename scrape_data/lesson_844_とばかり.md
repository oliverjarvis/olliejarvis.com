## About

About とばかり（に）

English

Japanese

とばかり is a construction that is often used to show that something is being done 'as if to say (A)', or 'seeming that (A)'. It is a combination of the case-marking particle と in its 'quotation' use, and the adverbial particle ばかり, exhibiting that something is 'basically exactly as described'. Due to this, とばかり simply indicates that while (A) is as described, it is not literally the case.

とばかり can appear after almost any word, as the と is creating a quote. に will often follow ばかり when the sentence is going on to describe what is actually happening.

中田なかた選手せんしゅのバランスが崩くずれたので、斉藤さいとう選手せんしゅはチャンスとばかりに中田なかた選手せんしゅの太ふとももを蹴けった。

Since Nakata's balance was disrupted, Saito, seeing it as a chance, kicked Nakata's thigh.

うちのわんちゃんが「待まってました」とばかりに尻尾しっぽを振ふって友達ともだちに飛とびついた。

Our dog wagged his tail and jumped onto my friend as if to say 'I have been waiting'.

先生せんせいが「俺おれの話はなし聞きいてるか」とばかりに生徒せいとたちを睨にらみつけた。

The teacher stared at the students as if to say 'Are you listening to me'.

とばかりに is an abbreviation of と言いわんばかり 'to only just not be at the point where (A) is said', which itself will still be seen quite often.

早はやくしろと言いわんばかりに上司じょうしが何回なんかいも「まだ？」って聞きいてきた。

My boss repeatedly asked me, 'Are you done?' as if to say 'hurry up'.

つまらないと言いわんばかりの顔かおでみられたから、話はなすのをやめた。

They were looking at me as if they were bored, so I stopped talking.

In this case, 言いわん is an abbreviation of 言いわぬ, making use of the classical auxiliary verb ぬ used for negation.

Caution

As とばかりに will be used to describe the way that something 'looks like it is about to say', it won't be used for describing the speaker themselves. However, it may occasionally be seen being used about the speaker if the sentence is in third person.

## Examples

ランナーが通とおり、ここぞとばかりに声援せいえんを送おくりました。

The runner passed, and as if to say ‘Now’s the time!’, I let out a cheer.

「おまえも黙だまれ」とばかりに手てでテーブルを叩たたいた。

She slammed the table with her hand as if to say 'Silence!'.

盗賊とうぞくたちが待まっていたとばかりに通行人つうこうにんを止とめたのです！

As if they had been waiting, the robbers stopped the passersby!

桜さくらは、今いまこの時ときを待まっていたとばかりに花はなびらを広ひろげています。

Seeming that they had been waiting for this time right now, the petals of the cherry blossoms spread.

社長しゃちょうに呆あきれたとばかりにため息いきをつかれて、クビを覚悟かくごしました。

Being sighed at by the president as if to say he was exasperated, I readied myself to be fired.

殺ころすぞとばかりに面識めんしきのない人ひとを突つき飛とばした彼かれは逮捕たいほされました。

Sending a stranger flying as if to say ‘I’ll kill you!’, he was apprehended.

この問題もんだいの解決かいけつは任まかせろとばかりにウインクをしてきた上司じょうしに恋こいをした。

I fell in love with my boss who winked as if to say ‘Leave solving this problem to me’.

小説しょうせつ：「もうあなたとは話はなしたくない」とばかりに彼かれはドアをばたんと閉しめた。

Novel: 'He slammed the door as if to say "I don't want to talk with you anymore".'

スーパーで閉店へいてんが近ちかづき食品しょくひんなどが割引わりびきされる時間帯じかんたいになると、主婦しゅふたちはここぞとばかりに多おおくの買かい物ものをした。

As the closing time of the supermarket approached, when food and other items were discounted, the housewives did a lot of shopping as if to say, 'This is our chance!'

小説しょうせつ：「洋服店ようふくてんに入はいるなり、長ながいことこのときを待まっていたとばかりに、店員てんいんが声こえをかけてきた。」

Novel: 'As soon as he entered the clothing store, the clerk approached him, as if she had been just waiting for this very moment for a long time.'

小説しょうせつ：「目めの前まえで起おこっていることなのに、皆みな信しんじられないとばかりに目めを見開みひらいていた。」

Novel: 'Even though it was happening right in front of them, they still looked at it with eyes wide open as if saying that they cannot believe this.'

小説しょうせつ：「 なかなかお目めにかかれない美うつくしい景色けしきなので武たけしはここぞとばかりにカメラを手てにし、撮影さつえいした。」

Novel: 'It's a beautiful scene that you don't see very often, so Takeshi took the opportunity to grab his camera and take some pictures.'
