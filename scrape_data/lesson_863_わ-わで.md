## About

About わ〜わ（で）

English

Japanese

As mentioned in the わ grammar point, わ is an adverbial particle that is commonly used in cases of stress of emphasis in Japanese. When used after either verbs, い-Adjectives, な-Adjectives, or nouns in their standard forms, it generally implies that (A) and (B) are negative factors that have lead to (C), where (C) is either a negative outcome, or a negative opinion. It will often be translated simply as 'with (A) and (B), (C)'.

To use this structure, simply place わ after a word, and then repeat the same after a second (different) word. In the case of な-Adjective or nouns, だわ will be used. The case-marking particle で will then follow the second わ, indicating 'with', or 'as a consequence of'.

昨日きのうはクライアントに怒おこられるわ、自分じぶんの上司じょうしに暴言ぼうげん吐はかれるわで大変たいへんだった。

I had a bad day yesterday because my client was mad at me and because my boss yelled at me.

このアパートはボロいわ狭せまいわで最悪さいあくな物件ぶっけんだ。

This apartment sucks because it is run-down and small.

新あたらしく入はいってきた新人しんじんはクライアントには無礼ぶれいだわ、クレーム対応たいおうが下手へただわで、どこから教育きょういくしていいかわからない。

The newbie that just joined is rude to our clients and bad at handling complaints, so I don't know how I should train him.

携帯けいたいを買かってくれたこと自体じたいは嬉うれしかったんだけど、５年ごねん前まえのモデルだわ画面がめんは割われているわで使つかい物ものにならなそうだったから、自分じぶんで新あたらしい携帯けいたいを買かった。

I am happy that they bought me a phone, but it was a 5 year old model and the screen was broken and didn't look like I would be able to use it, so I went ahead and bought myself a new phone.

わ～わで, like わ, primarily emphasizes the speaker's surprise in regard to the words that appear before it. However, unlike わ, where the same verb will be repeated in order to highlight an excessive extent, わ～わで simply indicates surprise in terms of irritation or frustration. This structure is predominantly used by men, but it may also sometimes be used by women. However, there are several different variations of わ depending on where they appear in sentences, each of which have gender usage tendencies that will need to be learned.

In essence, わ is used in the same way as し～し, where the words that they are being attached to are just being listed as reasons. Additionally, the で may occasionally be omitted.

Fun Fact

わ is simply an emphatic version of the usual は adverbial particle, and originates from は being changed to わ. Due to this, the literal meaning can be thought of as a stronger version of the standard 'as for (A)' meaning of は.

Caution

While this grammar pattern is used almost exclusively with a negative nuance, it may, in rare cases, have a positive nuance.

今年ことしは宝たからくじには当あたるわ、長年ながねん付つき合あっていた彼氏かれしにプロポーズされるわで、幸しあわせなことが沢山たくさんあってとても嬉うれしい。

I am very happy this year because I won the lottery and my boyfriend of many years is going to propose to me. (Positive meaning)

「今朝けさの通勤時つうきんじは、急きゅうに雨あめが降ふってくるわ傘かさは壊こわれるわで大変たいへんだった。」

'I had a hard time getting to work this morning because it suddenly started raining and my umbrella broke.'

「今日きょうはタンスに小指こゆびをぶつけるわ、道みちでつまずくわで、ついてなかった。」

'I was not lucky today because I hit my little finger on the dresser and tripped on the street.'

「朝あさから犬いぬに吠ほえられるわ、鳥とりのウンチが頭あたまに落おちてくるわで…（最低さいていな一いち日にちだったわ）。」

'The dogs barked at me in the morning and the bird pooped on my head, so... （it was a horrible day.）'

「あのレストラン、料理りょうりに虫むしが入はいってるわ、クレームいったら切きれられるわで最低さいていだったの。」

'Speaking of that restaurant, it was the worst since they had bugs in the food, and when I complained about it, I was ignored.'

お母かあさんに怒おこられるわ先生せんせいにも怒おこられるわ、気分きぶんが悪わるい。

Having been gotten angry at by my mother and my teacher one after the other, I feel terrible.

財布さいふをなくすわ鍵かぎをなくすわ、本当ほんとうに自己じこ管理かんりが全まったくできていない。

Losing my wallet and losing my keys one after the other, I honestly can’t look after myself.

このバイトは力仕事ちからしごとだわ未払みばらいの時ときがあるわ、もうやってられません。

This part-time job is physical work and sometimes the pay is overdue, I can’t put up with it anymore.

彼氏かれしに浮気うわきされるわ友人ゆうじんには裏切うらぎられるわ…もう生いきていたくなくなる…！

Being cheated on my boyfriend and betrayed by my friends one after the other... I’m starting to not want to live anymore...!

ここのレストラン、接客せっきゃく態度たいどは悪わるいわ値段ねだんは高たかいわで、全まったくいいところがない。

The customer service at this restaurant is bad and the price is high, so there aren’t any good points.

今日きょうはいつもより寒さむいわ風かぜは強つよいわで、観光かんこうなんてしていられません。

Today is colder than usual and the wind is strong, so it’s impossible to do something like sightseeing.

今日きょうは風かぜが冷つめたいわ曇くもっているわで、観光日和かんこうびよりではない。

Because the wind is cold today and it’s cloudy, it’s not ideal weather for sightseeing.

変へんな匂においがするわ湿気しっけがあるわで、このホテルの客室きゃくしつは最悪さいあくだ。

Because there is a strange smell and it’s humid, this hotel’s guest rooms are the worst.

このマットレス、硬かたすぎるわ小ちいさすぎるわで、買かう価値かちがまったくない。

Because this mattress is too hard and too small, it’s not worth buying at all.

友達ともだちとアニメのキャラについての話はなし、Ａエー：「だって興味深きょうみぶかいもん」
Ｂビー：「そのキャラがつまらないわ寒さむいわ退屈たいくつだわで擁護ようごのしようもない。」

Chat with a friend about anime character, A: 'But he is interesting!.'
B: 'This character is boring, dull and mind-numbing, so there's no way to defend him.'

あのレストランは、接客態度せっきゃくたいどが最高さいこうだわご飯はんは美味おいしいわで、また行いきたいの！

Because the service at that restaurant is amazing and the food is delicious, let’s go again. (Rare positive use for emphasis)

彼かれって料理りょうりが上手じょうずだわ、仕事しごとをちゃんとしているわで、本当ほんとうに魅力的みりょくてきでならない。

Because he is good at cooking, and he is thorough with his work, he is so attractive I can’t stand it. (Rare positive use for emphasis)

ずっと付つき合あってた彼女かのじょに振ふられるわ、両親りょうしんが亡なくなるわで、この一年いちねんは最悪さいあくだった。

Because I was dumped by the girl I was dating for ages, and my parents passed away, this last year was the worst.

Ａエー：「顔色かおいろが悪わるいね。」
Ｂビー：「そうなんだよね。昨日きのうの夜よる、ルームメイトがうるさいわ、蚊かにたくさんさされるわでちっとも眠ねむれなかった。」

A: 'Oh, you look pale!'
B: 'Yeah, I know. I couldn't sleep at all last night because of my noisy roommate and all the mosquito bites.'
