## About

About ～て頂戴

English

Japanese

て頂戴ちょうだい is a phrase that is used to make a request of someone, normally someone who has a very close relationship with the speaker or someone who is in a social position below the speaker. It is formed by taking the て form of a verb and then attaching 頂戴ちょうだい, originally a humble word that can be used to mean 'receiving', 'eating' or 'drinking'.

Although 頂戴ちょうだい can be used as a humble word in other contexts, when attached to the て form of a verb, this phrase is most often used informally, such as among family members. It is sometimes considered a more feminine version of てください.

お兄にいちゃん、ちょっとこれ開あけてちょうだい。

Hey big brother, can you please open this?

お父とうさん、コンビニ行いくなら砂糖さとう買かってきてちょうだい。

Husband, if you are going to the convenience store, can you get sugar?

これお姉ねえちゃんに渡わたしてきてちょうだい。

Go hand this to your big sister.

Caution

Sometimes 頂戴ちょうだい will be used with nouns or on its own as an informal demand, similar to 'gimme!' in English. This nuance will often be written in hiragana, not kanji, and can sound childish.

グミちょうだい！

Gimme gummies!

Fun Fact

When used as a humble word in more formal contexts, 頂戴ちょうだい will normally be used as a する verb. This meaning is more likely to be written in kanji, not hiragana.

少すこしお時間じかんを頂戴ちょうだいしますが、よろしいでしょうか。

We would need to take a moment of your time, but would that be okay?

こちらのお土産みやげはありがたく頂戴ちょうだいいたします。

I will receive this souvenir with great thanks.
