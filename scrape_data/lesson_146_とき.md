## About

About とき

とき, or 時とき in kanji, is a noun that is regularly used to signify 'when' something happened (a verb). However, unlike some other time expressions, とき is mainly used to express things that happen at regular intervals (habits, hobbies, etc.), or at broad times/individual events (when it rains, when there is a sale at the shop, etc.)

Using とき is quite easy! You just need to attach it to the end of any independent word (verbs, nouns, or adjectives). As with most cases where a noun will be attached to another word in Japanese, we will also be required to use な after な-Adjectives, and の after nouns. The case-marking particle に will generally come directly after 時とき, signifying that another verb is about to follow.

狭せまい駐車場ちゅうしゃじょうで車くるまのドアを開あけたときにドアを壁かべにぶつけた。

When I opened my car door in a small parking lot, I slammed it on the wall. (Individual event)

暑あつい時ときはエアコンを点つけましょう。

When it is hot, let's turn on the air conditioner. (Broad time)

彼かれは暇ひまなときに本ほんを読よみます。

He reads books when he is bored. (Hobby)

雨あめの時ときは家いえでゴロゴロしています。

When it rains, I laze around in my house. (Habit)

Caution

As discussed previously, ときに is primarily used to discuss when things happened. What this means is that it will not be used to highlight ongoing states that were true during the first action. For example 'the time you tripped over a log on Tuesday'.

学校がっこうから家いえに帰かえった時ときに火曜日かようびだった。

The time when I was going home from school was Tuesday. (Tuesday is a state, rather than an action, so とき will not be used to express something like this) (Unnatural Japanese)

When とき is used, the nuance will be that it is describing one thing leading to another, rather than a static thing.

俺おれは高校生こうこうせいの時ときに俺おれが好すきな女おんなの子こに告白こくはくして、「え、無理むり...」って言いわれた。

When I was in highschool I asked out a girl I liked, and she said 'umm, no way…' (時（とき） is describing the broad event of 'being a highschool student', while て goes on to describe another single event that happened during that time)

散歩さんぽをするときに音楽おんがくを聴きく。

I listen to music when I take a walk.

授業じゅぎょうのときは静しずかにしなくてはいけない。

When you are having class, you have to be quiet.

あの映画えいがを見みたとき、泣ないた。

When I watched that movie, I cried.

大変たいへんなときに彼かれの親おやは亡なくなった。

His parents died when he was going through a difficult time.

寒さむいときは、お風呂ふろに入はいりたくなる。

When it is cold, I want to take a bath.

東京とうきょうに行いったときに、スカイツリーを見みた。

When I went to Tokyo, I saw the Skytree.

写真しゃしんを撮とるときは、笑わらってください。

When you take a picture, please smile.

出でかけるときは、「行いってきます」と言いいます。

When you go out (from your house) you say 'いってきます' (I'm off!).

入院にゅういんしたとき、寂さびしかった。

When I was hospitalized I was lonely.

私わたしはカラオケに行いくときに幸しあわせな気持きもちになります。

When I go to karaoke I feel happy.

そこの公園こうえんに遊あそびに行いったときに、お金かねを見みつけた。

When I went to play at that park I found some money.

昨日きのうの夜よる布団ふとんに入はいったときに、トイレに行いきたくなった。

Last night when I got in my futon, I ended up needing to go to the bathroom.
