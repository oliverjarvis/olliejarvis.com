## About

About なにやら

English

Japanese

何なにやら is an adverb which expresses uncertainty about what exactly something is or why something is happening. Similar to どうやら, although it is often treated as a single word, 何なにやら is composed of the question word 何なに and the particle やら which denotes uncertainty and self-questioning.

何なにやら is often translated as 'something or other', 'some kind of', or 'for some reason' depending on the context.

あの人ひとに何なにやら言いわれても何なんにも感かんじない。

I don't feel anything when that person says something to me.

やっと子供こどもが落おち着ついたと思おもったら、何なにやら歌うたい出だした。

When I thought my kid finally settled down, he started to sing some kind of song.

家いえに帰かえってカバンを開あけたら何なにやら見覚みおぼえのないレシートが入はいってた。

When I opened my bag when I got home, there was a receipt in there that I have no recollection of for some reason.

洗濯せんたくやら何なにやらをしない奴やつは文句もんく言いうな！

As a person who doesn't do things such as cleaning and other things, don’t complain! (As someone who doesn't do chores, you have no right to complain)

Although 何なにか and 何なにやら can both be translated as 'something', they are not interchangeable. 何か can be used for general statements, but 何なにやら is only used when the speaker is unsure of what the thing in question is.

あの二人ふたりの間あいだで何なにやらあったらしい。

Apparently something happened between those two. (Natural Japanese)

お茶ちゃか何なにやら飲のみますか？

Do you want tea or something? (Unnatural Japanese)

Fun Fact

Although it is less common than 何なにやら or どうやら, やら is also sometimes attached to other question words like どこ or いつ to indicate that the speaker is uncertain about something.

何なにやらお隣となりの家いえが空あき巣すにあったみたい。

I heard that our next door neighbour got burglarized or something.

どうやら昨日きのうこの付近ふきんで事故じこがあったようだ。

I heard that there was an accident around here or something.

あの人ひとはどこやら怪あやしいんだよな。

Something about him is suspicious.

彼かれが帰かえってくるのはいつやら…

I wonder when he will come home…

Translation

I smell something or other nice.

That person seems to be bothered by something or other.

彼女かのじょのまなざしには、何なにやら言葉ことばにならない想おもいが隠かくされていた。

In her gaze, some kind of ineffable sentiment had been concealed.

何なにやら母ははは、宝たからくじの一等いっとうを当あてたことを、家族かぞく全員ぜんいんに黙だまっていたらしい。

For some reason, it seems my mother kept quiet to the entire family that she’d won the first prize in the lottery.

何なにやら社長しゃちょうに隠かくし子ごがいたらしく、その噂うわさがオフィスの他社たしゃにも広ひろまっているよ。

I heard like the boss might have a secret kid or something... The rumor's even spreading to other companies.

外そとは何なにやらにぎやかですね。

For some reason, it’s lively outside, isn’t it?

何なにやら、今週こんしゅうの会議かいぎは、空気くうきが重おもかったよね。

For some reason, the atmosphere at this week’s meeting was a bit heavy, don’t you think?

田中たなかさんの様子ようすが、何なにやら変かわったように思おもいませんか？

Don’t you think something or other is different about Tanaka?

この場所ばしょには、何なにやら懐なつかしい空気くうきが流ながれている。

For some reason, this place has a nostalgic air to it.

何なにやら胸むねがざわついて、落おち着つかないのであった。

For some reason, my chest felt uneasy and I couldn’t calm down.

彼かれの言葉ことばには、何なにやら裏うらがあるように感かんじた。

I felt his words had a hidden meaning or something.

二人ふたりの関係かんけいは、何なにやら、どうにもこうにも上手うまくいかなくなったらしい。

For some reason, their relationship seemed to not go well no matter what they did.

何なにやら急きゅうに天候てんこうが悪わるくなったらしく、すぐに帰かえってきたよ。

For some reason, the weather seemed to have suddenly taken a turn for the worse, so I came right back home.

今日きょうのプレゼン、何なにやら皆みなの反応はんのうがいつもと違ちがった気きがするが、君きみはどう見みたかね？

For some reason, I feel like everyone’s reaction was different at today’s presentation. How’d it seem to you?

何なにやら私わたしが不在ふざいの間あいだに、子供達こどもたちがパーティーをしていて、それが騒音被害そうおんひがいとして通報つうほうされたらしい。

For some reason, it seems that my children had a party while I wasn’t around, and they were reported for noise pollution.
