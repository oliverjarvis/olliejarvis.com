## About

About くせに

English

Japanese

癖くせ is a noun that means 'habit' or 'tendency'. However, as it usually refers to a negative thing, 'vice' is another accurate translation. くせに (usually appearing without kanji) is the combination of くせ, and the case marking particle に. This structure is often used to criticize the actions of others, and is used in a similar way to 'despite', 'even though', or 'and yet' in English.

As くせ is a noun, くせに may be used after the attributive form of any other word.

さっき寝ねるって言いってたくせにまだ起おきてるの？

Even though you said that you are going to sleep earlier, are you still up?

若わかいくせに何なにダラダラしているんだ。

Why are you working so sluggishly even though you are young?

自分じぶんだって下手へたなくせに何なに偉えらそうに言いってるんだ。

Despite you also being no pro, why are you talking so high and mighty.

この子こは犬いぬのくせにニャーと鳴なく。

Even though he is a dog, he meows.

Fun Fact

癖くせ is a word that has developed a 'negative' meaning over time. Originally, 癖くせ was used to convey a preference, or the side that someone or something tended to lean toward (due to it being favorable for some reason). When adding the case marking particle に to this, it merely expressed that (A) was the preferable state/location, so (B) not being/not acting in a way appropriate for (A) is less than desirable.

Translation

食たべきれない癖くせにたくさん注文ちゅうもんするのはやめてほしい。

Even though you cannot eat it all, you order a lot. I want you to stop (doing that). (despite)

昨日きのう休やすんだ癖くせに、なんで知しったかぶりするの？

Why do you pretend to know (what we are talking about) despite having the day off yesterday?

あの人ひとは先輩せんぱいの癖くせに、いつも僕ぼくにおごらせようとする。

Despite being my senior, he/she always tries to make me pay.

寒さむい癖くせに「寒さむくない」とやせ我慢がまんするのはやめてください。

Please stop pretending like you're not cold even though you are. (despite)

給料きゅうりょうを沢山たくさんもらっている癖くせに、貯金ちょきんがないらしい。

Despite getting paid a lot, I have heard that he has no savings.

休やすみで時間じかんがある癖くせに、家いえの手伝てつだいを何なにもしない。

Even though it is your day off, you don't help at all with the housework.

暑あつがりな癖くせに、あの人ひとは毎日まいにちセーターを着きて仕事しごとに行いく。

Despite hating the heat, that person wears a sweater to work everyday.

何なにも知しらない癖くせに、偉えらそうなことを言いうのはやめてください。

Despite not knowing anything, you surely act high and mighty. Please just stop.

「男おとこの癖くせに泣なくな」って言いわれるけど、たまには泣ないていいと思おもう。

I am told 'you are a man, don't cry,' but I think that it is okay to cry from time to time. (Despite)

お金かねもない癖くせに、カードで気軽きがるに高たかい物ものを買かうのはやめた方ほうがいいよ。

Even though you have no money, you freely use your credit card to buy expensive things. It would be better to stop (doing that). (despite)

本当ほんとうは行いきたい癖くせに。素直すなおに「行いきたい」って言いえばいいじゃないか。

You really want to go and yet... You should just honestly say 'I want to go,' right?

見みてた癖くせに、なんで「見みてない」って嘘うそをつくの？ なぜ真実しんじつを話はなしてくれないの？

Despite looking, why are you lying and saying 'I didn't.' Why won't you just tell me the truth?
