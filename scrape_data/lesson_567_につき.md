## About

About につき

English

Japanese

Although rarely seen in its kanji form, につき is a grammar pattern that comes from a combination of に and the conjunctive form of the う- Verb 就つく 'to take up (A)', or 'to assume (A)'. This meaning is usually in regard to assuming a new position, such as a new job.

However, the particular meaning conveyed here will be closer to 'due to (A), (B)', 'on account of (A), (B)', or 'for each (A), (B)'. につき will follow nouns directly, before the (B) part of the sentence describes what is happening 'due to' or 'on account of (A)'.

閉店へいてんにつき、特別とくべつセール開催中かいさいちゅう！

Due to our store closing, there is a special sale going on!

工事こうじ中ちゅうにつき、この先さき立たち入いり禁止きんし。

Due to construction, entry beyond this point is prohibited.

この商品しょうひんは大人気だいにんきのため、一人ひとりにつき二ふたつまでとさせていただいております。

Since this product is very popular, we are limiting it to two per person.

This grammar point is quite formal, so it is often seen in formal documents, announcements, and notifications.

Regardless of the several common English translations, the base meaning of 'to affix to (A)' is kept in all of the uses of につき, as it simply implies that (B) is in answer to, exists in a way that is directly linked to, suitable for, or caused by (A).

On account of opening a new shop, we are hiring clerks.

Right now, you get five points for each 100 yen you spend.

On account of tomorrow's festival, the company will be closed.

Due to this being private land, it is prohibited to cut through here.

On account of today's rain, the match has been canceled.

Today's special sale on eggs is limited to two cartons per person (each person).

On account of the head chef being on a business trip, today's menu has been changed.

この駐車ちゅうしゃ場じょうは３０さんじゅっ分ぷんに就つき３００さんびゃく円えんも取とられる。

This parking area takes as much as 300 yen for (every) 30 minutes.

好評こうひょうに就つき、期間きかん限定げんていでしたが定番ていばんメニューになりました。

On account of its popularity, the limited time item was made a standard menu item.

Due to the heat stroke alert's announcement, Katy Sherry's concert has been canceled. We are sorry for the inconvenience.

Due to ongoing renovation works, please use the restroom on the second floor. We are sorry for the inconvenience.
