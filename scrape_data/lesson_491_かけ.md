## About

About かけ

English

Japanese

Being an unofficial helper verb, かけ is what is known as a 造語ぞうご (neologism), and carries one specific meaning when used in conjunction with other words. This is similar to 向むけ. かけ comes from the verb 掛かける 'to suspend', and may be attached to the conjunctive form of other verbs, in order to convey that they are 'unfinished'. Usually this translates as 'half (A)', 'about to (A)', or 'not yet finished (A)'.

かけ behaves like a noun, so may be followed by the case marking particle の when attached directly to another noun, or by だ when at the end of a sentence.

俺おれの食たべかけだけど大丈夫だいじょうぶ？

This is half-eaten by me, is that okay?

これは私わたしの飲のみかけだ。

This is my half-drunk one.

彼かれは死しにかけの子犬こいぬを救すくった。

He saved a puppy that was about to die. (Half dead)

彼女かのじょは壊こわれかけのパソコンを買かって直なおすのが趣味しゅみだ。

Her hobby is to buy half-broken computers and fix them. (Computers on the verge of breaking)

Occasionally, the verb 掛かける itself may be used instead of the noun form, かけ.

やばい、死しにかけるところだった。

Wow, I was about to die right there. (Almost on the verge of dying)

飲のみかけた水みずを捨すてる。

I will throw away my half drunk water.

Fun Fact

As the literal meaning of 掛かける is 'to suspend', it expresses that something is stuck somewhere in the process of happening. This is similar to taking a photo of someone that is jumping. In the photo it will look like they are 'suspended', but that is only because one particular moment has been captured, somewhere between the start and the finish. かけ conveys exactly the same meaning, and therefore may be interpreted differently, depending on the verb it is paired with.

With verbs that express a momentary action (something that cannot be 'half' done), かけ will convey that the action was 'on the verge of' happening.

先週せんしゅうの事故じこで死しにかけそうになった。

I was on the verge of dying in an accident last week.

壊こわれかけていた椅子いすに座すわったら、椅子いすが壊こわれた。

When I sat on a chair that was on the verge of breaking, it broke.

However, with verbs that are ongoing actions, かけ will simply imply that they are 'in the process' of being done.

終おわりかけだからちょっと待まって。

I'm in the process of finishing, so can you wait a little.

Translation

食たべ掛かけのケーキでも良よければありますよ。

I have some half-eaten cake, if you would like.

描かき掛かけの絵えを早はやく完成かんせいさせたい。

I want to finish this half-drawn picture quickly.

この時計とけい壊こわれ掛かけだけど、ないよりはいいか。

This watch is half-broken, but is still better than nothing.

忘わすれ掛かけていた思おもい出でが急きゅうに蘇よみがえった。

The half-forgotten memory suddenly came back.

交通こうつう事故じこで危あやうく死しに掛かけた。

I nearly died in the car accident. (half-dead)

剥はがれ掛かけているポスターを貼はり直なおしてください。

Please, fix this half-peeled off poster.

書かき掛かけたまま出だしていない手紙てがみがあります。

I have a letter that I have yet to send that is still half-written.

拭ふき掛かけたテーブルをちゃんと最後さいごまで拭ふいてください。

Please finish wiping this half-wiped table.

飲のみ掛かけのジュースがあるのを忘わすれていた。

I forgot that I have some juice that I haven't yet finished. (Half-finished)

今日きょうの宿題しゅくだいはもう終おわり掛かけです。

Today's homework is about to be finished. (Half-finished)

私わたしは、何冊なんさつも読よみ掛かけの本ほんがある。

I have a lot of books that are half-read.

靴くつを履はき掛かけて、靴下くつしたを履はいていないことに気きづいた。

I noticed that I was not wearing socks when I was about to put on my shoes. (Half-on)
