## About

About ～てやる

English

Japanese

The う-Verb やる, occasionally seen in the kanji form 遣やる, primarily means ‘to do’, but can also mean ‘to give’ as the casual form of あげる. When attached to the て-form of verbs, it acts as a subsidiary verb. When substituting for あげる, it will often sound patronizing, or that the speaker is doing an action because it is unavoidable, and the person they are doing it for cannot do it themselves. This will be translated as ‘to do for’.

いいよいいよ、俺おれがやってやるからお前まえは見みとけ。

Don’t worry about it, I’ll do it for you, so just watch.

今夜こんやは俺おれが奢おごってやるから、どんどん好すきなもん頼たのめばいいからな。

I’m going to pay for you, so don’t hesitate and order whatever you want.

In other cases, やる acts more like 見みせる ‘to show’, and indicates the speaker’s strong will to perform an action. In these cases, it will be translated as ‘I will do (A), just wait and see’, or ‘I’ll definitely do (A)’.

絶対ぜったい綺麗きれいになって見返みかえしてやる！

I am going to become pretty and prove them wrong, just wait!

こうなったら勝かってやるしかないだろ。

Well, in a situation like this there is no other choice but to win!

However, in either the place of あげる or みせる, the underlying meaning is just ‘to do’, with emphasis on the doer being the person that is in power over the result of the action due to their superiority.

## Examples

Examples

00:04

Sentence
Translation

​​アクション映画えいがのマッチョな主人公しゅじんこう：「ぶっころしてやる！」

​​The macho protagonist in an action movie: 'I'm gonna kill you!'

​​お金かねがなくて行いきたい大学だいがくに行いけないという状況じょうきょう：「金持かねもちになってやる！」

​​The situation of not being able to go to the school one wants to, due to money problems: 'I will get rich, just you wait!'

​​低得点ていとくてんで馬鹿ばかにされた後あと：「絶対ぜったい、次つぎのテストでは百ひゃく点てん取とって、あいつをみかえしてやる。」

​​After being ridiculed for a low score: 'I will definitely score 100 on the next test and prove I'm better than that dude, just you wait!'

「絶対ぜったい、合格ごうかくしてやる！」

'I will surely pass, just wait and see!'

​​Ａエー子こ：「こっちからあっちまで競走きょうそうしよ！」
​​Ｂビー子こ：「いいよ、かってやる!」

​​Child A: 'Let's race from here to there!'
​​Child B: 'Okay! I'll win!'

​​アクション映画えいが：「殺ころせないけどぶちのめしてやる！」

​​Action movie: 'I can't kill you, but I'll beat the living daylights out of you, just you wait!'

近ちかづいたら ぶっ飛とばしてやる！

'Get a little close and I will send you flying!'

いつか真相しんそうをあばいてやる！

'One day I will uncover the truth, just you wait!'

​​ファンタジー小説しょうせつ、妖怪ようかい：「生いきたまま食くってやる！」

​​Fantasy novel, monster: 'I will eat you alive!'

「心配しんぱいしないで！つれていってやる！」

'Don't worry! I will get you out of here!'
