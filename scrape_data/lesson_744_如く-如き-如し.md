## About

About 如く・如き・如し

English

Japanese

如ごとし is an classical auxiliary verb in Japanese which has a very similar meaning to its modern equivalent, よう 'manner of'. Just like よう, ごとし (written primarily in hiragana) focuses on the appearance of (A) based on observation. Due to this, ごとし may be translated as 'like (A)', 'similar to (A)', or 'as if it were (A)'.

ごとし is the base form of this auxiliary verb, but ごとく (the conjunctive form), and ごとき (the attributive form) will also be seen. As only ごとき is attributive, it may be followed directly by other nouns, while ごとし most often comes at the end of sentences. ごとく acts as an adverb when preceding verbs, but may also be seen before breaks in clauses. Let's look at a few examples of each form.

Like よう, ごとし and its variations will follow の when coming after a noun. However, it will follow が when coming after a verb.

ことわざ：「 竜りゅうに翼つばさを得えたる如ごとし。」

Proverb: 'It is as if a dragon had wings.'

主婦しゅふの私わたしには休やすみなんかはあってなきが如ごとし。

As a housewife, it's like I don't have any days off.

高橋たかはし先生せんせいは鬼おにの如ごとく怖こわいと言いう噂うわさを聞きいた。

I have heard rumors that Mr. Takahashi is as scary as a demon.

生うまれてきたばかりの赤あかちゃんが天使てんしの如ごとくかわいい。

The baby that was just born is cute like an angel.

彼女かのじょの如ごとき頭あたまがいい人ひとでも失敗しっぱいすることはあるに決きまっている。

Even a smart person like her is bound to make mistakes.

風邪かぜごときで休やすんでいる余裕よゆうなど私わたしにはない。

I can't afford to take a break just because of something like a cold.

As ごとし is old-fashioned, it will primarily be encountered in books, rather than in speech.

Fun Fact

The kanji 如 itself has a meaning very similar to the word 'likeness' in English, indicating something that resembles (A) in every sense of the word, but is not actually (A). This kanji can be seen in several common words in Japanese such as 如何どう 'how'.

Translation

彼女かのじょの動うごきはバレリーナの如ごとし。

Her movements are like those of a ballet dancer.

ガンダルフの如ごとき英雄えいゆうはたんと無ない。

There are not many heroes like Gandalf.

ウサイン・ボルトの如ごとく走はしる。

He runs like Usain Bolt.

サービス業ぎょうのマネージャーを任まかされている私わたしにとって、休日きゅうじつなどはあってなきが如ごとし。

For me, who has been entrusted as the manager of the service sector, something like days off are virtually nonexistent.

忍者にんじゃについての小説しょうせつ：「カツラが疾風はやての如ごとく庭にわを駆かけ巡めぐり、塀へいを飛とび越こえていった。」

A novel about ninja: 'Katsura ran through the garden like a swift wind and jumped over the wall.'

古ふるい小説しょうせつ：「何なにかを思おもい出だしたかのような顔かおをして、悪夢あくむを見みているが如ごとき目めつきで問とうた。」

Old novel: 'He seemed to have remembered something, with his face looking like something out of a nightmare, he asked....'

懐なつかしい香水こうすいの匂においを嗅かいで、十年じゅうねん前まえの青春せいしゅん時代じだいの事ことが今いまさらの如ごとく思おもい出だされました。

Smelling a nostalgic perfume's aroma, as if it were right this moment, it brought back memories from the prime of my youth, 10 years ago.

葬儀そうぎのスピーチ：「子供こどもの頃ころ、おじさんが「光陰矢こういんやの如ごとし」とよく言いっていました。その意味いみが、今いまやっとわかるようになってきた気きがします。」

Funeral speech: 'When I was a kid, my uncle used to say 'time flies like an arrow.' Now I feel like I'm finally starting to understand the meaning of it.'

生徒せいとの一人ひとりが授業じゅぎょうを終おえて風かぜの如ごとく教室きょうしつを去さっていきましたが、一体いったい何故なぜあんなに急いそいでいたのでしょうか…。

Like the wind, one of the students left the classroom after finishing the lesson, but why the heck they were in such a rush, I have no idea.

経済けいざいについて長年ながねん研究けんきゅうしている知人ちじんの言いうが如ごとく、経済けいざいは忽たちまち回復かいふくし始はじめた。

In a way similar to what a friend of mine who has been researching economics for many years said, the economy suddenly started to recover.

あの頃ころは彼かれの如ごとき人ひとはこの世よに二人ふたりといないと思おもっていたが、年としをとって彼かれのような人ひとは沢山たくさんいる事ことを知しった。

Around that time, I thought there were no two people similar to him in this world, but as the years passed, I learned that there are actually many people just like him.

獲物えものに追おわれている兎うさぎの如ごとく私わたしの側そばから放はなれる同僚どうりょうを見みて、彼女かのじょが私わたしを避さけている事ことを確信かくしんした。

Seeing my colleague distance herself from me like a rabbit being chased as prey, I was convinced that she was avoiding me.
