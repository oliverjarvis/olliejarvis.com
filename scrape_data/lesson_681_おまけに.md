## About

About おまけに

English

Japanese

お負まけに is a phrase which often functions as a conjunction between the (A) and (B) parts of a sentence in order to show that (B) is 'in addition to (A)'.

This structure stems from the common use of the noun お負まけ which indicates a 'discount' or 'freebie' that is added in addition to the base of (A). Despite お負まけ itself primarily being used for positive things お負まけに may convey 'in addition', 'on top of that', or 'what's more' regardless of whether the original topic is a good thing or not. However, (A) and (B) will always either be both good things, or both bad things, never a mixture of both.

Being a combination of お負まけ and に, this phrase will appear either at the beginning of a new sentence, or at the beginning of a second clause of a 2-part sentence.

今日きょうは仕事しごとに遅刻ちこくして部長ぶちょうに怒おこられたし、おまけに取引先とりひきさきの人ひとも怒おこらせちゃったから、今日きょうは最悪さいあくの日ひだったよ。

Today was one of the worst days because I got yelled at by my boss for being late to work, and to make it worse, I upset one of our clients.

昨日きのうは彼氏かれしに美味おいしいご飯はんをご馳走ちそうしてもらって、おまけにプレゼントまでもらった。

Yesterday, my boyfriend treated me to a delicious dinner and on top of that he even gave me a gift.

パソコンが全然ぜんぜん立たち上あがらない。おまけにスマホの充電じゅうでんがないから仕事しごとが全然ぜんぜんできない。

My computer won't turn on. To make matters worse, my smartphone is out of battery, so I can't work at all.

Fun-fact

An important distinction between phrases like それに and お負まけに is that お負まけに keeps its original meaning of (B) being some kind of 'bonus'. What this means is that whatever the speaker is trying to convey, (A) by itself is enough to meet some specific qualifications, and (B) is just adding 'insult to injury', or 'icing on the cake', depending on whether it is a good or a bad thing.

家いえを掃除そうじした。御負おまけにランチを作つくっといた。

I cleaned the house. On top of that, I also made lunch in advance. (what is more)

宝石ほうせきを買かったら、御負おまけに香水こうすいをサービスしてくれた。

They included perfume in addition to the jewel I bought. (on top of that)

自転車じてんしゃが盗ぬすまれただけではなく、御負おまけに雨あめが降おりそうだ。

Not only has my bike been stolen, but to make matters worse, it looks like it is going to rain. (what is more)

競馬けいばで負まけ、御負おまけに帰かえりに財布さいふを落おとし、最悪さいあくだ。

I lost at the horse races and, to make matters worse, I lost my wallet on the way home. Worst day ever.

体重たいじゅうが激増げきぞうし、御負おまけに髪かみまで薄うすくなった。ストレスですね。

On top of my hair thinning, my weight suddenly increased. This is definitely stress. (what is more)

秘伝ひでんの味あじのレシピを教おしえてくれて、御負おまけに見学けんがくまでさせてくれました。

(She) shared the recipe to the secret flavor in addition to allowing me to watch (her) make it (herself).(on top of that)

紅葉こうようを見みに行いったら、御負おまけにリスを見みることができて良よかった。

I was glad that we were able to see squirrels in addition to going to see the fall colors. (on top of that)

胡椒こしょうを頼たのんだら、御負おまけに一味いちみも付ついてきたから両方りょうほうかけてみた。

On top of the pepper I requested, they also brought some cayenne pepper powder. I gave them both a try. (what is more)

頼たのまれた仕事しごとを一生懸命いっしょうけんめいやったら、御負おまけにお菓子かしもくれた。

What's more I also got some sweets for working hard on the job that I was requested. (on top of that)

荷物にもつを運はこぶのを手伝てつだったら、御負おまけにコレクションを見みせてくれた。

I helped carry the baggage, what is more they showed me their collection. (in addition)

あの店みせはサービスが悪わるいし、御負おまけに言葉ことば遣づかいも悪わるいから、もう二度にどと行いかない。

That shop's service is terrible and, to make matters worse, they are not very polite, so I will not be going a second time.

この中古ちゅうこのコンピューターは、ハードディスクの速度そくどは速はやいし、御負おまけに安やすいし、すごくいいよ。

This second-hand computer has a hard disc that is fast, and what is more, it is cheap. It is really great. (on top of that)
