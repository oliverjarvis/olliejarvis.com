## About

About ずにはいられない

English

Japanese

As mentioned in our ずに lesson, ず is an auxiliary verb that is not commonly used in modern Japanese, except for in set expressions. It has the equivalent role of ない, in expressing that something 'has not', or 'will not' happen. The case marking particle に is then combined with the adverbial particle は, and the negative-potential form of いる, いられない. ずにはいられない may be translated as 'cannot help doing (A)', or 'cannot resist doing (A)'.

ずにはいられない will be attached to the indeterminate form of verbs. This is the same stem form as ない uses.

美味おいしそうなステーキが目めの前まえにあったら、食たべずにはいられない。

If there is a delicious looking steak in front of me, there is no way I can resist eating it.

この映画えいがを見みると誰だれでも泣なかずにはいられない。

No matter who you are, if you watch this movie, there is no way you can not cry.

Although ずにはいられない is often translated as 'cannot resist (A)', the literal meaning is simply 'cannot be without (A)'.

Caution

As the modern indeterminate form of する has several different forms, care will need to be taken when conjugating する verbs. While ない joins with し to create しない, ず will join with せ to create せず.

この１０じゅう年間ねんかん色々いろいろとお世話せわになったから、先輩せんぱいには感謝かんしゃをせずにはいられない。

This past 10 years, I have been helped a lot by my senpai, so there is no way I can go on without showing them some appreciation.

私わたしは猫ねこアレルギーなのでくしゃみをせずにはいられない。

I am allergic to cats so I can't not sneeze.

When you witness an accident, you can't help but help out.

それを思おもい出だすたびに恥はずかしくならずには居いられない。

Everytime I recall that I can't help but get embarrassed.

His strange faces are just too funny, I can't help but laugh.

This chocolate is just too good. I can't stop myself from eating it.

新あたらしいラーメン屋やを見みつけたら、入はいらずには居いられません。

If I find a new ramen shop, I can't stop myself from going in.

風邪かぜをひいたから、くしゃみをせずには居いられないんです。

I can't stop myself from sneezing because I have a cold.

Even if I am told 'don't worry about it,' I can't help but worry.

I can't help but complain. The trial was just too unfair.

Hearing his story, I can't help but feel sympathy for him.

Since I am so curious about the ending of a book I read only part of, I can't stop myself from reading it.
