## About

About はずがない

Like はず, coming from the kanji 筈はず, which suggests that something is 'bound' to be true, はずがない suggests that something is 'not bound' to be true. This expression is usually translated as 'hardly possible', 'improbable', or 'unlikely', but in reality, it is a bit weaker than these, and translates more literally to '(A) is not the only possibility'.

As はずがない (a combination of 筈はず, the case-marking particle が, and the い-Adjective ない) is a phrase that starts with a noun, it may be used after any word that could usually connect with a noun.

一いち時間じかんも早はやく家いえを出でたから遅おくれるはずがない。

Because I left the house an hour early, it is improbable that I will be late. (That I am bound to be late is not true)

この箱はこは重おもいはずがない。

It is hardly possible that this box is heavy. (It is not the case that this box is bound to be heavy)

サリーの家いえは立派りっぱなはずがない。

It is highly unlikely that Sally's house is splendid. (That Sally's house is sure to be splendid is not the case)

あそこにいる人ひとはトムのはずがない。

It is highly unlikely that the person over there is Tom.

Caution

There is a big difference in nuance between ないはずだ 'bound not to', and はずがない 'not bound to'. ないはず strongly negates the (A) statement, and expresses the speaker's opinion that it is almost definitely not true. はずがない on the other hand, simply suggests that (A) being almost 100% true is not the case.

彼かれの車くるまは高たかくないはずだ。

His car would not be expensive. (His car is bound to not be that expensive)

彼かれの車くるまは高たかいはずがない。

It is hardly possible that his car is expensive. (That his car is bound to be expensive is not true)

It is improbable to think that one could build a house on such a low salary.

Famous people are popular so it seems hardly possible for them to be lonely.

Since it's a boy's room, it is highly unlikely that it is clean.

It is hardly possible to get proficient at Japanese just by watching NHK.

He is strong, but it is improbable that he could lift a car.

One cannot study properly while watching TV. (Hardly possible)

I studied over 4 hours a day, so it is highly unlikely that I will fail the test.
