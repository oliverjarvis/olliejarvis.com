## About

About ながらも

English

Japanese

ながらも is a combination of the conjunction particle ながら, and the adverbial particle も. Coming from the kanji 乍ながら, with the meaning of 'while', ながら almost always keeps this nuance. Because of this, ながらも can be translated as 'even while (A), (B)', or 'although (A), also (B)'.

ながらも usually expresses surprise at the coexistence of (A) and (B), and may be used after い-Adjectives, な-Adjectives, nouns, or the conjunctive form of verbs.

才能さいのうを持もちながらも、彼かれは色々いろいろと苦労くろうした。

Although he is talented, he struggled with various things.

彼女かのじょは悲かなしみを感かんじながらも、笑顔えがおで友達ともだちに話はなしました。

Even while she was sad, she talked with her friends with a smile on her face.

彼かれは料理りょうりが苦手にがてながらも、一生懸命いっしょうけんめい作つくりました。

Although he is not good at cooking, he gave it his all and cooked.

私達わたしたちは貧乏びんぼうながらも、定期的ていきてきにコンサートへ行いっている。

Although we are poor, we go to concerts every now and then.

ながらも is used to highlight a thing that comes after the existing state of (A) 'そのままで後あとに続つづく事こと'. Due to this, ながらも may only be used with verbs that highlight states of existence (like feelings), rather than ongoing actions.

「こんなことしちゃだめだ」と思おもいながらもやってしまった。 (Action)

Even while I thought to myself, 'I shouldn't do something like this,' I did it.

緊張きんちょうしながらも、初はじめての舞台ぶたいでのびのびと歌うたうことが出来できた。 (Feeling)

Although I was nervous, I was able to sing comfortably at my first concert.
