## About

About ときたら

English

Japanese

ときたら is an expression used casually when expressing mild frustration toward (A) in a lighthearted manner. This structure is a combination of the case-marking particle と, and 来くる combined with the conjunction particle たら 'if', 来きたら. It will often simply be translated as 'that blasted (A)', or 'that damn (A)'. Despite this, the literal meaning is just 'when it comes to (A)'.

ときたら will directly follow nouns.

うちの息子むすこときたら、就職しゅうしょくもしないで家いえでダラダラしている。

When it comes to my son, he is not even trying to get a job, and he is just sitting around at home.

うちの弟おとうとときたら、朝あさから文句もんくばかり言いってくるから本当ほんとうにうざい。

This (darn) brother of mine, he always complains to me in the morning, and it is really annoying.

私わたしの上司じょうしときたら、自分じぶんが仕事しごとできないくせに少すこしでもミスをしたらものすごく怒おこるからみんなから嫌きらわれている。

When it comes to my boss, he gets super mad if you make the slightest mistake, even though he is not the best worker, so he is disliked by everyone at work.

This grammar pattern will almost exclusively appear in kana, so memorizing the stem verb it comes from will help to remember the meaning.

Fun Fact

In line with the literal meaning of 'when it comes to (A)', ときたら may also be used when the speaker wants to highlight something about (A) that is 'to be expected', or 'only natural'. Determining whether ときたら is being used in this way, or to express frustration, is usually quite clear in the second half of the statement.

うちの犬いぬときたら、家いえにお客きゃくさんが来くるとその人ひとに飛とびつくからもっと躾しつけをちゃんとしないといけない。

When it comes to my dog, when a guest comes to our place, he jumps on them so I need to train him properly. (To be expected)

カセットテープを使つかう機会きかいがなくなったため、最近さいきんの若者わかものときたら、カセットプレイヤーが使つかえない子達こたちが多おおい。

Since there is no opportunity to use cassette tapes, when it comes to the younger generation, there are many who do not know how to use cassette players. (Only natural)

Despite having two almost opposite nuances, it may help to think of ときたら as just 'when it comes to (A)' in all situations, with the (B) part of the phrase indicating whether it is positive or negative.

## Examples

Examples

Sentence
Translation

「義理ぎりの母ははときたら私わたしのことをぼやいてばかりよ。」

'That mother-in-law of mine, she always complains about me!'

「このプリンターときたら、忙いそがしい時ときに限かぎって故障こしょうするぞ！」

'This freaking printer of mine, it breaks only when I am busy.'

「娘むすめは真面目まじめに勉強べんきょうしているのに、息子むすこときたら・・・（何なにもやらない）」

'Even though my daughter studies very seriously, that son of mine... (Doesn't do anything at all)'

「うちの旦那だんなときたら、連休中れんきゅうちゅううちでゴロゴロばかりで、子こどもの面倒めんどうをみてくれないの。」

'That husband of mine, he is always loafing around during the holidays and doesn't take care of children!'

「隣となりの人ひとときたら、毎日まいにち、毎日まいにち、朝あさの６ろく時じからラジオを聞きくからうるさくて眠ねむれない。」

'That freaking neighbor of mine, every single day he listens to radio from 6 a.m. so loud that I can't sleep.'

「最近さいきんの若者わかものときたら・・・（年上としうえに敬意けいいを払はらっていない）」

'When it comes to young people these days… (they do not respect their elders!)'

末すえっ子この息子むすこときたら、また長男ちょうなんや次男じなんにおねだりをしているみたいだわ。

That youngest son of mine, it appears he is pestering his older brothers again!

あの子こときたら、また先生せんせいに媚こびを売うって内申点ないしんてんを稼かせごうとしている。

That blasted kid. She’s trying to get grades by buttering up the teacher again.

「武たけさんのお父とうさんは格好かっこういいよ。うちときたら水みずを節約せつやくしてとか電気でんきを無駄むだ使つかいするなとかうるさくて腹はらが立たつ。」

'Take, your father is so cool. When it comes to mine, he is always fussy, telling things like 'don't waste water' or 'don't waste electricity' and it pisses me off.'

他ほかの先進国せんしんこくには女性じょせいの議員ぎいんがもっといるのに、私わたしの国くにときたらほんの少すこししかいない。

There’re more female members of parliament in other countries, but my blasted country has nobody but a few.

友達ともだちは結婚けっこんとか妊娠にんしんとか昇進しょうしんとか人生じんせいの階段かいだんを順調じゅんちょうに上のぼっているのに私わたしときたら…。

Despite my friend rising through the stages of life smoothly with things like marriage, pregnancy, and a promotion, when it comes to me...

ネットが普及ふきゅうしてからのネット上じょうの情報じょうほうときたら、デマばかりで全まったく信用しんようできません。

When it comes to information on the internet since the internet’s popularization, there’s nothing but rumors, so you can’t trust it.

友達ともだちの彼氏かれしときたら、彼女かのじょを蔑ないがしろにして友達ともだちばかり優先ゆうせんするんだよ？早はやく別わかれちゃえばいいのにさ…。

When it comes to my friend’s boyfriend, he makes light of her and always prioritizes his friends, right? She should just hurry up and break up with him...

私わたしが飼かっているゴールデンレトリーバーときたら本当ほんとうにやんちゃで、少すこし目めを離はなせばいつも家いえが滅茶苦茶めちゃくちゃになっています。

That blasted Golden Retriever of mine, he’s so mischievous and as soon as I take my eyes off him even a little bit, the house gets destroyed.
