## About

About おわる

To express that actions are beginning, or ending in Japanese, the verbs 始はじめる (to commence), or 終おわる (to end) will need to be used. In this lesson, we will focus on actions that are 'ending'.

To say that (A) is ending/finishing, we will need to attach おわる to the ます stem of almost any verb. Whether you use the hiragana form, or the kanji form does not matter, as there will be no change in nuance.

荷物にもつは全部ぜんぶ届とどけ終おわりました。

I have finished delivering all the packages.

この車くるまのローンは明日あした払はらい終おわる。

I will finish paying the loan for this car tomorrow.

Due to おわる being a verb itself, the tense can be changed to show that something will end, is ending, or has ended.

ご飯はんを食たべ終おわるまで、デザートは禁止きんしだ。

You're not allowed to have dessert until you finish eating your food.

そのコーヒー、まだ飲のみ終おわってないの？

Have you not finished drinking that coffee?

長ながいレポートでしたが、今日きょうやっと読よみ終おわりました！

It was a long report, but today I finally finished reading it!

Caution

Unlike はじめる, which uses the transitive form, 終おわる uses the intransitive form of its transitive/intransitive pair. This is due to most actions in Japanese being considered to be things that 'come to an end' naturally (intransitive), rather than forcefully (transitive). However, there are exceptions to this, and 終おえる (the transitive version) may also be seen used as an auxiliary to other verbs.
