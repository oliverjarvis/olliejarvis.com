## About

About ところだった ②

English

Japanese

When combined with the dictionary form of verbs, ところだった will indicate that something was about to happen, and will regularly be translated as 'was just about to do (A)', or 'was in the middle of (A)'. In many cases, ところだった will indicate either a positive or negative result that came about from 'not doing (A)'.

As this grammar pattern is a combination of ところ 'place', and the past tense of だ, だった; it will put emphasis of 'having been in the place or situation of (A)', before something happened that caused the expected result to change.

危あぶなかった。危あやうくあの人ひとをひくところだった。

That was close. I was about to run over that person.

起おきるのが３さん分ぷん遅おそかったら、遅刻ちこくするところだった。

I was about to be late if I had woken up three minutes later.

In cases where a positive result was narrowly missed out on, のに will often appear directly after ところだった. This can be interpreted as 'despite almost doing (A), (B)'.

今いまから家いえを出でるところだったのに、いきなり雨あめが降ふってきたから雨あめが止やむまで待まつ。

I was just about to leave the house, but it suddenly started raining, so I'm going to wait until the rain stops.

もうすぐ犯人はんにんが自白じはくするところだったのに、お前まえが余計よけいなことを言いうから...。

The killer was just about to confess, but you went off on a tangent...

I was just about to apply the paint.

I was just about to narrowly miss the deadline.

今いまから関西弁かんさいべんを教おそわるところだったのに。

I was just about to be taught the Kansai dialect.

出掛でかけるところだったが、雨あめが降ふってきたのでやめました。

I was just about to go out, but because it started raining, I stopped.

捕つかまえた犯人はんにんを危あやうく逃のがすところだった。

The captured criminal was just about to barely escape.

悪友あくゆうと付つき合あって、寿命じゅみょうを縮ちぢめるところでした。

I was just about to become friends with a bad person and shorten my lifespan.

お風呂ふろに入はいっているところだったから、電話でんわに出でられませんでした。

Since I was in the middle of taking a bath, I couldn't answer the phone.

あんな人ひとだって知しらなかったら、危あやうく付つき合あうところだった。

I was just about to go out with that person, if I didn't know who they were.

今いま作つくるところだったけど、「夕飯ゆうはんはいらない」と連絡れんらくが入はいった。

I was just about to make it, but I got a message saying, 'I don't need dinner.'

携帯けいたいの電池でんちがなくなるところだったが、なんとか間まに合あった。

The battery on my phone was just about to die, but I somehow managed to make it in time.

やっと慣なれてきたところだったのに、仕事しごとを辞やめなければいけなくなった。

Despite just about getting used to it, I had to quit my job.

お湯ゆの温度おんどを調整ちょうせいしているところだったのに、ブレーカーが落おちてしまった。

Just as I was in the middle of adjusting the hot water temperature, the breaker unfortunately tripped.
