## About

About によると・によれば

English

Japanese

によると, a combination of the case marking particle に, the う - Verb よる, and the conjunction particle と, is a phrase that is used to express that something is 'according to (A)', or 'going off (A)'. In these cases, (A) is considered to be the source of some form of information which will be conveyed in the (B) part of the sentence.

This expression will be used after nouns, or with verbs that have been paired with ところ (to indicate the 'place' where the information came from).

今朝けさの天気てんき予報よほうによると、今夜こんやから雪ゆきが降ふるらしいですよ。

According to the forecast, it is apparently going to start snowing from tonight.

先生せんせいに聞きいたところによると、来週らいしゅうからオンライン授業じゅぎょうになるらしいよ。

Going off of what I heard from the teacher, we are going to be taking online classes from next week.

In newspapers and in articles, the more formal によりますと variant can also regularly be seen.

愛知あいち県警けんけいによりますと、犯人はんにんは四十よんじゅう代だいの男性だんせいだと言いう事ことです。

According to the Aichi Prefectural Police, the criminal is a 40 year old male.

However, this structure is not limited to using と, and the same meaning can be conveyed with によれば. This is just よる in its conjunctive form that pairs with the conjunction particle ば.

今朝けさのニュースによれば、来月末らいげつまつから海外かいがい旅行りょこうが出来できるようになる。

According to this morning's news, we are going to be able to travel abroad starting from the end of next month.

私わたしが聞きくところによると、部長ぶちょうが転職てんしょくするそうだ。

Going off of what I heard, the manager is going to be switching jobs.

Caution

Although によると and によれば are largely interchangeable, によると will convey a higher level of 'certainty', or 'reliability' in regard to the source of information. Due to this, によると is more likely to be used when referring to legitimate sources (police, news, etc).

警視庁けいしちょうによると、イマダ容疑者ようぎしゃは寝ねている母親ははおやを包丁ほうちょうで刺さしていたという事ことです。

According to the Tokyo Metropolitan Police department, Imada, the suspect, stabbed his mother who was sleeping, with a knife.

Fun Fact

よる is almost always written in hiragana alone. However, there are several different kanji that can be used to represent this verb. Each of these kanji have slightly different nuances, but they generally all represent the same 'base' meaning. This meaning is that '(B)'s foundation is (A)'. The most common kanji used for よる are as follows:

依よる - (B) depends on (A).

拠よる - (B) extends from (A).

因よる - (B) results from (A).

由よる - (B) is due to (A).

## Examples

Sentence
Translation

天気予報てんきよほうによると、明日あしたは晴はれです。

According to the weather forecast, tomorrow will be sunny.

警察けいさつによると、犯人はんにんが逃にげたそうです。

According to the police, the criminal escaped.

先輩せんぱいの話はなしによると、あの会社かいしゃはブラック企業きぎょうらしい。

According to what my senior said, that company is one of those 'black companies'.

目撃者もくげきしゃによれば、犯人はんにんは２０代にじゅうだい男性だんせいだ。

According to the witness, the criminal is a man in his 20s.

聞きくところによれば、彼かれはあの子こが好すきみたいだ。

According to what I hear, it seems as though he likes that girl.

統計とうけいによれば、明日あしたは雨あめが降ふる確率かくりつが高たかい。

According to statistics, there is a high probability it will rain tomorrow.

地震じしん速報そくほうによると、この地域ちいきは震度しんど３さんだ。

According to the Earthquake Early Warning System, this area is having an earthquake with an intensity of three.

医者いしゃによれば、二に、三さん日にちゆっくりすればよくなるらしい。

According to the doctor, I will get better if I take it easy for 2 or 3 days.

専門家せんもんかによれば、あの歌うたい方かただと喉のどに悪わるいらしい。

According to an expert, that style of singing is bad for your throat.

この地図ちずによるともうすぐ着つくはずなのに、家いえが全然ぜんぜん見みえてこない。

According to the map here, I should be arriving soon, but I don't see the house at all.

今朝けさの新聞しんぶんによると、あの犯人はんにんはまだ逃走とうそうしているようだ。

According to the newspaper issued this morning, that criminal is still on the run.

私わたしの母ははによると、毎日まいにち野菜やさいを食たべる必要ひつようがあるらしいです。

According to my mother, it is necessary to eat vegetables every day.
