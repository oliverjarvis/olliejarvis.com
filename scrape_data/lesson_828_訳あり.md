## About

About 訳あり(訳あって)

English

Japanese

訳わけあり is a phrase that is often used before nouns to express that there is a reason for something being a certain way. The reason is normally complicated or something that the speaker does not want to or cannot explain. 訳わけあり is literally made up of 訳わけ, 'reason' or 'cause', and the continuative form (連用形れんようけい) of ある.

訳わけあり will often be connected to nouns with の but sometimes it will be used like a な-adjective and be connected with な.

訳わけありの商品しょうひんは普通ふつうよりかなり安やすいことが多おおいから、いらなくてもつい買かっちゃう。

Products with a reason are way cheaper than usual, so I tend to buy them even if I don’t need them. (Defective products are way cheaper than usual, so I tend to buy them even if I don’t need them.)

最近さいきんなんでこのあたりが「訳わけありの地域ちいき」って言いわれてるかわかってきた気きがする。

Recently, I feel like I have a better grasp on why this area is known as the ‘Region with a reason’. (Recently, I feel like I have a better grasp on why this area is known as the ‘Defective Region’)

この会社かいしゃってなんかみんな訳わけありな人ひとだよね。

Everyone in this company is a person with a reason. (Everyone in this company has a colorful background)

この車くるまは訳わけありな車くるまだから、買かってすぐ壊こわれても修理代しゅうりだいはすべてこっちもち。

This car has a reason, so even if it breaks immediately after purchase, I have to pay out of pocket if anything happens to it. (This car is defective/has been in an accident, so even if it breaks immediately after purchase, I have to pay out of pocket if anything happens to it)

訳わけあり can also be seen directly before some words, without な or の, however these are normally considered to be fixed expressions. This is often a way to euphemistically avoid giving negative descriptions of things.

訳わけあり商品しょうひんは欠陥けっかんがあっても、安やすいからすぐ売うれることが多おおい。

Even if a defective product has defects, they sell fast because they are cheap.

私わたしが住すんでいるところは訳わけあり物件ぶっけんだから、東京駅とうきょうえきから徒歩とほ４分よんぷんで２ＬＤＫにエルディーケーだけど家賃やちんが５ご万まんなんだよ！

The place that I am staying at is a place with reason, so the rent is only 50,000 even though it is a 2LDK that is a 4 minute walk from Tokyo station. (The place that I am staying at is a stigmatized property, so the rent is only 50,000 even though it is a 2LDK that is a 4 minute walk from Tokyo station.)

Fun Fact

A closely related phrase is 訳わけあって, meaning 'for a reason (which isn't specified)'. It is often used at the start of sentences when the speaker wants to say that there are some deeper reasons behind what they're saying, however they do not want to or cannot explicitly say what those reasons are. When not used in front of a noun, 訳わけあり can also be used in a similar way.

訳わけあって、明日あしたから数日間すうじつかん出勤しゅっきんできません。

Due to reasons, I will not be able to come into work for a few days starting tomorrow.

色々いろいろと訳わけありで訳わけありで、来年らいねんから日本にほんで生活せいかつすることが決きまりました。

Due to many reasons, I will be living in Japan starting next year.
