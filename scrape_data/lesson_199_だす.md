## About

About だす

だす, or 出だす in its kanji form is a verb that is regularly attached to the conjunctive form of other verbs. When it is connected in this way, it has the meaning of 'to suddenly do (A)', or 'to burst into (A)'.

To use this construction, simply add だす to the ます stem (conjunctive form) of the verb that you want to highlight as happening 'suddenly'.

最初さいしょから速はやく走はしりだすのは体からだに良よくない。

It is not good for your body to suddenly start running fast.

転ころんだ息子むすこが泣なきだす前まえに、あめをあげる。

I give my son candy before he bursts into tears after falling.

Caution

Unlike はじめる, another common structure which means 'to begin', だす quite often has the nuance of being unintentional, or something that the subject cannot avoid doing (or cannot be controlled). On the other hand, はじめる expresses the direct intention of the subject to do (A).

コンサートで歌手かしゅが歌うたを歌うたい始はじめる。

At the concert, the singer will start singing. (Something that has been planned to be started)

最近さいきん、友達ともだちが授業じゅぎょう中ちゅうに歌うたいだした。

Recently, my friend suddenly burst into song during class. (Something that happened without any prior plan/expectation)

Sentence
Translation

あの子こは急きゅうに泣なき出だした。

That child suddenly started crying.

面白おもしろいことを今いま思おもい出だした。

I suddenly remembered something funny.

晴はれていたのに、雨あめが急きゅうに降ふり出だした。

Although it was clear, it suddenly began raining.

夜中よなかに夫おっとが急きゅうに餃子ぎょうざを作つくりだした。

My husband suddenly started to make dumplings in the middle of the night.

何なにも言いわないで走はしり出だした。

Without saying a word, he suddenly began running.

彼かれは日本語にほんごが出来できないと思おもっていたのに、日本語にほんごでしゃべり出だした。

Although I thought that he couldn't speak any Japanese, he suddenly started speaking in Japanese.

今日きょうは公園こうえんに行いきたかったが、雨あめが降ふり出だしたから家いえでゲームをする。

I wanted to go to the park today, but because it started raining, I am going to play video games at home.

彼かれは答こたえを言いい出だす時ときに、くしゃみをした。

He sneezed just as he started to say the answer. (sudden)

乾杯かんぱいと言いわないで、飲のみ出だした。

Without saying 'cheers', I started drinking.

警察官けいさつかんが後うしろを向むいた時ときに犯人はんにんが逃にげ出だした。

When the police officer turned her back, the criminal suddenly began to run away.

一番いちばん悲かなしいシーンで彼かれが笑わらい出だした。

He suddenly started laughing at the most somber scene.

この辺あたりは子供こどもが多おおいので、急きゅうに飛とび出だしてくることがあります。

There are many children in this area, so there are times when they suddenly jump out (e.g. in front of cars). (start to)
