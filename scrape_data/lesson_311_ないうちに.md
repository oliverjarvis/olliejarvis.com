## About

About ないうちに

English

Japanese

In contrast to うちに, which is often used to express the 'optimal', or 'best' time within which something should occur, ないうちに expresses the time within which something 'does not' occur, and results in (or causes) something negative, or will cause something negative if not dealt with. This means that 'before (A) happens (B)', or 'without (A) happening, (B).

うちに will be paired with verbs in their ない (negative) form, before the (B) phrase highlights something that 'is done to prevent (A) from happening', or 'happened unfortunately during (A)'.

こうき君くん、全然ぜんぜん会あわないうちに、凄すごく大おおきくなったね！

Kouki-kun, you have grown so much during the time we haven't met. (Since the last time I saw you)

また電話でんわをかけ忘わすれるところだった。忘わすれないうちに電話でんわしておかなきゃ。

I was just about to forget to make a phone call again. I should call before I forget.

Fun Fact

前まえに and ないうちに are used in very similar situations, with the primary difference being that ないうちに focuses more on unfavorable results.

出でかける前まえにバイオリンの練習れんしゅうをしないといけない。

I need to practice playing my violin before I go out.

知しらないうちに家いえの前まえにあるラーメン屋やが潰つぶれていた。

Without realizing it, the ramen shop in front of my house went out of business.

Come back home before it gets dark.

Before I knew it, my friend was divorced.

冷さめない内うちにお召めし上あがりください。

Please eat it before it gets cold.

眠ねむくならない内うちに宿題しゅくだいを終おわらせよう。

Let's finish the homework before I get sleepy.

いくらも泳およがない内うちに、向むこう岸ぎしについてしまった。

Without swimming that much, I reached the other shore.

まだ親したしくならない内うちに、同僚どうりょうが辞やめてしまった。

Before we became close, my co-worker quit.

先方せんぽうの気きが変かわらない内うちに、契約けいやくを済すませてください。

Close the deal with them before they change their mind.

第だい１いっ巻かんを読よみ終おわらない内うちに、第だい２に巻かんが出でてしまった。

The second volume came out before I had finished reading the first volume.

忘わすれない内うちに言いっておきますが、私わたしは来週らいしゅうから出張しゅっちょうです。

I should tell you, before I forget, I am going on a business trip next week.

お客きゃくさんが何人なんにんも集あつまらない内うちに、スピーチが始はじまった。

The speech began before many people had gathered.

気きづかない内うちに、列車れっしゃが出発しゅっぱつしてしまっていた。

Before I could notice, the train had started to depart (without me). (without noticing・before noticing occurs)

飽あきられない内うちに、新商品しんしょうひんを出だした方ほうがいいな。

I think we should introduce a new product before the people lose interest.
