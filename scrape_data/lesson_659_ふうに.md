## About

About ふうに

English

Japanese

風ふう 'wind', 'air', commonly appearing as kana, is a suffix in Japanese used to indicate that something is in the style, manner, or method of what comes before it.

This use of ふう appears following verbs, and works in the same way as a な-Adjective. This means that it will be followed by に when being used adverbially to further describe something that is being done 'in the way of (A)'.

彼かれは仕事しごとをやっているふうに見みえるけど、ただそう見みえるだけで、何なにもしていない。

He looks like he is doing his job, but it just looks like it and he isn't actually doing anything.

先輩せんぱいには頑張がんばっているふうには見みえないかもしれないけど、これでもめちゃくちゃ頑張がんばっているんです。

Senpai, it may not look like I'm working hard, but this is me trying my hardest.

Also, it is common for ふうに to be used after pre-noun adjectival words such as こんな, そんな, and あんな. This just highlights that something is being done 'like this', 'like that', etc.

こんなふうに結むすんだらすぐ解ほどけちゃうよ。

If you tie a knot like this, it will get undone easily.

そんなふうに私わたしをみないでよ。私わたしは本当ほんとうに何なにもやってないから！

Don't look at me like that. I really didn't do anything!

あんなふうには絶対ぜったいなりたくない。

I never want to be like that.

Fun-fact

For ease of remembering, coming from the kanji for 風かぜ 'wind', ふう can be thought of as similar to English phrases like 'to have an air of (A)', etc. However, this is just a tool for memorization, as the overlap in meaning is not 100%.

## Examples

Examples

Sentence
Translation

いつか自分じぶんもこんなふうになりたい。

At some point, I too want to become like this (way).

どんなふうにやればいいのか分わからない。

I don't know in what way doing it would be good. (I don't know how to do it)

どうすればあんなふうになれるのだろうか。

I wonder what it would take to (what if done would) be able to become that way.

兄あにはすぐに嘘うそをつく。僕ぼくはそんなふうにはなりたくない。

My older brother will instantly lie. I don't want to become like that (way).

聞きいていないふうに振舞ふるまっているが、本当ほんとうは聞きいている。

He acts in a way that suggests he isn't listening, but he really is listening.

あの態度たいどは、昇進しょうしんを考かんがえているふうには見みえない。

With that attitude, (he) doesn't appear to be thinking about a promotion. (In that way)

見みているふうではなかったが、彼かれはよく見みているタイプです。

He didn't act in a way that suggested that he was looking, but he's the type who watches closely.

彼かれはお金かねがないふうに見みえるが、大だい富豪ふごうです。

He looks like he doesn't have money, but he is extremely rich. (In a way that suggests)

知しらないふうにしていたが、顔がおに出でてバレてしまった。

I was acting in a way that suggested I didn't know, but it accidentally showed on my face.

彼かれは日本語にほんごが喋しゃべれないふうにしているが、実じつはペラペラだ。

He acts in a way that suggests he can't speak Japanese, but he is actually fluent.

飛とべないふうに見みえる鶏にわとりの中なかにも、かなり飛とべるものもいる。

Even among the chickens that look like (in a way that suggests) they cannot fly, there are ones that can fly well.

思おもってもいないのに、好すきだと思おもっているふうに装よそおうのは不誠実ふせいじつだ。

Pretending in a way that suggests you think you like it, even though you really don't, is insincere.
