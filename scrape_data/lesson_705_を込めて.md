## About

About を込めて

English

Japanese

をこめて, or を込こめて when appearing in its kanji form, is a phrase that expresses when something is 'full of (A)', or 'filled with (A)'. The structure is comprised of を, and the て-form of the る-Verb 込こめる, which means 'to pack into', or 'to load with'. In this way, the literal meaning is fairly consistent with the standard English translations.

をこめて will appear directly after the noun expressing the particular feeling or emotion being discussed.

感謝かんしゃの気持きもちを込こめて、１いち年間ねんかんお世話せわになった先生せんせいに花束はなたばをあげた。

Full of the feeling of gratitude, I gave a bouquet of flowers to a teacher who has taken care of me for the past year.

夫おっとは毎晩まいばん、愛あいを込こめて晩御飯ばんごはんを作つくってくれる。

My husband cooks dinner filled with love every night.

歌うたうのが下手へたでも、心こころを込こめて歌うたえば上手じょうずに聞きこえる。

Even if you are not a good singer, you can sound like a good singer if you sing with all your heart.

Caution

It is important to note that this particular structure is treated as a set phrase, and will primarily only be used when some particular feeling or emotion is the thing that is being 'packed into (A)'.
