## About

About げ

English

Japanese

げ, sometimes seen in the kanji form 気げ is a suffix in Japanese that expresses that something is 'giving off the feeling of (A)', or 'seems like (A)'. げ is frequently used in relation to emotions, feelings, and things that the speaker generally can't be 100% sure of due to them not being directly visible.

げ is primarily seen used after the stem form of い-Adjectives, but may also appear following the stem forms of nouns of な-Adjectives. In all of these cases, the addition of げ creates a new な-Adjective meaning '(A) seeming'. Due to this, げ may then be followed by either な or に, depending on whether it is altering a verb or a noun later in the sentence.

自信じしんありげに見みえたかもしれないけど、実じつはめちゃくちゃ緊張きんちょうしてて吐はきそうだった。

I may have given off the appearance of being confident, but actually, I was super nervous and felt like I was going to throw up.

高校生こうこうせいたちがファストフード店てんで楽たのしげに話はなしているのを見みて、自分じぶんが高校生こうこうせいの頃ころを思おもい出だした。

When I saw highschool students seemingly having a good time talking to each other at a fast food restaurant, it reminded me of the time when I was a highschooler.

高橋たかはしくんは不安ふあんげに手てを上あげました。

Seemingly nervous, Takahashi-kun raised his hand.

げ is more subjective (based on the speaker's opinion) than そう, and is considered to be very low confidence. This may be considered a byproduct of the original 気き, meaning of 'attention', referring to the way that the speaker 'senses (A)', regardless of what is actually true for (A) on the inside.

I wonder about things that are too refined and therefore don't give off the appearance of being cute.

Seemingly embarrassed, he told us about his weak points.

涼すずし気げな顔かおでマラソンを走はしりきるんだから大たいしたもんだよ。

It was considerable that he finished running the marathon while seemingly undeterred.

彼女かのじょはとても素直すなおだが、どことなく儚はかな気げなのが気きになります。

She is very honest, but I am concerned by her somehow fickle appearance.

子供こどもたちが羨うらやまし気げに見みているから、お菓子かしを分わけてあげた。

Looking at the children they were giving the appearance of being envious, so I divided up the candy and gave it to them.

楽たのし気げに遊あそぶ子供こどもを見みるたびに、私わたしは子供こどものころを思おもい出だす。そして思おもわず笑顔えがおになる。

Every time I see children seemingly enjoying themselves, I recall my childhood. And, without thinking, I smile.

優勝ゆうしょうした選手せんしゅを見みて彼かれは悔くやし気げな表情ひょうじょうをしていた。

Looking at the winning athlete, his facial expressions gave off the appearance of being frustrated.

彼女かのじょが意味いみあり気げな表情ひょうじょうで僕ぼくを見みつめている。何なにかあったのかな？

She is staring at me with the facial expression that gives off the appearance of knowing something about me. I wonder if something happened.

Ａエー：「どうしてそんなに満足まんぞく気げなの？」
Ｂビー：「日本語にほんご能力のうりょく試験しけん、合格ごうかくしたんだよ！」

A: 'Why are you giving off the appearance of being so satisfied?'
B: 'I passed the Japanese Language Proficiency Test!'

彼女かのじょは私わたしの言葉ことばを聞きいて悲かなし気げな顔かおで出でかけた。どうしてそんな態度たいどを取とったのか分わからない。

She left with a seemingly sad look upon her face after hearing my words. I don't understand the change in her demeanor.

誰だれかに「私わたしの言いうとおりにすれば、いつの間まにかお金持かねもちになれるんだ」と言いわれると、頭あたまの中なかに赤あかランプが点灯てんとうして、これはなにか怪あやし気げな儲もうけ話ばなしだと思おもうようになる。このような人ひとには、いつも気きを付つけなければならない。

When someone says: 'Do as I say, and you will get rich in no time', a warning light in my head turns on and I begin to think that 'this gives off the appearance of a suspicious get-rich-quick scheme'. One must always be wary of people like this.

父ちちが懐なつかし気げに自分じぶんの子供こどもの頃ころの話はなしをしてくれた。

My father talked about his childhood while giving off the appearance of nostalgia.
