## About

About あっての

English

Japanese

あっての is a pattern which emphasizes that (B) is something that only exists because of (A). It will often be translated as '(B) owes everything to (A)', or '(B) existing is only due to (A)'. This structure is a combination of the う-Verb ある 'to be' in its て-form, and the case-marking particle の.

あっての will be preceded and followed directly by nouns, where the first noun is (A), and the second noun is (B).

お客様きゃくさまあっての仕事しごとだと思おもうのですが、理不尽りふじんなクレームを言いわれるとちょっとイラっときます。

My job only exists because of the customers, but I get a little annoyed when people make unreasonable complaints.

今回こんかいは皆様みなさまの努力どりょくあっての結果けっかです。皆様みなさま本当ほんとうにありがとうございました。

This time, the results are because of your efforts. Thank you very much.

あっての will primarily be used when (B) is something that benefits positively from (A), and therefore is showing or should show some kind of gratitude toward it.

Occasionally, the case-marking particle が will come between the (A) noun and あっての. However, it is often omitted.

私わたしはお金かねがなかったら愛あいもない、お金かねがあっての幸しあわせだと思おもいます。

I think that without money, there’s no love and happiness is something that exists only due to money.

Fun Fact

あっての can be thought of as an evolution of the basic grammar structure がある 'to have'. The main difference being that の allows (B) to be marked as the thing that has (A), while the て-form indicates that there is more information that will be given later about why this is important. Since this information is an explanation, (B) will almost always be followed by structures like から, or ので.

## Examples

Examples

Sentence
Translation

彼かれが大学だいがくをやめるのは、何なにか理由りゆうがあってのことだろう。

I guess he is dropping out of university only because some sort of reason exists.

「あなたあってのわたしです。あなたがいなければ私わたしの人生じんせいは無意味むいみですよ。」

'I can only exist because of you. My life would be meaningless without you.' (owe everything to)

支配人しはいにん、社員しゃいんに：「ゲストあっての遊園地ゆうえんちだということを忘わすれてはいけません。」

Manager to employees: 'You must not forget that the amusement park exists only because of the customers.'

恋愛れんあいは相手あいてあってのことだから一人ひとりだけでは無理むりだ...と思おもっていませんか？

Love only exists because you have a partner, so it is impossible to love when you are alone… don't you think? (owe everything to)

部員ぶいんあってのクラブ活動かつどうなんだから、部員ぶいんに対たいしてもっと優やさしくするべきだ。

Since club activities exist only because of our club members, we must be nicer to them.

娘むすめ：「お金かねと愛あい、どっちが大事だいじだと思おもう？」母はは：「そうね〜、お金かねあっての幸しあわせだと思おもうわ。」

Daughter: 'Money and love, which do you think is more important?'Mom: 'Hmm, happiness exists only because of money I think.'

兄あにが一人ひとり暮ぐらしを始はじめ、私わたしたちの家族かぞくは兄あにあっての家族かぞくだった事ことが分わかった。

When he started living independently, I realized that my family existed only because of my older brother.

君きみが志望しぼう校こうに合格ごうかくしたのは君きみの努力どりょくあっての成果せいかで、僕ぼくはただ側そばにいただけさ。

Successfully getting your first-preference school was only possible due to your own efforts, all I did was stay by your side. (owe everything to)

結婚けっこんという契約けいやくは愛あいあってのものであって、愛あいの無ない結婚けっこんなど全まったく意味いみを成なしません。

The contract of marriage owes everything to love, and something such as a loveless marriage will amount to nothing.

お客きゃく様さまや取引とりひき先さき相手あいてあってのビジネスなので、ビジネスにおいて礼儀れいぎを欠かく事ことは命取いのちとりになります。

Since we are a business that owes everything to our customers and clients, lacking manners in business would prove fatal.

ベストセラー作者さくしゃの演説えんぜつ：「 私わたしの本ほんは、家族かぞくの支ささえあってのものです。息子むすこと夫おっとがいなければここまでやってこれなかったと思おもいます。」

Best Selling author's speech: 'My books exist only because of the support from my family. I believe, if it was not for my son and husband, I would not have gotten this far.' (owe everything to)

切手きってのコレクションが火事かじで全焼ぜんしょうして悲かなしいのは分わかっているけどさ、生いきて逃にげられたのは何なによりだよ。「命いのちあっての物種ものだね」だね。

I understand that you are sad because your stamp collection was destroyed in the fire, but getting out of there with your life is more important than anything. 'While there is life, there is hope,' right? (only exist because of)
