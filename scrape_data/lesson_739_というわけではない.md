## About

About というわけではない

English

Japanese

と言いうわけではない is often considered an emphasized version of わけではない in stating that something 'doesn't mean that (A)', '(A) is not always the case', or 'it's not that (A)'. Being that という often presents (A) as a matter for discussion, or an important quotation structure, it will regularly be used to refer back to something that another person has said, in order to clarify that it may not be 100% accurate.

というわけではない may be used after any structure that could usually be paired with という, and a more literal translation may be thought of as 'it is not so that (A) can be said'. Although not required, だ will often be seen between nouns and な-Adjectives, and というわけではない.

日本にほんに２年間にねんかん住すんでいたからと言いって、日本語にほんごを話はなせるというわけではない。

Just because I lived in Japan for two years does not mean I speak Japanese.

数学すうがくが嫌きらいというわけではない、ただ苦手にがてなだけだ。

It's not that I don't like math, I'm just not good at it.

いつもニコニコしているからって、親切しんせつだというわけではない。

Just because they are always smiling doesn't mean they are nice.

離婚りこんというわけではないが、しばらく別居べっきょすることになった。

It was not a divorce, but we had to live separately for a while.

と言いうわけではない will regularly be used when the speaker is denying that something said earlier is the reason for something. This could either be a misunderstanding that they themselves want to avoid, or something that the listener asked them to clarify.

Fun Fact

When used in the positive form, という訳わけだ is often attached to the end of statements that are considered to be either obvious, or clear due to context or common sense. This form will just be translated as 'so that means (A)', or 'so the reason is (A)'.

ということは、彼女かのじょは彼かれを助たすけるために、彼かれを押おし倒たおしたという訳わけだ。

So that means that she pushed him in order to save him.

彼かれがこの国くにに入国にゅうこくできないのは、以前いぜんこの国くにで犯罪はんざいを犯おかしたからというわけだ。

So the reason why he cannot enter this country is because he has committed crimes in this country in the past.

苦情くじょうという訳わけではなく、提案ていあんです。

It is not (that it is) a complaint, it is a suggestion. (not the reason)

全すべてのキノコが毒どくを持もっているという訳わけではない。

It is not the case that all mushrooms are poisonous. (not the reason)

儀式ぎしきという訳わけではないけど、あれはもはや儀式ぎしきに近ちかい。

It's not to say that it's a ritual, but at this point it's close enough to being a ritual. (not the case)

正式せいしきな基準きじゅんという訳わけではないですが、参考さんこうにしてください。

It is not that it is officially the norm, but please take note. (not the reason)

警備けいびという訳わけではないが、近ちかくにいてもらえるとありがたいです。

It doesn't mean that (I need) security, but I would be grateful if you stayed near me. (not the reason)

見学けんがくという訳わけではないが、工場こうじょうの中なかを見みせてくれた。

It is not that it was an inspection, but they let me see the inside of the factory. (not the reason)

謙遜けんそんという訳わけではないが、あなたからいろいろ学まなびたいことがある。

It is not that I am being modest, I want to learn a number of things from you. (not the reason)

突拍子とっぴょうしもない発想はっそうに見みえるが、空想くうそうという訳わけではない。

It may look like an astounding concept, but it doesn't mean that it is a fantasy. (not the reason)

このパソコンは一番いちばん速はやいという訳わけではないのに、多おおくの人ひとに買かわれた。いったい何なんなんだ？

This computer, despite not being the fastest, many people bought it. What the heck? (not the case/reason)

休業きゅうぎょうという訳わけではないのだが、お客きゃくさんが来こないから休業きゅうぎょうみたいなものだ。

It is not that it is a business holiday, but since customers don't come in, it is like a business holiday. (not the reason)

月給げっきゅうという訳わけではないが、毎月まいつき収入しゅうにゅうが得えられる仕事しごとの依頼いらいがあった。

It is not that I got a monthly salary, but I was on a commision job where I would receive income each month. (not the reason)

母はは：「何なんでこれを食たべないの？あたしの料理りょうり、嫌きらいなの？」
息子むすこ：「嫌きらいだという訳わけではないけど、単たんに今いまは食たべたくない。」

Mother: 'Why aren't you eating? Do you hate my cooking?'
Son: 'It is not the case that I hate it, I am simply not hungry at the moment.' (not the reason)
