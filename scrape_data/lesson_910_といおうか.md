## About

About といおうか

English

Japanese

と言いおうか is an expression that combines the case-marking particle と in its quotation use, the volitional form of the う-Verb 言いう, 言いおう ‘to say’, and the adverbial particle か used for mid-sentence questions. It may be translated as ‘how to put it’, or ‘perhaps it’s (A), or perhaps it’s (B)’. However, the more literal ‘shall I say it is (A), or shall I say it is (B)’ is also a common interpretation.

This expression will be used twice, where both the (A) and the (B) phrase are being questioned by the speaker. As with many other uses of と of a quotation, the preceding word may be almost anything.

また彼女かのじょに誕生日たんじょうびを忘わすれられたんだ。あきれていると言いおうか、傷きずついたと言いおうか、よくわからない。毎年まいとしのことだから慣なれてきたけど…。

My girlfriend forgot my birthday again. How shall I put it, I don’t know whether I am disappointed or sad. Well, this happens every year so I’m starting to get used to it…

あいつの運転うんてんは荒あらいと言いおうか、危あぶないと言いおうか、とにかくあいつが運転うんてんする車くるまには乗のりたくない。

How shall I put it, his driving is rough, perhaps dangerous, either way I don't want to get in a car operated by him.

自分じぶんの体からだを使つかってお客きゃくさんの車くるまを守まもろうとするなんて、バカと言いおうか、真面目まじめすぎると言いおうか分わからない。

How shall I put it, I don't know whether to call him stupid or too serious for trying to protect a customer's car with his own body.

彼かれはいとこと言いおうか、ハトコと言いおうか、私わたしもどういう関係かんけいかはわからないけど親戚しんせきだということはわかっている。

How shall I put it, I don’t know if he is my cousin or my second cousin. I don’t know how we are related, but I know that we are relatives.

と言いおうか is interchangeable with the more common と言いうか, but it is advisable to only use it when specific emphasis needs to be made, or for some type of dramatic effect. In many cases, it will sound unnatural in daily speech.

と言いおうか will frequently be used with fairly vague or imprecise descriptions, in order to avoid but hint at what the speaker actually wants to say. Often, this will be something negative, and will therefore be omitted.

お前まえは努力どりょくが足たりないと言いおうか、才能さいのうがないと言いおうか…。（俺おれにはお前まえがこの仕事しごとに向むいていないと思おもう）

How shall I put it, you aren’t trying hard enough, or perhaps you are not talented… (I think that you are not suited for this job)
