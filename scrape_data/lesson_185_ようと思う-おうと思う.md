## About

About 〜ようと思う・〜おうと思う

The combination of a verb with the auxiliary verb う, or よう, followed by the う-Verb 思おもう gives us the common expression ようと思おもう. This expression is often translated as 'I think I will (A)', or 'I shall (A)'. It is also close to the casual expression 'I'm gonna (A)'.

Remembering that う and よう can have one of three possible meanings in Japanese (as discussed in the よう grammar point), ようと思おもう is usually only used to express one of those three. That 'one' is as follows:

意志いし - Expresses intentions (has a similar meaning to ～するつもりだ).

このゲームはもうあきらめようと思おもう。

I think I will give up on this game already. (I have arrived at the conclusion that I will give it up)

私わたしは明日あしたから電車でんしゃで通かよおうと思おもう。

I think I shall commute by train from tomorrow. (I have arrived at the conclusion that I will do it)

来月らいげつ、バイクを買かおうと思おもう。

I think I will buy a bike next month. (I have arrived at the conclusion that I will buy one)

This expression is also often used with 思おもっている. In these cases, it highlights that the intention of the speaker is something that they regularly think, or have been thinking about for some time.

今晩こんばんはレストランで美味おいしいものを食たべようと思おもっているけど、ナオミも来くる？

I am thinking of eating something delicious at a restaurant tonight, but do you want to come too, Naomi?

思おもっている may also be used when describing the intentions of another person, as discussed in our lesson about と思おもう.

タケル、お前まえは絶対ぜったいサチコと別わかれようと思おもっているでしょう。

Takeru, you are definitely thinking of breaking up with Sachiko, aren't you?

Caution

Let's be careful to remember the difference between the auxiliary verbs よう, and ようだ. They are different constructions that have different meanings, and behave in unique ways grammatically.

明日あした、勉強べんきょうをしようと思おもう。

I think I will study tomorrow.

来年らいねん、家いえを買かおうと思おもう。

I think I will buy a house next year.

帰かえってから、お菓子かしを食たべようと思おもいます。

I think I will eat candy after returning home.

今日きょうから文法ぶんぽうの勉強べんきょうを始はじめようと思おもう。

I think I will start studying grammar from today.

息子むすこはレゴを欲ほしがっているから、買かってあげようと思おもう。

My son wants lego, so I think I will buy him some.

牛乳ぎゅうにゅうがないから、スーパーに行いこうと思おもう。

There is no milk, so I think I will go to the store.

冷蔵庫れいぞうこに入はいっているチーズを誰だれも食たべないから、食たべようと思おもう。

Since no one is going to eat the cheese in the refrigerator I think I will eat it.

この週末しゅうまつは雪ゆきがいっぱい降ふるから、スキーをしに行いこうと思おもう。

This weekend it will snow a lot, so I think I will go skiing.

私わたしは家事かじをしたくないから、弟おとうとにしてもらおうと思おもう。

I don't want to do housework, so I think I will have my little brother do it for me.

後あとでクッキーを作つくろうと思おもっているけど、あなたも一緒いっしょに作つくる？

I am thinking I will make cookies later, but do you want to make them with me?

今日きょうは面白おもしろそうなテレビ番組ばんぐみがあるから、見みようと思おもう。

There is a TV program on today that looks interesting, so I think I will watch it.

先生せんせいが説明せつめいをしてくれたことがあまり分わからなかったから、後あとで先生せんせいに聞きこうと思おもう。

I didn't really understand what the teacher was explaining, so I think I will ask the teacher about it later.
