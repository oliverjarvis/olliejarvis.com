## About

About はたして

English

Japanese

果はたして, being the て-form of the う-Verb 果はたす 'to fulfill', or 'to come to fruition', is an expression that is used adverbially to strengthen phrases of speculation. It may be translated as 'I wonder if (A)', 'how actually (A)', or 'how really (A)'.

果はたして will often be seen paired with other structures that indicate speculation, such as だろうか, でしょうか, かな, or かしら; and can be thought of as just emphasizing them further.

As this grammar point will be used adverbially, it will primarily appear at the beginning of sentences.

果はたして彼かれが言いっていることは本当ほんとうなのだろうか。

I wonder if what he is saying is the truth.

果はたして、あのやり方かたで本当ほんとうに成功せいこうするのでしょうか。

I wonder if that approach will really work.

In sentences that do not include other speculative grammar patterns such as だろうか, 果はたして will regularly be translated as 'sure enough', or 'as expected'.

彼かれは取引先とりひきさきの信頼しんらいを失うしなう事ことをしてしまった。果はたして、彼かれはクビになった。

He did something to lose the trust of his clients. As expected he was fired.

彼女かのじょは自分じぶんの意見いけんを彼かれに押おし付つけようとしたが、果はたして彼かれは自分じぶんの意見いけんを変かえなかった。

She tried to push her opinions onto him, but as expected he did not change his.

Fun-fact

Coming from the base meaning of 果はたす 'to come to fruition', 果はたして can be thought of as similar to 'in the end' in almost all cases, as it merely expresses the speakers thought process about 'what will' or 'what has' happened 'in the end'.

大学院だいがくいんに進学しんがくするのは果はたして私わたしにメリットがあるのでしょうか？

I wonder if going on to graduate school will actually benefit me in the end.

彼かれ以外いがい誰だれも仕事しごとに来こなかったため、果はたして彼かれが全員ぜんいんの仕事しごとをすることになった。

Since no one came to work except him, he ended up having to do everyone's job in the end as expected.

If using this translation to memorize 果はたして, it should also be noted that the nuance of what happens 'in the end' is almost always something that the speaker either expected, or assumed would happen in the first place.

At the symposium, scientists wondered if time travel is really possible.

私わたしが今いまの二倍にばいの給料きゅうりょうを得えるとしたら、東京とうきょうで３さんＬＤＫエルディーケーの家いえを果はたして買かえるだろうか。

I wonder if I would actually be able to buy a 3LDK in Tokyo, assuming that I earn twice as much as I do now.

Yuki-san and Tomoko-san had been going out for a long time. Sure enough, they got married.

A certain person tried to prove that the Earth was flat, however, just as expected that person failed to do so.

Lately, I often hear the slogan 'Sport is equal to health'. I wonder if that is really the case?
