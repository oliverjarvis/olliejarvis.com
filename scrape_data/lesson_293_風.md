## About

About 風

風ふう is a suffix that is used in Japanese to convey the meaning of 'in the style of (A)', or 'in the fashion of (A)'. Literally, this translates to 'in the wind of (A)', which is just the Japanese way of expressing the former statements.

To use 風ふう, attach it to the end of a noun that you want to label something as being in the 'style' of.

９０きゅうじゅう年ねん代だい風ふうファッションがまた流行はやってきています。

90's style fashion is starting to gain popularity again.

アメリカ風ふう料理りょうりが好すきです。

I like American style food.

の may also be attached to the end of 風ふう, before connecting it to another noun. This will show that '(B) is in the style of (A)'.

メキシコ風ふうの料理りょうりを食たべたいです。

I want to eat Mexican food.

広島ひろしま風ふうのお好このみ焼やきが一番いちばんおいしい。

Hiroshima-style okonomiyaki is the most delicious.

Caution

As a compound structure, this suffix is always pronounced ふう, never かぜ (the standard reading of 風かぜ).

Translation

My mother likes Dutch-style salad.

パブは韓国かんこく風ふうのほうがいいと思おもう。

When it comes to pubs, I think I prefer the Korean style.

中国ちゅうごく風ふうのドレスがほしいよ！

I want the Chinese-style dress!

このイラストは漫画まんが風ふうだね。

This illustration is in manga style, right?

それはアメリカ風ふうの生活せいかつと言いわれている。

It is said that that is the American style of life.

「なあんだ。ここには関西かんさい風ふうのお好このみ焼やきしかないのか。」

'What?! They only have Kansai-style okonomiyaki here?'

日本にほんでは洋よう風ふうの結婚式けっこんしきが人気にんきです。

A western-style marriage ceremony is popular in Japan.

新聞しんぶんを読よむサラリーマン風ふうの中年男ちゅうねんおとこがひとりで座すわっていた。

The newspaper-reading, salaryman-style, middle-aged man sat by himself.

「このドレスは今いま風ふうだと思おもったら大間違おおまちがいだ。」

'If you thought that this dress is in a modern style, then you were greatly mistaken.'

１９じゅうきゅう世紀せいき風ふうの時計とけいを買かってよかった。

I'm glad that I bought this 19-century-style clock.

大学生だいがくせい風ふうの若わかい女性じょせいはタバコを吸すいながら電話でんわで話はなしていた。

The young woman in university-style (appearance) was talking on the phone while smoking a cigarette.

ネットショップの広告こうこく：「ヴィンテージ風ふう家具かぐ、ご覧らんください！」

Online shop's advertisement: 'Vintage-style furniture, please have a look!'
