## About

About ことなの

English

Japanese

When the noun こと (or 事こと in its kanji form) is combined with なの, it conveys an explanatory meaning that is similar to what can be seen in the んです・のです grammar pattern. As with んです, ことなの transforms 'it is (A)', into 'it is that it is (A)', simply emphasizing the point.

ことなの may be seen in several different forms, and is quite often used in conjunction with というのは. In these types of sentences, というのは will present the topic, before ことなの will be used to show the conclusion that the speaker has drawn about that topic.

This expression may be used with any word in its attributive form, nouns followed by の, or な-Adjectives followed by な.

漫画家まんがかというのは漫画まんがを描かく人ひとのことなのだ。

Manga artists are people that draw manga.

付属品ふぞくひんというのはメインの物ものに付属ふぞくしている物ものの事ことなんだ。

Accessories are things that come with the main product.

雨あめが上あがるというのは雨あめが止やむことなのです。

The thing which is 'for rain to rise' means 'to stop raining'.

という may also be used directly before ことなの. However, this is not always the case, and is even more unlikely when it was already utilized in the first half of the sentence.

あの人ひとは超ちょう一流いちりゅう歌手かしゅということなの？

Do you mean that person is the most skilled singer?

輸送ゆそうとは荷物にもつを送おくることなの。

'Freight' means to ship a package.

Caution

As というのは is quite long, it will often be shortened to って or とは in casual speech.

Caution

ことなの is also frequently used when asking questions. This is usually when the speaker has drawn a conclusion about something that has been said, and wants to confirm their understanding.

つまりそこに一人ひとりで行いくと危あぶないということなのか。

You mean that it is dangerous to go there alone?

そんなに怒おこることなのですか。

Is this something you should get so worked up over?

なのか is very frequently heard/seen in drama, novels, manga, and similar types of media, but may be considered rude in daily speech.

## Examples

Examples

Sentence
Translation

ハダウェイ：「恋愛れんあいとはどういう事ことなのだろうか。」

Haddaway: 'Love, what does it mean?'

つまり、その病気びょうきが感染症かんせんしょうではないという事ことなのである。

In other words, it means that the disease is not infectious.

チーターのように走はしるとは足あしが速はやいという事ことなのだ。

'Running like a cheetah' means to be fast.

「ザクシャ・イン・ラブ」とは「愛あいを永遠えいえんに」という事ことなのだ。

'Zawsze in love' means 'forever in love'.

頭あたまが切きれるというのは頭あたまがいい事ことなのだ。

'To be sharp' means ' to be smart'.

「月つきに代かわって、お仕置しおきよ!」というのはどういう事ことなのでしょうか？

What does 'in place of the moon, I will punish you' mean?

「冬ふゆがやってくる」というのはいつでも予想外よそうがいの事態じたいに対たいして準備じゅんびをしておくべき事ことなのだ。

'Winter is coming' means that one always has to be prepared for the unexpected.

干かんばつということは食品しょくひんの価値かちがあがる事ことなのだろう。

The drought means that prices of food will probably rise.

満身創痍まんしんそういというのは傷きずだらけの事ことなのである。

'Manshinsoui (Having wounds all over the body)' means 'to be covered in wounds'.

「笑わらい死じに」とは笑わらうことが原因げんいんとなって死しぬ事ことなのだ。

'Death by laughter' means to die due to laughter.

親愛しんあいなる読者どくしゃ。この文章ぶんしょうを読よむことができるという事ことは日本語にほんごがかなり上手じょうずだという事ことなのだ。

Dear reader, the fact that you are able to read this sentence means that your Japanese is quite good.

幽霊船ゆうれいせんというのは生いきた乗組員のりくみいんのいない船舶せんぱくの事ことなのである。

A ghost ship is (means) a vessel with no living crew.
