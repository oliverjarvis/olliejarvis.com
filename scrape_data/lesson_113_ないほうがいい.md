## About

About ないほうがいい

Like たほうがいい, ないほうがいい is used in Japanese for giving advice. However, this expression suggests that it is better 'not' to do something.

This phrase is constructed by using the negative form of a verb grouped with 方ほう, the particle が, and the い-Adjective, いい.

あそこは危あぶないから、行いかない方ほうがいいよ。

Because that place over there is dangerous, it would be better not to go there.

それは触さわらない方ほうがいい。

It would be better not to mess with that. (触（さわ）る has the nuance of 'mess around with', whereas 触（ふ）れる is closer to simply 'touch')

As 方ほう can be translated closely to 'way', or 'direction', this expression is suggesting that one specific choice is simply the better 'way'.

This expression is quite direct, and can imply that there may be some sort of negative consequence if the (A) verb is done.

虫むしが入はいってくるから、窓まどを開あけない方ほうがいいよ。

Because bugs will come in, it would be better not to open the window. (The negative consequence is that bugs will come inside)

Sentence
Translation

その肉にくを食たべないほうがいい。

It'd be better not to eat that meat.

タバコを吸すわないほうがいい。

It would be better not to smoke cigarettes.

汚よごれないほうがいい。（汚れる）

It would be better not to get dirty.

一人ひとりで行いかないほうがいい。

It would be better not to go alone.

毎日まいにち肉にくを食たべないほうがいい。

It would be better not to eat meat every day.

この靴くつを履はかないほうがいい。

It would be better not to wear these shoes.

風邪かぜを引ひいたので、仕事しごとをしないほうがいい。

Since you caught a cold, it would be better not to (do) work.

雨あめだから、家いえを出でないほうがいい。

Because it is rainy, it would be better not to leave the house.

親おやが子供こどもに：「犬いぬのうんこをつつかないほうがいいよ。」

Parent to child: 'It'd be better not to poke the dog poop.'

その言葉ことばを使つかわないほうがいい。

It would be better not to use those words.

ここを右みぎに曲まがらないほうがいい。

It would be better not to turn right here.

これはお兄にいちゃんが買かったピザだから、食たべないほうがいいよ。

Since this is pizza my brother bought, it'd be better not to eat it.
