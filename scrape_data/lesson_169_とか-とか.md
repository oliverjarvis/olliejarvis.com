## About

About とか～とか

とか is a word that belongs to a unique family of particles in Japanese called listing particles. As the name suggests, these particles are used for listing groups of items that share similarities. とか can be used after verbs, or nouns, to express 'among other things, (A)'.

健康けんこうのために野菜やさいを食たべるとか水みずを飲のむとかをしています。

For my health, I do things like eat vegetables and drink water.

亀かめとかすっぽんとかは美味おいしいんですか。

Are things like turtles and softshell turtles delicious?

When multiple things are listed, the last item in the list can (and often does) have とか omitted.

かぼちゃとかサツマイモとか白菜はくさいとかシイタケが入はいっている鍋なべが好すき。

I like hot pots that include things like pumpkin, sweet potato, cabbage, and shiitake.

Caution

とか is used to list items in a non-exhaustive way. What this means is that the listener will assume that there are other items in the list, apart from what is stated directly. The speaker is just providing a few examples of a (potentially much larger) category.

Fun Fact

As we have seen, とか may be used with verb phrases. This is in contrast to や, a similar grammar point that can only be used for listing nouns.

冷蔵庫れいぞうこの中なかにあるものを食たべるとかしてください。

Eat what's in the refrigerator or something. (Natural Japanese)

冷蔵庫れいぞうこの中なかにあるものを食たべるやしてください。

Eat things in the refrigerator or something. (Unnatural Japanese, as や cannot be used with verbs)

Sentence
Translation

青あおとか水色みずいろが好すき。

I like colors such as blue and light blue. (among others)

明日あしたとか今週末こんしゅうまつとか、暇ひまなら、映画えいがを見みに行いこう。

If you are free tomorrow or, for example this weekend, let's go watch a movie. (among others)

トム・クルーズとかブラッド・ピットとかがかっこいいと思おもう。

I think people like Tom Cruise for example, or Brad Pitt are cool. (among others)

日本にほんとか、韓国かんこくとかに行いってみたいですね。

I want to go to places such as Japan and Korea. (among others)

日本語にほんごで「th」の音おととかはないです。

Japanese doesn't have a 'th' sound (among other things).

私わたしは色々いろいろなフルーツが好すきだけど、苺いちごとか、りんごとかは嫌きらいです。

I like a variety of fruits, but I don't like (fruits) such as strawberries and apples.

お客きゃくさんが来くるのに、掃除そうじとか、料理りょうりとか、何なにもしていない！

Even though we are having guests, I haven't done things such as cleaning or cooking! (among others)

ラーメンの中なかではとんこつラーメンとか、味噌みそラーメンが好すきですね。

Among (types of) ramen, I like Tonkotsu and Miso (among others). (such as)

テストを受うける人ひとは、鉛筆えんぴつとか、消けしゴムとかが必要ひつようでしょう。

People who are going to take a test will need things such as a pencil and an eraser, right? (among others)

まだ家事かじとかしなくてはいけないから今日きょうは遊あそべない。

I still have to do things such as cleaning, so I can't hang out. (among others)

日本語にほんごはひらがなとか、カタカナとか、漢字かんじもあるから、勉強べんきょうをするのが大変たいへんです。

In Japanese, there are things such as hiragana, katakana and kanji that make studying the language tough. (among others)

あの人ひと凄すごいね。人ひとの顔かおを見みるだけで性格せいかくとか分わかるって。

That person is amazing. I heard that she can determine someone's personality (among other things) just by looking at their face.
