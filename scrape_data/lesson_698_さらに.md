## About

About 更に

English

Japanese

更さらに is word that is often used adverbially in Japanese to convey the meaning of 'more and more', 'even more than (A)', or 'again'. This use is an extension of the base meaning that the kanji 更さら has in indicating that something is 'approaching change'. Because of this, the expression used following 更さらに will always be something that is either further along than the initial statement either in regard to time, quantity, or volume. Taking this into consideration, 'in addition to (A)' may also be used as a fairly standard translation.

As さらに is used adverbially, it often appears at the beginning of part (B) of a sentence, after the 'base' information has already been indicated.

電気代でんきだいがさらに高たかくなった。

The electricity bill has become even more expensive.

刺身さしみには刺身さしみ専用せんようの醤油しょうゆをかけると、さらに美味おいしくなります。

Sashimi tastes even better when served with soy sauce specially designed for sashimi.

パソコンにウイルス対策たいさくソフトを入いれたら、パソコンが更さらに遅おそくなった。

When I installed antivirus software on my computer, my computer became even slower.

Fun-fact

The meaning of 更さら as 'approaching change' merely indicates that something has already reached 'fullness', and anything more than that will cause a fundamental change in the state of (A). Due to this, さらに primarily indicates things where (A) is considered 'complete' or 'enough' in and of itself, and what happens in (B) pushes that thing beyond a certain threshold. This may be either positive or negative depending on the sentence.

病院びょういんに行いき始はじめてから彼かれは更さらに元気げんきになった。

Ever since he started going to the hospital, he has gotten even better.

仕事しごとをクビになって更さらに生活せいかつが苦くるしくなった。

My life became even more difficult after being fired.

Taxes got higher again. (more and more)

My salary has gone up even more and that is very helpful. (more and more)

さらに乾燥かんそうがひどくなり、保湿ほしつするのが大変たいへんだ。

The dry air has become even more terrible and moisturizing is difficult. (more and more)

国家こっかからの融資ゆうしでさらにグラフェンの研究けんきゅうを進すすめる。

Financing from the state will push the graphene research further. (more and more)

雪ゆき解どけにつれて川かわや湖みずうみの水みずのかさがさらに増ます一方いっぽうだ。

As the snow thaws, the quantity of water in lakes and rivers keeps on increasing further. (more and more)

スマホを見みる時間じかんがさらに増ふえた。依存いぞん症しょうだな。

The time I spend looking at my smartphone has increased even more. I have become dependent on it, haven't I.

さらに加速かそくがよくなったが、公道こうどうでは役やくに立たちません。

The acceleration has become even better, but that isn't helpful on public roads. (more and more)

The standard will apparently become even more expensive, making it difficult to match. (more and more)

If you can strengthen your body more and more, victory next year won't just be a dream.

さらに行列ぎょうれつが長ながくなり、お店みせの人ひとも整理せいりしきれなくなってしまった。

As the line grew even more, it became impossible for that store's staff to regulate everyone. (more and more)

さらにクーラーがきいてきて、風邪かぜをひきそうだ。

As the air conditioner runs more and more, I appear to be catching a cold.
