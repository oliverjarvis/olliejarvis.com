## About

About 合う

English

Japanese

When following the conjunctive form of another verb, the う - Verb 合あう means to do that action in concert with another person/group. This is often translated as 'to mutually do (A)', or 'to do (A) for one another'. Realistically, it just means 'to do (A) reciprocally', with involvement from both sides being required. In this usage, 合あう is considered to be a suffix.

This construction is often used with expressions like お互たがいに 'mutually', which simply emphasizes the point.

彼女かのじょと将来しょうらいの事ことを話はなし合あう。

I am going to talk with my girlfriend about our future.

子供こどもたちがおもちゃを取とり合あうのを見みてお兄にいちゃんの事ことを思おもい出だした。

The kids were fighting with each other over a toy, and that reminded me of my older brother.

友達ともだちとポケモンカードを見みせ合あう。

My friend and I show each other our Pokemon cards.

Fun Fact

While 合あう is regularly translated as 'to fit', or 'to meet' in English, its meaning is somewhat broader in Japanese. 合あう means to perform an action 'as one', where it is not possible (or would be difficult) to complete the action by oneself. Due to this, it is regularly used to describe ideal situations in which individuals should be doing something together, without thinking about their own personal gain.

こういう時ときこそ、助たすけ合あうのが大切たいせつだよ。

At times like this, it is important to help each other out.

私達わたしたちは夫婦ふうふなんだから、どんなことがあっても協力きょうりょくし合あわないといけないよ。

We are husband and wife, so no matter what happens, we need to cooperate.

Caution

合あう may also be used to in regard to sports/contests. In these cases, it may seem like players are not doing the action 'for' each other, but involvement from both sides is required to make the act possible.

全ぜんチームが賞金しょうきんをかけて競きそい合あう。

Every team competed against each other with the prize money on the line.

Japan is famous for businessmen ceremoniously exchanging business cards with one another.
