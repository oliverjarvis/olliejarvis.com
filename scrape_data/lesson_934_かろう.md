## About

About かろう

かろう is an old-fashioned grammar pattern that stems from the indeterminate form of the い-Adjective, かろ, and the addition of the auxiliary verb う. This transforms what would be seen in modern Japanese as いい + でしょう into よかろう.

The meaning itself is the same as だろう and でしょう in expressing agreement or volition, but tends to only be used in dramas or things like anime these days. To use this grammar pattern, simply replace the terminal い of the adjective with かろう.

ふむふむ...まあ、よかろう。

Hmmm, well, that's probably fine.

寒さむかろう、とりあえず中なかに入はいれ。

Isn't it cold? Come on in.

The combination of ない and かろう is also fairly common, and will conjugate in the same way as above, as ない itself is an い-Adjective.

証拠しょうこがあるわけでもなかろうによくそんなことを言いえるな。

I am surprised that you can say such a thing without any proof.

好すきであろうとなかろうと、君きみはその作業さぎょうをしなくてはいけない。

Whether you like it or not, you must complete that task.

Even within drama and manga, this construction tends to only be used by elderly characters, or characters that have a high level of authority.

Sentence
Translation

That brand-bag is probably expensive.

The dango from that long-established dango shop are probably delicious.

家いえで一人ひとりで留守番るすばんをするのは寂さみしかろう。

Looking after a house by yourself is probably lonely.

このドレスは生地きじがペラペラだから安やすかろう。

Because the fabric of this dress is weak, it's probably cheap.

友達ともだちが多おおい人ひとは、学校がっこうも毎日まいにち楽たのしかろう。

For people that have a lot of friends, even school is probably fun every day.

彼女かのじょはペットを亡なくしたらしい。悲かなしかろう。

It seems that she lost her pet. That would be sad, right?

桜さくらが咲さく季節きせつの日本にほんの景色けしきは美うつくしかろう。

The sights in Japan in the season when the cherry blossoms bloom would be gorgeous, right?

社長しゃちょう：「明日あしたは休やすみたい？ふむ、よかろう。」

President: 'You want to have tomorrow off? Hmm, that's probably fine.'

犬いぬがずっと吠ほえているのなら、うるさかろう。

If a dog is constantly barking, it's probably a pain.

この商品しょうひんは安やすすぎる…。クオリティも悪わるかろう。

This product is way too cheap… The quality is probably bad too.

今日きょうは暑あつかろう。歩あるかないでタクシーを使つかいなさい。

Today will probably be hot. Don't walk, use a taxi please.

新入社員しんにゅうしゃいんにとって、その難むずかしい仕事しごとは難むずかしかろう。

For a new employee, that difficult work is probably tricky.
