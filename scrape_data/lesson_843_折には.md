## About

About 折には

English

Japanese

When the noun 折おり 'fold' or 'pleat' is used adverbially with the case marking particle に, it indicates a time which may be used as an opportunity to do something. It is often translated as 'on the occasion of (A)', or 'when the chance of (A) comes up'. It is an expression that is primarily used as a formal, positive alternative for 時ときに, but there are times that it may indicate something negative.

折おりに may be seen after any structure that could connect to a noun, but will primarily appear after verbs in their standard forms, or nouns followed by の.

こちらの旅館りょかんにいらっしゃる折おりには、事前じぜんの予約よやくをお願ねがいいたします。

On the occasion that you visit our ryokan, please make a reservation in advance.

今度こんどお会あいした折おりに、旅行りょこうへ行いった時ときのお土産みやげをお渡わたしします。

When the chance comes up that we meet again soon, I'll give you the present that I got when I went on a trip.

卒業そつぎょうの折おりに担任たんにんの先生せんせいに手紙てがみを渡わたそうと思おもっている。

On the opportunity of my graduation, I am thinking that I'd like to give a letter to the teacher that was looking after me.

Additionally, 折おりに may occasionally appear as 折おりには, but the additional は is often omitted.

In most cases, 折おりに will be used to indicate things that happen 'from time to time', rather than one off events.

Caution

折おりに appears in several common expressions that each have a slightly different meaning.

折おりに触ふれれて - On the occasion of (A), always (B).
折おりがあれば - If there is the chance of (A), (B).
折おりを見みて - When the conditions are good for (A).
折おりも折おり - At the exact moment of (A).

## Examples

メール、本社ほんしゃの上司じょうしに：「お暇ひまな折おりにぜひ我わが支店してんにお越こしください。」

E-mail to a superior from the head office: 'Please, make sure to come to our branch when you are free.'

手紙てがみ：「新春しんしゅんのお支度したくにお忙いそがしい折おりから、風邪かぜなどひかれぬようご自愛じあい下ください。」

Letter: 'Please take care not to catch a cold at this busy time of preparations for a new year.'

お近ちかくにいらっしゃる折おりには、ぜひうちにご飯はんを食たべに来きてください！

On the chance that you will be close by, you’re certainly welcome to come to my place to eat!

宴会えんかいをする折おりには、ぜひうちの居酒屋いざかやをご利用りようくださいませ！

On the occasion of having a banquet gathering, you’re certainly welcome to make use of our izakaya.

オーストラリアへ来くる折おりには、ぜひカンガルーのお肉にくを食たべてみてください。

At the opportunity of coming to Australia, certainly try eating kangaroo meat.

あの作家さっかさんとお会あいした折おりには、著書ちょしょへの愛あいをしっかりと伝つたえました。

At the opportunity of meeting that author, I properly conveyed my love for his work.

両家りょうけの食事会しょくじかいをする折おりには、ちゃんと自分じぶんで司会しかいをしようと思おもっています。

On the occasion of both of our families having a dinner, I will make sure to properly lead processions myself.

自伝じでん：「初はじめてのパリへの出張しゅっちょうの折おりにはルーヴル美術館びじゅつかんに立たち寄よった。」

Autobiography: 'On the occasion of my first trip to Paris, I stopped by the Louvre museum for a short visit.'

手紙てがみ、名誉めいよあるゲストの再さい招待しょうたい：「北海道ほっかいどうにいらした折おりに、是非ぜひお寄よりください！」

A letter, re-invitation of a reputable guest: 'Please, make sure to drop by when you come to Hokkaido.'

大学だいがくの研究室けんきゅうしつを訪たずねた折おりに、学生がくせい時代じだいにお世話せわになった先生せんせいに挨拶あいさつをしました。

On the opportunity of visiting the university laboratory, I said hello to the teacher who looked after me during my time as a student.

自伝じでん：「京都きょうとへの観光かんこう旅行りょこうの折おりに、駅えきで偶然ぐうぜん小学校しょうがっこうの先生せんせいに会あった。」

Autobiography: 'On the occasion of a sightseeing trip to Kyoto, I came across my elementary school teacher at the train station.'

献辞けんじ：「折おりに触ふれて相談そうだんに乗のって下くださった、敬愛けいあいしてやまない友人ゆうじん、黒木くろき智子ともこさんに心こころから感謝かんしゃいたします。」

Dedication: 'I am grateful from the bottom of my heart to my friend I greatly respect, Tomoko Kuroki, who has helped me wherever the occasion arose. '
