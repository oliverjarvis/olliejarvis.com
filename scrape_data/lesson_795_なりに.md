## About

About なりに

English

Japanese

When used as a noun, なり often presents an example group from which something exhibits traits of, regardless of whether that group is actually a good example of the trait being described. This use can be translated as 'for (A) in the way of (B) to do (C)'. Or more simply, 'to do its best in its own way', or 'to do as much as one can'. This use stems from 形なり, meaning 'shape' or 'pattern', and can be thought of as meaning 'in the pattern of'.

In these sentences, (A) will be a specific example from within a group, (B) will be representative of the whole group, and (C) will be the trait or behavior in question related to (B).

Either of the case marking particles に or の will come after なり, depending on whether なり is linked directly to a noun, or is being used adverbially. なりに and なりの may follow almost any word in its plain form, with the word it follows being the group or quality that is being represented.

自由じゆう時間じかんが増ふえたら、増ふえたなりに有効ゆうこうに使つかいたい。

If I get more free time, I would like to use all that free time as much as I could.

広ひろい部屋へやは広ひろいなりのデメリットがあります。

Large rooms have their own disadvantages.

彼かれは仕事しごとが下手へたなりに、他ほかの社員しゃいんのモチベーションをあげてくれたりしている。

For all of his lack of skill, he does his best to motivate other workers.

自分じぶんなりに頑張がんばってみたが、やっぱりダメだった。

I tried my best in my own way, but it was still not enough.

This use of なり is considered a neologism, which is a pre-existing word that has been adapted to have a new meaning or nuance. When used this way, なり will almost always express that (A) is not doing something adequately or as desired, but is doing the best it can based on the 'pattern' or 'mold' that it comes from.

彼かれは下手へたなりに頑張がんばってラブレターを書かいた。

For all of his lack of skill, he did the best that he could (in his own way) to write a love letter.

こまりちゃんは小ちいさいなりに両親りょうしんの農家のうかを手伝てつだっていた。

For all of her small size, Komari-chan did as much as she could to help on her parent's farm. (as much as one can)

会計士かいけいしは会計士かいけいしなりに頑張がんばって全すべての手段しゅだんを尽つくしましたが、解決策かいけつさくを見みつけられませんでした。

The accountants have done their best to leave no stone unturned, but they could not find a solution. (as much as one can)

「その問とい合あわせに対たいする私わたしなりの回答かいとうをご紹介しょうかいいたします。」

'I would like to present my own answer to that query.'

僕ぼくなりにベストを尽つくしましたが、搬送はんそうされてきた患者かんじゃを救すくうことが出来できませんでした。

I exhausted my efforts as best as I could, but I was unable to save the patient that was transported to the hospital.

「子供こどもはいいね。何なんにも心配しんぱいごとも無ないし...」
「でも、子供こどもには子供こどもなりの苦くるしみがあるんだけど。」

'Children have it nice. No worries at all…'
'But children have worries of their own though.'

才能さいのうが無ないなりに頑張がんばろうと思おもっていたのに、母ははにやる気きをへし折おられてしまった。

Despite thinking that I would do my best as someone with no talent, my enthusiasm was crushed by my mother.

それなりに勉強べんきょうしたところで、学校がっこうの定期ていきテストで上位じょういを狙ねらうことは不ふ可能かのうでしょう。

No matter how much I do my best to study in my own way, aiming for a high rank on the periodic tests is probably not possible.

相撲すもうの監督かんとく：「小柄こがらなりに賢かしこい相撲すもうをとることができていて、僕ぼくは君きみのことを誇ほこりに思おもうよ。」

Sumo coach: 'Being able to compete in your own clever way as a small framed sumo, I really respect you.'

私わたしたち家族かぞくは貧まずしいなりに楽たのしく生活せいかつしてきましたが、そろそろ限界げんかいが来きそうです。

We did our best to have fun lives as a poor family, but it seems like we're about to reach our limit. (as much as one can)

自分じぶんなりによく考かんがえて行動こうどうしたのに、その行動こうどうが裏目うらめに出でてしまうことが度々たびたびあります。

Despite considering and acting in the best way that I can, those actions occasionally backfire.

なつみちゃん：「田舎いなかって本当ほんとうに不便ふべんだよね」
こまりちゃん：「田舎いなかは不便ふべんな時ときもあるけど、それなりに魅力みりょくもあるよ。」

Natsumi: 'The countryside is really inconvenient, right?'
Komari: 'It might be inconvenient sometimes, but it also has its own charm.'
