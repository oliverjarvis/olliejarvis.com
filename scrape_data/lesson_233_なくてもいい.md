## About

About なくてもいい

なくてもいい is simply the opposite of てもいい, and highlights that it is 'ok even if (A) is not done'. This usually comes across as being quite polite, and sounds like 'don't worry about doing (A)' in English.

なくてもいい is a combination of the auxiliary verb ない in its conjunction form, with てもいい.

ミカは悪わるくないから謝あやまらなくてもいいよ。

Mika, you are not in the wrong, so you don't have to apologize.

その手紙てがみはもう届とどけなくてもいい。

It's okay if you don't deliver that letter anymore.

In order to make this phrase sound even more casual, the adverbial particle も may be omitted. This does not change the nuance at all, but will sound a bit more friendly (when used with people that you know well).

今日きょうは会社かいしゃに来こなくていいよ。

It is fine if you don't come to the office today.

You don't have to drink more than this.

何なにもしなくてもいい？

I don't have to do anything?

I heard that it is alright even if you don't do that.

昨日きのう掃除そうじをしたから、今日きょうはしなくてもいいだろう。

Since I cleaned yesterday, I don't have to clean today, right?

日本にほんにはバスと電車でんしゃとタクシーがあるので、車くるまを買かわなくてもいい。

In Japan there are buses, trains and taxis, so you don't have to buy a car.

今日きょう買かい物ものに行いけば、週末しゅうまつは行いかなくてもいい。

If I can go shopping today, then I don't have to go on the weekend.

全部ぜんぶの漢字かんじを覚おぼえなくてもいいよ。２０００にせん字じ覚おぼえたら新聞しんぶんを読よめるよ。

It is okay even if you don't remember all of the kanji. If you remember 2000 characters, you can read the newspaper.

明日あした試合しあいがあるでしょう？練習れんしゅうをしなくてもいいの？

You have a match tomorrow, right? Is it alright even if you don't practice?
