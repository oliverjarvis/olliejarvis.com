## About

About さすが

English

Japanese

さすが, also often appearing in the kanji form 流石さすが, is an adverb that indicates the speaker's feeling of being impressed that something has lived up to its reputation, or what was expected of it. In English, this comes across as 'as expected of (A)', or 'that is just like (A)'.

As 流石さすが is an adverb, it may appear at the beginning of phrases (either with or without に), or directly preceded a noun when の is used.

すごい！さすが先輩せんぱい！

Wow! That's what I would expect from you senpai!

流石さすがに今日きょうは雨あめが降ふりすぎだろ...

As one would expect, it is raining way too much today.

さすがの君きみでもこの問題もんだいは難むずかしいだろう。

Even for someone like you, this problem is difficult, isn't it?

Caution

Although uncommon, さすが may occasionally have a negative connotation. In these cases, it is similar to 'even' in English. This use of さすが implies that 'as one had thought, even (A) couldn't (B)', and similar nuances.

さすが田中たなかくん！発注はっちゅうミスの天才てんさいだね！

As expected, Tanaka-kun! You are a genius at making order errors!

Fun-fact

The kanji structure of 流石さすが is a type of kanji use known as 借字しゃくじ in Japanese. This basically means 'borrowed symbol', and indicates that the meaning of the kanji is not related to the meaning of the word at all.

いいカーブだ。流石さすがダルビッシュ。

What a nice curveball. That's just like Darvish.

流石さすがに食くいすぎたな。椅子いすから立たてない。

As expected, I ate too much. I can't get up from a chair.

こんなおめでたい席せきに、流石さすがにあの格好かっこうはない。

As one would expect, that is not suitable for this highly auspicious seat.

一流いちりゅうのオーケストラの演奏えんそうは流石さすがですね。

The performance was as would be expected from a first rate orchestra.

流石さすがに落おとし物ものが多おおすぎる。注意ちゅういしないと。

As one would expect, you lose too many things. You need to be careful.

指圧しあつのしすぎで、流石さすがに親指おやゆびが痛いたい。

I gave too many massages and as would be expected, my thumbs hurt.

涙なみだが出でるほど夕焼ゆうやけが綺麗きれいだ。流石さすが神様かみさまだ。

The sunset was beautiful enough to elicit tears. As one would expect from the gods.

流石さすがの君きみでも、あれだけ食たべればカロリー取とり過すぎです。

Even as would be expected from you, consuming that many calories is too much.

会話かいわが漫才まんざいのようだ。流石さすが関西かんさい人じん。

The conversation was like talking with a comedian. That's just like a person from Kansai.

流石さすがの神戸牛こうべぎゅうだ。世界一せかいいち脂あぶらの乗のった肉にくだ。

As one would expect from Kobe beef, it has the best marbling in the world.

流石さすがの解説者かいせつしゃも、今日きょうの試合しあいの結末けつまつは予想よそうできなかった。

Even (an established) commentator couldn't have predicted the ending of today's match.

流石さすがに空手からての稽古けいこを積つんできただけに、１５じゅうごｋｇキロのダンベルは持もてる。

As might be expected from a person who trains karate, he can lift 15kg dumbbells.
