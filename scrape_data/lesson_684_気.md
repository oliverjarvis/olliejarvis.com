## About

About 気

English

Japanese

When used after verbs, 気き often indicates either the will or desire to do that particular action. This use of 気き is often interpreted as 'to feel like doing (A)' in English. More literally, 気き may be translated as 'mind', as is similar to the way phrases like 'to have half a mind to (A)' in English, indicating that something is either positively or negatively drawing the speaker's attention toward doing (or avoiding) the action of (A).

お風呂ふろでゆっくりしていたら、勉強べんきょうをする気きがなくなった。

I lost my motivation (spirit) to study, when I was relaxing in the bath.

よーし、やる気きが出でてきたぞ！

Alright! I am starting to get motivated!

やばい、明日あしたまでに終おわる気きがしない。

Shoot, I don't feel like I can finish this by tomorrow.

As 気き itself is a noun expressing the 'mind' or 'intention' to do (A), it may be followed by a variety of different verbs indicating an array of slightly unique nuances. Common patterns are 気きがある 'to have the feeling of (A)', 気きがない 'to not have the feeling of (A)', 気きがする 'to feel like (A)', 気きがしない 'to not feel like (A)', 気きが出でる 'for the feeling of (A) to come about', and 気きが出でない 'for the feeling of (A) to not come about'.

Sentence
Translation

誰だれかに見みられている気きがする。

I feel like I am being watched by someone.

何なにもやる気きが起おきない。

I can't muster any motivation (spirit). (Feel like doing)

応援おうえんされて、やる気きが出でた。

I was cheered on and found motivation (spirit). (Feel like doing)

娘むすめは好すき嫌きらいが多おおすぎる気きがする。

I feel like my daughter is too picky.

彼かれの送別そうべつ会かいには行いく気きがしない。

I don't feel like going to his farewell party.

僕ぼくの実力じつりょくでは勝負しょうぶにならない気きがする。

I feel like it wouldn't be a contest with my real strength.

結局けっきょく賞金しょうきんはもらえない気きがします。

I feel like I won't receive the prize money after all.

相手あいてチームは攻せめる気きがないらしい。

It seems like the opposing team doesn't feel like attacking. (lack the spirit to attack)

確たしか彼かれは、醤油しょうゆが苦手にがてだった気きがします。

If I remember correctly I feel like he disliked soy sauce.

あの助教授じょきょうじゅは本当ほんとうに教おしえる気きがあるのだろうか？

I wonder if that assistant professor really feels like teaching. (has the spirit)

信しんじる気きがない人ひとには、何なにを言いっても無駄むだです。

Anything you say to someone who doesn't feel like believing it, is a waste. (spirit)

営業えいぎょうマンなのに、全然ぜんぜん宣伝せんでんする気きがない。

Despite being a salesman, he lacks the spirit to advertise at all.
