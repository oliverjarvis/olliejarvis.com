## About

About あり

English

Japanese

あり itself is the literary form of the う-Verb 有ある 'to be', and is used in a similar way to なし. However, rather than meaning that something is 'without (A)', it shows that something is 'with (A), amongst other things'. This 'amongst other things' is important, as あり will primarily be used when something is being presented as a single possibility among many possibilities.

ラーメンもありじゃない？

Ramen is possible, isn't it?

駐車場ちゅうしゃじょうありのホテルを取とっておいてください。

Please book a hotel with a parking lot.

字幕じまく有ありで見みたいから字幕じまくつけてもらえない？

I want to watch it with subtitles, so could you please turn it on?

あり can be and often is used simply as a reply to a statement to indicate agreement, or that the listener thinks that what the speaker is saying is possible.

鈴木すずき先輩せんぱい：「来週らいしゅうＢＢＱバーベキューも有ありじゃない？」
浜崎はまざきくん：「めっちゃありです。」

Suzuki senpai: 'Don't you think a BBQ would be a good option next week?'
Hamazaki-kun: 'That is totally possible.'

Caution

Occasionally, the kanji 在ある may sometimes also be used. Although the difference between these two kanji is slight, 有あり indicates 'inclusiveness as part of a whole', while 在あり focuses more on 'stand-alone existence'.

Let's go to a restaurant with a kids menu.

This scent is also an option, but I like that scent better!

Alcohol is a possibility, but I think I'll drink juice today.

この飲食店いんしょくてんはキッズスペースありだって！珍めずらしいね！

I heard that this is a restaurant with a kids' space! Pretty rare, huh?

あの白しろいドレスもありだし、ピンクのドレスも君きみに似合にあうよ。

That white dress is a possibility, and the pink dress also suits you.

Kids are not able to see movies with violent scenes.
