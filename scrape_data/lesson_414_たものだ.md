## About

About たものだ

English

Japanese

As mentioned in the ものだ lesson, the combination of the noun 物もの 'thing', with the auxiliary verb だ forms a strong determination about whatever comes before it as being 'the way it is'. As an extension of this nuance, ものだ can be paired with verbs in the past tense, creating a phrase similar to 'I used to (A) in the past'. However, the literal meaning is closer to '(A) was the way it used to be'.

子供こどもの頃ころはよく先生せんせいに怒おこられたものだ。

I used to get yelled at by the teacher often when I was still a kid.

昔むかしはよく親父おやじと釣つりに行いったものだ。

I often used to go fishing with my father in the past.

若わかい頃ころは色々いろいろと苦労くろうしたものだ。

When I was young, I used to suffer a lot.

Due to ものだ being a strong determination of something's existence/truth, this expression quite often carries the nuance that the speaker is nostalgic about (A), reminiscing about (A), or being introspective about (A). This means that it will not sound natural when describing things that are seemingly inconsequential, or have no meaningful significance.

小学生しょうがくせいの頃ころは毎日まいにち学校がっこうに行いったものだ。

I used to go to school everyday when I was an elementary student. (Unnatural, as this is a normal event)

昔むかしは毎日まいにちのように親父おやじとけんかをしたものだ。

In the past I fought with my father everyday. (Natural, as this is a meaningful event)

When highlighting things in the past that are not particularly important, たことがある will be used instead.

多分たぶん子供こどもの頃ころに食たべたことがある。

I may have eaten this when I was a kid. (Unimportant)

子供こどもの頃ころはよく、神社じんじゃで遊あそんだ事ことがある。

When I was a kid, I played at the shrine a lot. (Potentially important, but not in this speaker's opinion)

I used to have lots of hardships when I was young.

子供こどもの頃ころはよく海うみに泳およぎに行いったものだ。

I used to go swimming in the ocean a lot when I was a child.

昔むかしはよく、車くるまを運転うんてんして遠とおくに出でかけたものだ。

A long time ago, I often used to go out and take long drives.

子供こどもの頃ころは授業じゅぎょう中ちゅう、よく居い眠ねむりをしたものだ。

When I was a child, I used to nod off a lot in the middle of class.

私わたしが子こどもの頃ころ、よく父ちちに映画えいがを見みに連つれていってもらったものだ。

Dad used to take me to the movies when I was a child.

小学生しょうがくせいの頃ころは雪ゆきが降ふるたびに、必かならず雪ゆきだるまを作つくったものだ。

I used to make a snowman every time it snowed when I was in elementary school.

子供こどものころ、父親ちちおやと様々さまざまな教科書きょうかしょを読よみながら田舎いなかを歩あるいたものだ。

As a child, I used to walk through the countryside while reading various textbooks with my father.

昔むかしは井戸いどから水みずをくんだものだが、今いまは水道すいどうを出だすだけでいい。

People used to take their water from wells, but now you can just get it (directly) from the water supply.

昔むかし、母親ははおやは寝ねる前まえにはほとんど毎晩まいばん絵本えほんを読よんでくれたものだ。

Long ago, my mom used to read me picture books almost every night before going to bed.

夏休なつやすみは友達ともだちのクリスと彼かれの別荘べっそうに行いっていろいろ遊あそんだものだ。

During summer vacation I used to go with my friend Chris to his summer house and would play a lot.

今いまはこの場所ばしょはガソリンスタンドになっているけど、昔むかしはここには図書館としょかんがあってよくそこで本ほんを読よんだものだ。

This place is a gas station now, but in the past there was a library here and I often used to read there.

この庭にわには１５じゅうご年ねん前まえまでは桜さくらがあって、昔むかしは家族かぞくで花見はなみをしたものだ。

A cherry tree was in this garden until 15 years ago, and in the past I used to look at it with my family.
