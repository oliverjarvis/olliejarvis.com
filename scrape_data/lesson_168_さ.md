## About

About さ

さ is a suffix in Japanese that can be attached to the stem form of い-Adjectives and な-Adjectives. When this happens, it transforms the target adjective into a noun that illustrates the 'amount' of that particular adjective. This commonly translates as '-ness' in English (sweetness, tallness, spiciness, etc), but is referred to as degree in Japanese.

私わたしが道みちの長ながさを測はかります。

I will measure the length of the road. (The road's lengthiness)

大人おとなになってから家族かぞくの大切たいせつさが分わかってきた。

After becoming an adult, I came to understand the importance of family. (Family's importantness)

Fun Fact

It is thought that this suffix originally stemmed from the kanji 差さ (meaning 'distinction'), and was used for highlighting things that were distinctive features of something. In this way, さ is also sometimes used after the dictionary form of verbs, to highlight a distinct point in time that something happens.

学校がっこうからの帰かえるさにお金かねを拾ひろった。

On the way back from school, I picked up some money. (At the 'point' of returning home)

行いくさで家いえに忘わすれ物ものをしたことを思おもい出だした。

When I was on my way there, I remembered that I had left something at home. (At the 'point' of going somewhere)

However, this use of the suffix is far less common in modern Japanese, and is known as 'poetic language'. It was primarily used during Japan's 'middle ages', which spanned from (around) 1100, until close to the end of the 1500's.

This beauty is a first for me.

This sweetness is delicious.

Please weigh the heaviness of the box for me.

Just by this coldness you will die.

琵琶湖びわこの深ふかさって知しっている？

Do you know the depth of Lake Biwa?

日本にほんの素晴すばらしさを体験たいけんしたいよ。

I want to experience Japan's greatness.

何なにこの汚きたなさ！早はやく掃除そうじをしてください。

What is this filthiness! Please hurry up and clean it.

この景色けしきの綺麗きれいさは言葉ことばでは説明せつめいできない。

I am unable to explain the prettiness of this scenery with words.

食たべたことのない甘あまさのクッキーを作つくりたい。

I want to make a cookie that has a sweetness that I have never tasted before.

昨日きのう食たべたラーメンの美味おいしさを友達ともだちに教おしえたい。

I want my friend to know the deliciousness of the ramen I ate yesterday.

彼かれがやったことの酷ひどさは永遠えいえんに忘わすれない。

I will never forget the harshness of what he did.
