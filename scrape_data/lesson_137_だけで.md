## About

About だけで

だけで is a combination of the adverbial particle だけ, and the case marking particleで. This expression is regularly thought of as meaning 'just by (A), (B)'. However, the literal translation is a bit closer to 'with only (A), (B)'. This reflects the で particle's standard usage of showing a 'tool' that is being used to achieve a certain result.

To use だけで, simply attach it to the end of any (non ます) form of verb, or noun.

彼女かのじょと話はなすだけで楽たのしい。

Just talking with my girlfriend is fun. (We don't need to do anything else, because talking is fun enough)

これは電子でんしレンジだけでオーブンの機能きのうはついていない。

This is just a microwave, an oven function is not included.

Fun Fact

だけ differs from しか in one very important way. だけ highlights that there is/was more than one option, but that only one thing was chosen/used. However, しか implies that there is only one possible option to begin with.

Sentence
Translation

パーティーに行いくだけで楽たのしい。

Just (by) going to a party is fun.

顔かおを見みただけで、彼かれはいい人ひとだと分わかった。

I could tell he was a good person just by his face.

一緒いっしょに時間じかんを過すごすだけで、仲良なかよくなりました。

We became good friends just by spending time together.

彼かれを見みただけで性格せいかくが悪わるいと分わかった。

Just by looking at him I determined that he had a bad personality.

家族かぞくと一緒いっしょに居いるだけで、幸しあわせ。

Just by being with my family I am happy.

空港くうこうと聞きくだけで、飛行機ひこうきに乗のるのが怖こわくなる。

Just by hearing the word 'airport' I get scared of flying.

彼かれと話はなしをするだけで、疲つかれますよ。

Just by talking to him I get tired.

そういうことを言いってくれるだけで、君きみは優やさしいと分わかる。

Just by you saying that, I know that you are nice.

外国人がいこくじんだけど、着物きものを着きるだけで、日本人にほんじんみたいになった。

I am a foreigner, but just by wearing a kimono I look Japanese.

階段かいだんを上のぼるだけで、しんどくなります。

Just by going up some stairs I become exhausted.

行いってもいいって彼かれが言いってくれたが、言いい方かただけでダメだと分わかった。

He said that it was okay to go, but just by the tone of his voice I knew it wasn't okay.

辛からいものを食たべるだけで、お腹なかが痛いたくなる。

Just by eating something spicy my stomach starts to hurt.
