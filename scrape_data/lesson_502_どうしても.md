## About

About どうしても

English

Japanese

どうしても is a construction in Japanese that is often considered to be a stand-alone adverb meaning 'no matter how', or 'regardless of anything'. Originally, it is a combination of the adverb どう 'how', the て-form of する, and the adverbial particle も, meaning 'to even do'.

どうしても will regularly be used at the beginning of sentences before conveying something that is considered by the speaker to be 'absolutely true'.

どうしても欲ほしいのなら、自分じぶんで買かいなさい。

If you want it no matter what, buy it yourself.

どうしても我慢がまんができなくて買かっちゃった。

I accidentally bought it because I couldn't resist no matter what.

Alternatively, どうしても may be used after verbs, following the conjunction particle と. This will express that 'if (A) happens, (B) is unavoidable'.

牛乳ぎゅうにゅう飲のむとどうしてもお腹なかが痛いたくなるんです。

Regardless of anything, If I drink milk, I get a stomach ache.

寝ねる前まえにお腹なかいっぱいに食たべるとどうしても寝ねれなくなっちゃうんだよね。

When I eat till I'm full, before I sleep, I am unable to sleep no matter what.

Fun Fact

As the closest literal meaning of どう in English is 'any', どうしても also translates quite accurately as 'in any case'.

どうしてもヘリコプターには乗のりたくない。

In any case, I don't want to ride a helicopter.
