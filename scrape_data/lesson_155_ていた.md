## About

About ていた

To convey that '(A) was happening', or that somebody 'was doing (A)' in Japanese, ていた (or ていました) will be used. ていた is a construction which uses the conjunction particle て, and the ichidan verb いる (to exist), in its past tense form いた.

シャワーが壊こわれていたから二日ふつか間かんもシャワーを浴あびれなかった。

Because the shower was broken, I was not able to take a shower for 2 days. (Was in the state of being broken)

昨日きのうは夜遅よるおそくまでカラオケで歌うたっていたので、のどが痛いたいです。

I was singing until late last night at karaoke, so my throat hurts. (Was doing karaoke)

昔むかしはパーマをかけていました。

In the past, I used to have my hair permed. (Was getting perms)

窓まどが割われていました。

The window was smashed. (Was in the state of being smashed)

Sometimes ていた is used to express things that are still ongoing, but the speaker is not sure about, or would like to politely advise the listener about. This is very similar to the way in which English uses '(A) was doing (B)'.

お父とうさんがその椅子いすに座すわっていたから、こっちに座すわりな。

Dad was sitting there, so sit here please.

In this example, although the action of 'sitting' is not finished, we can imagine that the speaker is suggesting that 'dad was sitting here, and he still is, but he just went to get a drink'.

The same kind of sentence could be used when the speaker has walked away from where their dad was sitting, and doesn't actually know if he is still sitting there or not.

I was studying but am taking a break right now.

音楽おんがくを聴きいていたから、電話でんわが聞きこえなかった。

Because I was listening to music I couldn't hear the phone.

昨日きのう１いち時じごろに寝ねていた。

Yesterday around one o'clock I was sleeping.

４よ時間じかんも椅子いすに座すわっていたから、お尻しりが痛いたい。

I was sitting for as much as four hours, so my butt hurts.

お母かあさんが帰かえって来きた時とき私わたしはご飯はんを作つくっていた。

When my mom came home I was making food.

あっ、ごめん。腰こしが痛いたいって忘わすれていた。

Oh, sorry. I was forgetting that your lower back hurt.

長ながい間あいだランニングをしていたから、喉のどが乾かわきました。

Because I was running for a long time, I became thirsty.

テレビを見みていた時とき停電ていでんになった。

When I was watching TV the power went out.

日本にほんで日本語にほんごがだんだん上手じょうずになっていたのに、アメリカに帰かえってきて凄すごく下手へたになった。

In Japan my Japanese was getting better, but now that I have come back to America my Japanese ability has gone way down.

一生懸命いっしょうけんめい走はしっていたから、疲つかれた。

I was running my hardest, so I became exhausted.

また僕ぼくが勉強べんきょうしていた時とき邪魔じゃましたね。やめてくれる？

You bothered me again when I was studying, yeah? Would you cut it out?

神田かんださんは増田ますださんより練習れんしゅうをしていたから、神田かんださんの方ほうがうまいです。

Kanda-san was practicing more than Masuda-san, so Kanda-san is more skilled.
