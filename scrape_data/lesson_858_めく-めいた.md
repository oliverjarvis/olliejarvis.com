## About

About めく・めいた

English

Japanese

めく is a fairly unique う-Verb in Japanese in that it will only be used as a suffix. This is due to its meaning of 'to show signs of (A)', or 'to have the appearance of (A)'. It will be attached to nouns that the speaker experiences to be the case.

As めく itself is a verb, it may appear in several different conjugation forms depending on how it is describing the rest of the sentence. When connected directly to a second noun, the past-tense form めいた is most common.

昨日きのうまでは嘘うそみたいに暑あつかったのに、やっと春はるめいてきた！

It has been hot until yesterday, but it's finally starting to become spring-like!

冗談じょうだんめいて言いったが、多分たぶん冗談じょうだんだったということは気きづいていないと思おもう。

I said it like a joke, but I don't think they understood it as a joke.

皮肉ひにくめいたことを言いってしまって、相手あいてが黙だまり込こんでしまった。

I said something which sounded like sarcasm and the other person fell silent.

彼女かのじょはなんか謎なぞめいた雰囲気ふんいきがあるよね。

She has a somewhat mysterious vibe, doesn't she?

This 'that the speaker experiences to be the case' part is important, as めく will almost always highlight the speaker's own personal feelings in regard to (A), and how they perceive it. The thing being described as (A) will usually not actually be (A), and the speaker is often aware of this fact.

Caution

めく is mostly used with set nouns, so it is best to only use it with expressions that you have already heard. Some examples are 謎なぞめく 'to appear to be a riddle', 皮肉ひにくめく 'to appear to be sarcastic', 言いい訳わけめく 'to appear to be an excuse', 作つくり物ものめく 'to appear to be fictitious', and seasons of the year.

It seems a threatening sounding email was delivered to the city office.

桜さくらも咲さいてすっかり春はるめいてきました。

The cherry blossoms are blooming, and it starting to thoroughly show signs of being spring.

作つくり物ものめいて見みえるほど、平穏へいおんで美うつくしい景色けしきだった。

It was calm and beautiful scenery, to the point of looking man-made.

色いろめいた彼女かのじょの表情ひょうじょうに、思おもわずドキッとしました。

My heart skipped a beat when her expression showed signs of being amorous.

芝居しばいめいた彼かれのプレゼンには、あまり魅力みりょくを感かんじません。

I don’t feel any allure toward his presentation that feels almost like an act.

母ははが「宿題しゅくだいしなさい」と言いうときは、決きまって説教せっきょうめいた口調くちょうになる。

Whenever my mom tells me to do my homework, she always uses a lecture-like tone.

小説しょうせつ：「いつのまにか冬ふゆは去さり、かなり春はるめいていた。」

Novel: 'Before I knew it, it had already gotten quite spring-like.'

小説しょうせつ：「ともこの皮肉ひにくめいて聞きこえた言葉ことばに、ともきが黙だまり込こんだ。」

Novel: 'Tomoki fell silent on the words of Tomoko, which sounded like irony.'

彼女かのじょの気分きぶんを上あげるため、何なにか冗談じょうだんめいた、とるに足たりない話はなしをしようとした。

In order to improve her mood, I tried to tell a joke-like, non-serious story.

会社かいしゃの後輩こうはいから言いい訳わけめいた報告ほうこくを長々ながながと聞きかされる、金曜日きんようびの午後ごご６ろく時じ。

It's 6 p.m. on a Friday, and I'm being made to listen to a drawn-out report from a junior colleague that sounds like an excuse.

小説しょうせつ：「その知しらせを聞きいて彼女かのじょの顔かおには謎なぞめいた微笑びしょうが浮うかんでいた。」

Novel: 'Hearing the news, a somewhat mysterious smile appeared on her face.'

手紙てがみの挨拶あいさつ：「九月くがつになると空そらも風かぜもだいぶ秋あきめいてまいりました。お変かわりありませんか。」

Greetings in letter：In September, the sky and wind start to get quite autumn-like. How have you been?
