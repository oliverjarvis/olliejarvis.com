## About

About 決して〜ない

English

Japanese

決けっして by itself is the て form of the verb 決けっする 'to determine'. However 決けっして is special in that it is considered to be a stand-alone adverb. As an adverb, it carries the meaning of 'decidedly', or 'assuredly', and is used in sentences that contain ない, in order to convey 'never (A)', 'under no circumstances (A)' or 'by no means (A)'.

Like many other adverbs in Japanese, 決けっして can appear at the beginning of a sentence, or directly before the word it is modifying (which will be in its ない form).

この道みちはものすごく危あぶないので、決けっしてグループから離はなれないで下ください。

This road is very dangerous, so please under no circumstances leave the group.

決けっして痛いたくないとは言いえないです。

By no means, can I say that it does not hurt.

この乗のり物ものは決けっして危険きけんではないです。

This ride is by no means dangerous.

この子こは決けっして悪わるい人ひとではないけど、時々ときどき失礼しつれいなときがある。

By no means do I think that this person is bad, but they are rude at times.

While the translation is 'never', the literal meaning of 決けっして is 'decidedly not (A)', or 'assuredly not (A)', simply emphasizing the negative point within a sentence.

Caution

決けっして may also sometimes be paired with other structures that are not specifically negative, the primary example of this being ものか (a grammar point that we will learn about later). ものか strongly confirms a statement as being something that does not exist/will not be done.

この宝石ほうせきを決けっして渡わたすものか！

There is no way I will hand over this treasure!

お前まえに決けっしてお金かねを貸かすものか！

There is no way I will lend you money!
