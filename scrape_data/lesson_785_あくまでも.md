## About

About あくまでも

English

Japanese

飽あく is the old-fashioned and somewhat poetic version of 飽あきる the る-Verb meaning 'to tire of (A)', or 'to get sick of (A)'. When used in conjunction with the adverbial particles まで 'until', and も 'even', 飽あくまでも will imply that (A) is being done 'persistently', 'stubbornly', or 'to the bitter end'. The more literal translation of 'to continue (A) even to the point of getting tired of it' fits these interpretations quite well.

飽あくまでも will often come at the beginning of sentences before going on to describe that thing that is being done persistently.

容疑者ようぎしゃはあくまでも自分じぶんがしたことを認みとめようとしませんでした。

The suspect stubbornly refused to admit what he had done.

私わたしたちはあくまでもあなたのお子こさんを見みつけるつもりです！

We intend to look for your child until we can find them!

彼女かのじょはあくまでも子供こどもたちには秘密ひみつにしておこうと思おもっていたが、これ以上いじょう子供こどもたちには嘘うそをついていられないと思おもい、子供こどもたちに全すべてを話はなした。

She thought that she would have kept it a secret from her kids till the bitter end, but she couldn't lie to her children any longer, so she told them everything.

も is not required, and 飽あくまで may sometimes be seen without it. However, as the phrase is quite strong, it is far more common for も to be included to highlight the emphasis.

犯人はんにんのことなら私わたしにお任まかせください！あくまで彼かれを捕つかまえるまで探さがし続つづけるつもりです！

Leave it up to me to find the culprit! I intend to look for them until I catch them!

Due to the persistence that the verb 飽あく itself can highlight, it will often be seen together in sentences that include words like つもり 'intention', to show that someone intends to do something regardless of the consequences.

Despite 飽あくまで being a combination of 飽あく and まで, it will appear in most dictionaries as a standalone adverb in modern Japanese.

## Examples

Sentence
Translation

飽あくまでも、不正ふせいに立たち向むかおうとします。

I will fight against injustice to the end.

飽あくまでもやると言いうなら、私わたしは止とめません。

If you (stubbornly) insist on doing this, I won't stop you. (to the end)

困難こんなんに対たいしてあくまでも抵抗ていこうした。

He persistently opposed all difficulties.

被告ひこくは飽あくまでも無罪むざいを主張しゅちょうするつもりらしい。

I have heard that the defendant insists on claiming his innocence until the bitter end.

彼女かのじょは政治的圧力せいじてきあつりょくにも関かかわらずその新税しんぜいに飽あくまで反対はんたいした。

Despite political pressure, she opposed the new tax to the bitter end.

僕ぼくは正義せいぎを守まもる警察官けいさつかんであり、拳銃けんじゅうを使つかうのはあくまでも身みの危険きけんを感かんじた時ときだけだ。

In addition to being an officer that upholds righteousness, I will stubbornly only use a pistol if I feel in danger of bodily harm.

父ちちは不倫ふりんをした母ははと結婚けっこん生活せいかつを続つづけているが、あくまでも私わたしが成人せいじんするまでだそうです。

My father is persistently continuing daily life with mom, who cheated on him, but I heard that it is only until I become an adult. (to the end)

そのスパイはあくまでも秘密ひみつを守まもるつもりだったが、家族かぞくのために秘密ひみつを漏もらした。

That spy intended to keep his secrets til the bitter end, but he leaked them for the sake of his family.

確たしかに被害者ひがいしゃにも非ひはあるが、あくまでも虐いじめの問題もんだいで悪わるいのは加害者かがいしゃの方ほうである。

While it is certainly true that the victim is also at fault, on the question of persistently tormenting someone, it is more the aggressor who is the one to blame.

イラ次長じちょうは飽あくまでも意見いけんを変かえませんでした。結果けっか、このプロジェクトを葬ほうむらざるを得えませんでした。

Vice Director Ira stubbornly didn't change her decision. As a result, we had no choice but to shelve the project. (to the end)

私わたしは周まわりにどんなに可能性かのうせいが低ひくいと諭さとされても、あくまでも彼かれが見みつかるまで探さがし続つづけるつもりです。

No matter how much I am hassled by those in my surroundings about the low possibility, I intend to persistently keep searching for him until he is found.

警官けいかんとの話はなし：「証拠しょうこがあるのに、飽あくまで自分じぶんは何なにも知しらないと彼かれはとぼけた主張しゅちょうを繰くり返かえしている。」

Chat between policemen: 'Even though we have evidence, he repeatedly and childishly insists to the bitter end that he doesn't know anything.'
