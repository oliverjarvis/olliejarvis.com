## About

About ようだ

In a similar way to みたい, ようだ is used when making observations about something in Japanese. This auxiliary verb is used to express three primary things.

たとえ - Using (A) as an example to highlight the way that (B) looks/is acting.

推定すいてい - Using (A) as a reason for an assumption made about (B).

例示れいじ - Presenting (A) as an example of what (B) is.

ようだ is usually based on direct information/experience, and shows that the speaker has high confidence in what they are saying. It may also frequently appear in its kanji form, 様ようだ.

ようだ can be used with any verb, い-Adjective, な-Adjective, or noun. However, it requires な before it when used with な-Adjectives, or の when used with nouns.

ここに大おおきなビルが出来できるようだ。

It seems like a big building will be made here.

あそこのプールは深ふかい様ようだ。

It seems like the pool over there is deep.

彼かれは明日あしたのテストに自信じしんがあると皆みんなに言いっているが、本当ほんとうは不安ふあんなようだ。

He is telling everyone that he is confident about the test tomorrow, but it seems like he is actually worried.

ヨーダは宇宙人うちゅうじんのようだ。

Yoda appears to be an alien.

Although ようだ and そうだ are often used in similar situations, そうだ shows that the speaker has a lot less confidence in their statement than ようだ. Because of this, ようだ is a much better choice when stating things that are obvious/observable by anyone.

Caution

ようだ is considerably more formal than みたい, and is therefore less common in casual conversation.

Sentence
Translation

ほとんど無なくなっているようだ。

It seems that most of it is gone.

彼かれはここに来こないようだ。

It seems like he won't come here.

今日きょうの天気てんきは台風たいふうのようだ。

Today's weather looks like a typhoon.

彼かれの話はなし方かたは歌うたっているようだ。

His way of speaking seems like singing.

あの人ひとはお金かねを持もっていないようだが、本当ほんとうはお金かね持もち。

That person seems like he doesn't have any money, but he really is rich.

このコーラは苦にがい。まるでコーヒーのようだ。

This cola is bitter. It tastes like coffee.

彼かれはいつも変へんな事こと言いっているけど、本当ほんとうは真面目まじめなようだ。

He is always saying strange things, but it appears that he is really a serious person.

私わたしは誕生日たんじょうびにグーグルピクセルが欲ほしいけど、もらえないようだ。

I want a Google Pixel for my birthday, but it appears that I won't get one.

彼女かのじょは話はなしにくいようだが、話はなしやすい。

She seems to be difficult to talk to, but she is actually quite friendly.

すみません！私わたしの荷物にもつを勘違かんちがいして、持もって行いったようだ。

Excuse me! It appears that you have made a mistake and taken my luggage.

あの選手せんしゅはこの選手せんしゅより速はやく走はしれるようだ。

It looks like that athlete can run faster than this athlete.

子供こどもは先週せんしゅうもらったゲームにもう飽あきたようだ。

It appears that the child has already grown tired of the game he received last week.
