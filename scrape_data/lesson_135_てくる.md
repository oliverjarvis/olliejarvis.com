## About

About てくる

This grammar point is a combination of the conjunction particle て, and the verb 来くる 'to come'. To use this grammar point, you will need to attach て to any verb, and then follow it with くる. This nuance is usually that '(A) happened, and then came', or 'will happen, and then come'. In other words, it will depend on whether くる is in present, or past tense.

今日きょうも宿題しゅくだいを持もってくるのを忘わすれた。

I forgot to bring my homework today too. (To 'come with' homework was forgotten)

パパ〜、じゃが芋いもを買かってきたよ！

Dad, I bought the potatoes. (Bought and 'came with')

てくる can be used when representing time, or when representing a physical place. When representing a physical place, it is more likely that the kanji structure, 来くる will be used. However, this is not a set rule, and depends on the writer.

When used in relation to time, the nuance is usually that something has already come to pass, or will start to happen from now.

最近さいきん、日本語にほんごが上手うまくなってきた感かんじがする。

Recently I feel like I have come to be better at Japanese. (Very similar to 'gotten' in English)

This is the opposite of ていく, which indicates that something will change from the way that it is now, or will continue progressing in a certain way.

Fun Fact

When compared to English, てくる is very similar to the phrase 'to have come to (A)', in relation to time. This means that it focuses on the fact that the speaker will 'become (A)', should things continue in a specific way. In practice though, it is used like 'gotten' in most situations.

## Examples

Examples

00:02

Sentence
Translation

すぐ帰かえってくるね？

You will come back home soon, right? (Literally - Return and come)

お金かねがなくなってきた。

I have started to lose money. (has become)

トイレに行いってきます。

I will go to the bathroom and come back.

お母かあさんに似にてきたね。

You have come to look like your mother.

お弁当べんとうを持もってきてください。

Please bring a bento with you. (hold and come)

やっと日本語にほんごが上手じょうずになってきた。

I finally came to be better at Japanese.

私わたしはアメリカに戻もどってから、どんどん太ふとってきた。

Since coming back to America, I have rapidly come to be fatter.

君きみが出来できることが分わかってきただろう。

You have come to understand what you are capable of, haven't you?

ニュースではずっと雨あめが降ふると言いっていたけど、晴はれてきた。

The news said that it was going to keep on raining, but the weather has come to be clear.

クリスマスが近ちかづいてきたから、子供こどもたちが色々いろいろなものを欲ほしいと言いっている。

Christmas has drawn near(er), so the children are saying that they want various things. (Literally - Has come to be near)

朝あさから家いえの掃除そうじをしているので、家いえが綺麗きれいになってきています。

Since I've been cleaning the house from this morning, the house is becoming cleaner.

この商品しょうひんがだんだん安やすくなってきたから、また今度こんど買かうつもりです。

This product has gradually started to get cheaper, so I intend to buy it next time. (has come to be)
