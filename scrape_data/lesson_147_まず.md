## About

About まず

Like many other adverbs in Japanese, まず can be used to modify a whole phrase/sentence. This means that it will regularly appear at the beginning of a statement. まず is associated with kanji 先まず and therefore carries the meaning of 'first', or 'starting with'.

まず宿題しゅくだいをしたほうがいい。

First, it would be best for me to do my homework.

まずエンジンをかけて。

First, please turn on the engine.

まず is regularly used when you want to convey that something 'should' be done before anything else/discussed first, and not when something is first in rank.

赤あかい車くるまがまず、ゴールをしました。

The red car finished to start with. (Unnatural Japanese)

In this kind of sentence, it would be far more natural to use a statement like 一番いちばん, 最初さいしょ or 一位いちい if discussing rank.
Caution

When using まず, it can sound like you are giving someone advice, so be careful to use polite language, or perhaps と思おもう to show that the suggestion is just an opinion.

まず別わかれた方ほうがいいと思おもう。

I think you should break up first.

First let's go dancing.

To start with, please paint the walls.

This one first right?

To start, finish this portion. (First)

First let's go shopping then after that, shall we go out to eat?

First read the book, then let's go see the movie.

まずお湯ゆを沸わかして、野菜やさいを入いれましょう。

To start, boil some water and put in the vegetables.

行いく前まえにまず何なにかを食たべましょう。

Before we go, first let's eat something.

まず犬いぬに餌えさをやってから、掃除機そうじきをかけましょう。

To start with, I will feed the dog, and then I shall do the vacuuming.

テレビを見みたいけど、まず買かい物ものに行いかなくちゃいけない。

I want to watch TV, but first I have to go shopping.

まずひらがなとカタカナを覚おぼえてから、漢字かんじの勉強べんきょうをしましょう。

Start with memorizing hiragana and katakana, then let's move on to studying Kanji. (First)

まず、この中なかで一番いちばん好すきな飲のみ物ものを選えらんでください。

To start, please choose your favorite drink from these.
