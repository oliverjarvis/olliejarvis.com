## About

About からすると・からすれば

English

Japanese

からすると is an expression combining the conjunction particle から 'from', する 'to do', and either of the hypothetical markers と or ば. It may be translated as 'judging from (A), (B)', or 'considering (A), (B)'. However, the way that する behaves in these types of sentences is quite comparable to expressions like 'going off' in English, in that it just indicates that (A) is the basis from which the speaker is about to make their judgment.

からすると or からすれば will be seen following nouns, before the (B) part of the sentence will highlight the speaker's assessment of (A).

この成績せいせきからすると、あなたは全然ぜんぜん勉強べんきょうをしていないでしょう？

Judging from these grades, you didn't study at all, did you?

この甘あまさからすると、塩しおと間違まちがえて砂糖さとうを入いれてしまったに違ちがいない。

Judging from how sweet this is, they must've mistakenly added sugar instead of salt.

日本にほん育そだちの私わたしからすれば、これは寿司すしではない。

Going off being a person raised in Japan, this is not sushi.

彼女かのじょの反応はんのうからすると、本当ほんとうに驚おどろいているようだ。

Going off her reaction, it seems like she is genuinely surprised.

Fun-fact

In almost all cases that する is paired with a hypothetical structure like と, ば, or たら, it indicates that the speaker is 'going with' the assumption of (A) being true, before further expressing what applies based on that assumption.

あの煙突えんとつからすると、たぶん薪まきストーブですね。

Judging from that chimney, maybe it is a wood stove.

歴史れきしからすると、京都きょうとはかつて首都しゅとでした。

Judging from history, Kyoto was once the capital.

あなたが言いっていることからすると、彼かれはそこにいなかったようですね。

Judging from what you are saying, it seems that he wasn't there right?

足あしの速はやさからすると、彼かれはプロの選手せんしゅになれるだろう。

Considering how fast he is, he can probably become a professional athlete.

あのアクセントからすると彼かれは日本人にほんじんではないようです。

Judging from his accent, it appears that he is not Japanese.

あの手てさばきからすると、あの人ひとは編物あみもののプロに違ちがいない。

Judging from that handiwork, there is no doubt that that person is a knitting pro.

お辞儀じぎの仕方しかたからすると、彼かれは日本にほんにいたことがあるはずだ。

Judging from how he bowed, he is sure to have been to Japan before.

植木うえきのかんじからすれば、かなり腕うでのいい植木うえき職人しょくにんが手入ていれをしているな。

Judging from my impression of these trees, a rather skilled gardener is tending to them.

あの写うつし方かたからすると、これは有名ゆうめいな写真家しゃしんかが撮とったものだと思おもいます。

Judging from the way that it was taken, I think this was taken by a famous photographer.

この仕上しあがりからすれば、作成さくせい者しゃは大雑把おおざっぱな性格せいかくですね。

Judging from this finish, the author is someone who doesn't worry about details.

あの落おち着つきからすれば学生がくせいには見みえないけど、まだ彼かれらは中学生ちゅうがくせいなんだって。

Judging from their composure, they don't look like students, but I heard they are still junior high schoolers.

あのウエイトレスの対応たいおうからすれば、この店みせは社員しゃいん教育きょういくがしっかりしているのだろう。

Judging from my interactions with that waitress, this restaurant has a solid employee training program.
