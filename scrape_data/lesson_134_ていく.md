## About

About ていく

This grammar construction is a mix of the conjunction particle て, and the verb 行いく 'to go'. To use this grammar point, you will need to attach て to any verb, and then follow it with いく.

This nuance is usually that '(A) happened and then went', or 'will happen, and then go'. In other words, it will depend on whether いく is in present, or past tense.

今日きょうはうちで食たべていく？

Do you want to go and eat at my place tonight? (Literally 'will you', but comes across more as an invitation in casual Japanese)

今晩こんばんは雨あめが降ふると聞きいたから、傘かさを持もって行いった。

Because I heard that it was going to rain tonight, I took an umbrella.

ていく can be used when representing time, or when representing a physical place. When representing a physical place, it is more likely that the kanji structure, 行いく will be used. However, this is not a set rule, and depends on the writer.

When used in relation to time, the nuance is usually that something has already started/is about to start, and then will continue to progress in the same way.

毎日まいにちゴルフの練習れんしゅうをしたらだんだんと上手うまくなっていく。

When you practice golf everyday, you will gradually get better.

This is the opposite of てくる, which indicates that something has finally reached a certain state, after progressing in that way for some time.

Fun Fact

When compared to English, ていく is very similar to the phrase 'to go on to (A)', in relation to time. It focuses on the fact that the speaker is required to make some type of journey to arrive at a specific point. For example, 'She went on to become a doctor after university'.

Translation

大おおきい音おとで、鳥とりが飛とんでいく。

With a loud noise, the birds will fly away. (fly and go)

これを先生せんせいに持もっていってください。

Please take this to the teacher. (hold and go)

友達ともだちにプレゼントを買かっていく。

I am going to buy a present and take it to my friend. (buy and go)

大丈夫だいじょうぶ！お弁当べんとうを持もっていくから。

It's okay! Because I will take a lunch (bento) with me. (hold and go)

今日きょうはテストを受うけるから鉛筆えんぴつを持もっていく。

Because I am going to take a test today, I will take a pencil. (hold and go)

何なにか食たべていってください。

Please eat something before you go. (eat and go)

お医者いしゃさんに行いって、薬くすりをもらっていくね。

I am going to go to the doctor and get some medicine. (receive and go)

車くるまで行いくのは危険きけんなので、電車でんしゃに乗のっていってください。

It is dangerous to go by car, so please take a train. (ride and go)

服ふくを買かって、お祖母ばあさんに持もっていく。

I'm going to buy clothes and take them to grandma. (hold and go)

車くるまに乗のる前まえに、朝あさごはんを必かならず食たべていく。

Before getting in the car, I make sure to eat breakfast and then go.

今いまから日本語にほんごの勉強べんきょうをし始はじめたら、だんだん上手じょうずになっていくよ。

If you start studying Japanese now, you will go on to become more proficient.

どんどんお金かねが貯たまっていくのを見みるのが好すき。

I like to see my money go on to accumulate steadily.
