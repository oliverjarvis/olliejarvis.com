## About

About 割に

English

Japanese

As an adverb, 割わりに is usually used in a similar way to 'although', 'despite', or 'comparatively' in English.

割わりに may be used at the beginning of a sentence, or after any word/phrase that it is highlighting as being 'comparative'. When following な-Adjectives or nouns, な or の will be required.

この肉にく、高たかい割わりには味あじがないね。

Although this meat is expensive, it is not flavorful.

太ふとっている割わりには運動うんどう神経しんけいがいいんだね。

Although you are fat, you are very athletic, aren't you?

この機械きかいのパワーは強つよい割わりにめちゃくちゃ遅おそいです。

Although this machine is very powerful, it is very slow.

この部屋へやは綺麗きれいな割わりには臭くさいね。

Although this room is clean, it smells bad.

ラッシュアワーの割わりには空すいていますね。

Although it is during rush hour, the road is very clear.

割わり itself is a noun meaning 'proportionate', and is primarily used as a suffix. 割わりに may be thought of as a direct extension of this use, as 'proportionately' is its closest literal meaning.

Fun Fact

While 割わりに is often thought to make comparisons between things, it actually only ever compares something to itself (or things within the same category). The 'proportion' that is then highlighted is something that is not common within the category of (A).

Caution

Both 割わりと and 割わりに are quite common expressions. However, 割わりと is used far more often in casual speech to show that something is 'unexpectedly (A)'. It will also frequently be used as a stand-alone expression, appearing directly before the word (usually an adjective) that is of a 'considerable' amount.

これ割わりと美味おいしいよ。

This is unexpectedly tasty.

昨日きのう買かった包丁ほうちょうは割わりといい。

The knife I bought yesterday is quite good.
