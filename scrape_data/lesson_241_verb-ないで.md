## About

About Verb[ないで]

ないでください is a polite way to express 'please don't do (A)'. However, such politeness is often not required, like in conversations between close friends. In these cases, ないで by itself may be used for the same purpose. Although grammatically the same as the ないで with the meaning of 'without doing (A), (B)', rather having a part (A) and a part (B) to the sentence, this ないで acts more like a simple statement.

ないで combines the auxiliary verb ない, with the case marking particle, で. To use this structure, simply attach it to the negative stem of the verb that you wish to request someone not to do. The sentence itself usually finishes with ないで.

先生せんせい：「そこの生徒せいと、授業中じゅぎょうちゅうに寝ねないで！」

Teacher: 'The student there, please don't sleep during class!'

母ははが娘むすめに：「知しらない人ひとについていかないで！約束やくそくよ。」

Mother to her daughter: 'Please don't go with people you don't know! That's a promise.'

妻つまが夫おっとに：「貯金ちょきんをしたいから、無駄むだな物ものにお金かねを使つかわないで。」

Wife to her husband: 'I want to save up, so please don't use money recklessly.'

Since that park is dangerous, (please) don't play there.

(Please) don't eat the cake in the refrigerator yet.

食たべてからジェットコースターに乗のらないで。

(Please) don't ride a rollercoaster after eating.

「バカにしないでよ！はんだくん。」

'(Please) don't make a fool out of me! Handa.'

ここは病院びょういんなので、走はしったり、騒さわいだりしないで。

Since this is a hospital, (please) don't do things like run or be boisterous.

母親ははおやが子供こどもに言いう：「まだ熱あついから、触さわらないでね。」

Mother speaking to her child: 'It is still hot, so (please) don't touch it.'

母親ははおやが子供こどもに言いう：「いい子こだから、泣なかないでね。」

Mother speaking to her child: 'You are a good child, so (please) don't cry, okay? '

寝ねる前まえに携帯けいたいを使つかったり、おかしを食たべたりしないで。

Before going to bed, (please) don't do things like use your phone or eat candy.

明日あしたから旅行りょこうに行いくから、風邪かぜを引ひかないでね。

Since you are going on a trip tomorrow, (please) don't catch a cold.

姉あねが弟おとうとに言いう：「頼たのむから、親おやには何なにも言いわないで。」

Elder sister speaking to her younger brother: 'I am asking you, so (please) don't tell our parents.'
