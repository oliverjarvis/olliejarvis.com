## About

About に足る

English

Japanese

足たる, an う-Verb meaning 'to be sufficient', is often paired with に and either verbs or nouns in order to indicate that something is 'worthy of (A)', or 'enough for (A)'. This is quite a formal grammar structure, so is primarily seen in books, or may be used in speeches.

に足たる will follow verbs in their dictionary form, or nouns. As 足たる itself will always appear in the dictionary form, it must be followed by a second noun which it will be modifying as the thing that is or is not worthy of something.

彼かれはこの試合しあいに勝かつに足たる能力のうりょくはないだろう。

He is probably not worthy to win in this competition.

信頼しんらいに足たる友達ともだちに、家族かぞくのことをすべて話はなした。

I told my trustworthy friend everything about my family.

Caution

に足たる may come after する, or immediately follow the noun that would usually have する added to it. The primary difference is that に足たる following する will put more emphasis on the action of (A).

お腹なかはいっぱいにならなかったものの、満足まんぞくに足たる量りょうだった。

Even though I did not get full, it was enough to satisfy me.

お腹なかはいっぱいにならなかったものの、満足まんぞくするに足たる量りょうだった。

Even though I did not get full, it was enough to satisfy me.

Caution

When wanting to say that something is 'not worth (A)', に足たる will not appear as に足たりない. In these cases, the following verb will need to be negated, not 足たる itself.

あの先生せんせいは尊敬そんけいに足たりない先生せんせいだと思おもう。

I think that teacher is not worthy of respect. (Unnatural Japanese)

あの先生せんせいは尊敬そんけいに足たる先生せんせいではないと思おもう。

I think that teacher is not worthy of respect.
