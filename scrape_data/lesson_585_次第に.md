## About

About 次第に

English

Japanese

次第しだいに is an adverb that expresses that something is happening 'gradually' or 'bit by bit' over time. This phrase is made up of the noun 次第しだい, 'course of events' or 'circumstances', and the particle に, which turns it into an adverbial phrase.

While the noun 次第しだい implies a sequence or order, when used as the adverb 次第しだいに, the main focus shifts to how something progressively and gradually changes over time.

わずか数ヶ月すうかげつのうちに二人ふたりは次第しだいに疎遠そえんになった。

Within a few months, the two gradually drifted apart.

次第しだいに can be used for both positive and negative changes.

ガソリンの価格かかくは次第しだいに安やすくなると予測よそくされている。

Gasoline prices are expected to gradually become cheaper.

友人ゆうじんの祖父そふの健康状態けんこうじょうたいが次第しだいに悪化あっかしていることも知しらずに、変へんなことを聞きいてしまった。

I asked him such insensitive questions, unaware that his grandfather’s health was gradually deteriorating.

だんだん and 次第しだいに both mean the same thing however 次第しだいに sounds slightly more formal and is more likely to be seen in formal written language than だんだん.

この対策たいさくにより、次第しだいに経済けいざいが回復かいふくすると予想よそうされています。

This measure is expected to gradually restore the economy.

なんか最近さいきん物価ぶっかがだんだん上あがってきたね。

Don't you think prices of things have been gradually going up?
