## About

About ところを

English

Japanese

Being a combination of the noun ところ 'place' or 'situation', and the adverbial particle を, ところを is a grammar construction that will be used in order to express 2 primary meanings. The first is 'in spite of (A)', or 'regardless of (A)'. This nuance of ところ will be that the speaker is politely acknowledging the situation that someone else is in, and apologizing for interrupting them in that moment.

ところを may come after any structure that could pair with a noun. This means verbs in their standard forms, い-Adjectives, な-Adjectives followed by な, or nouns followed by の.

先輩せんぱい、休やすんでいるところを申もうし訳わけないんですけど、エクセルの使つかい方かたを教おしえてください。

Senpai, I'm sorry to bother you while you are on vacation, but could you please teach me how to use Excel?

お忙いそがしいところをすみませんが、相談そうだんしたいことがあるので少すこしだけお時間じかんよろしいですか。

I am sorry to bother you while you are busy, but I want some advice. May I have some of your time?

ご多忙たぼうなところをご対応たいおういただきありがとうございました。

Thank you for taking time out in spite of you being busy.

お急いそぎのところを恐縮きょうしゅくですが、何時なんじぐらいに向むこうに着つくか教おしえてもらってもよろしいでしょうか。

I'm sorry to bother you when you are in a hurry, but may I ask what time you expect to arrive over there?

The second meaning that ところを is often used for will simply be 'when (A)', or 'at the time of (A)', with a focus on being a situation in which the action of (A) is interrupted by (B). This may have either a positive or negative nuance.

This use of ところを will appear exclusively after verbs in any of their standard forms.

警察けいさつに逃にげるところを目撃もくげきされた。

I was seen by the police while I was fleeing.

母ははに悪戯いたずらをしているところを見みられてしまった。

My mother caught me while I was playing a prank.

彼かれは強盗ごうとうをしていたところを周まわりの人ひとに目撃もくげきされていたので、どこにも逃にげられなかった。

Since he was seen when he was robbing the store, he had nowhere to run.

The を itself may be omitted when used to express either of these meanings.

Fun Fact

ところを is occasionally split into two different grammar points depending on the source. However, it is important to remember that the underlying meaning of both of these is the same. ところ marks the place or situation, while を marks how that situation is being utilized either positively or negatively. When it comes to apologizing, the speaker themselves is the one 'using' that moment with を, and is therefore acknowledging that.

先生せんせいからの返信へんしんのお礼れいを言いう：「お忙いそがしいところ、ご返信へんしんありがとうございます。」

Expressing gratitude to a teacher for their reply: 'In spite of being busy, thank you for your reply.'

目上めうえの人ひとに尋たずねる：「お急いそぎのところ恐縮きょうしゅくですが、このプロジェクトについてのご意見いけんをお聞きかせ願ねがえませんか。」

Asking a superior: 'I hate to trouble you when you are in such a hurry, but could you let me hear your opinion about this project?' (in spite of)

メール：「ご病気びょうき中ちゅうのところ恐おそれ入いりますが、田中たなか先生せんせいの携帯けいたいの番号ばんごうを教おしえていただけますでしょうか。」

Email: 'I am awfully sorry to trouble you when you are ill, but could you tell me Mr. Tanaka's cell number?' (in spite of)

駅えきの放送ほうそう：「この電車でんしゃは、三さん分ぷん遅おくれて到着とうちゃくいたします。お急いそぎのところ、ご迷惑めいわくをおかけいたします。」

Station announcement: 'This train will arrive three minutes late. We apologize for the inconvenience while you are in a rush.' (in spite of)

会議かいぎでの冒頭ぼうとうの挨拶あいさつ：「皆様みなさま、本日ほんじつはご多忙たぼうのところお越こしいただき、ありがとうございます。」

Greetings on the outset of a conference: 'Ladies and gentlemen, thank you very much for coming today despite being so busy.' (in spite of)

容疑者ようぎしゃ：「コロンボ警部けいぶ、仕事中しごとちゅうですが・・・」
コロンボ警部けいぶ：「そこのところ、もうすこしお話はなししていただけませんか？」

Suspect: 'Inspector Columbo, I am in the middle of work…'
Inspector Columbo: 'Despite that, would you mind speaking with me a little further?' (in spite of)

お楽たのしみのところ邪魔じゃまして申もうし訳わけありませんが、何なにが原因げんいんで笑わらっているのですか？

I apologize for being a bother while you're having fun, but what's the cause of your laughter?

お取とり込こみ中ちゅうのところ失礼しつれいだけど、何なにを必死ひっしになって望遠鏡ぼうえんきょうを覗のぞき込こんでいるの？

Sorry for the impoliteness while you're in the middle of it, but why are you so frantically peering through your telescope?

お休やすみのところを申もうし訳わけございませんが、少々しょうしょうお話はなしを聞きいてもらっても良いいですか？

I apologize for interrupting in spite of you resting, but do you mind if I have you tell me something quickly?

店長てんちょう「お暑あついところをこちらまでご来店らいてん頂いただき、誠まことに有難ありがとう御座ございます。」

Store manager: 'Thank you so terribly much for visiting this store when it is so hot!'

ご多忙たぼうのところ恐縮きょうしゅくですが、私わたしが先さきほど言いった件けんについて考かんがえてくれましたでしょうか？

I am being ashamed to interrupt you while you are busy, but I wonder if you have had time to consider the matter I spoke to you about some time ago?

お忙いそがしいところわざわざ私わたしのために駅えきまで会あいにきてくれて、本当ほんとうに有難ありがとうね！

Expressly coming to the station to meet me while you are busy, thank you so much, really!
