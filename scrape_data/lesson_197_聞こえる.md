## About

About 聞こえる

聞きこえる is a verb that is often used to describe things that can be heard, or the way in which something is heard. Because of this, the most common translations of this verb are 'to be audible', or 'to sound like (A)'. 聞きこえる is an intransitive verb, and literally means 'to give off sound'.

When using 聞きこえる, (A) will always be followed by が. (A) is considered to be the 'source' of the sound (a noun). However, the 'way that something sounds' will be marked adverbially. This means that an い-Adjective will be changed to its く form, and な-Adjectives/nouns will be followed by に.

車くるまの音おとが聞きこえる。

I can hear the sound of a car.

アニメに出でてくる女おんなの子この声こえは可愛かわいく聞きこえるけど、声優せいゆうはいつもおばさんだ。

Voices of girls that appear in anime sound cute, but the voice actor is always an old lady.

こう歌うたうと上手じょうずに聞きこえる。

If you sing like this, you will sound good.

電車でんしゃの音おとのように聞きこえるけどあれは隣となりの人ひとのいびきだ。

It sounds like the noise from a train, but it's the neighbor's snoring.

Fun Fact

The primary difference between 聞きこえる, and 聞きける (the potential form of 聞きく), is that 聞きこえる is used to identify things that do not require the active concentration of the listener to be heard. In other words, ambient noise. However, 聞きける is used when the speaker is trying to concentrate on some specific sound, and refers to their 'ability' to hear it.

静しずかな部屋へやにいると聞きこえるよ。

You can hear it if you are in a quiet room. (Without effort)

静しずかな部屋へやにいると聞きけるよ。

If you are in a quiet room, you can hear it. (If you focus and try to hear the sound)

「今いまのノック聞きこえた？」
「ううん、聞きこえなかったよ。空耳そらみみじゃない？」

'Did you hear a knock just now?'
'Nope, I didn't hear it. Aren't you hearing things?'

「西山にしやまさんは生うまれつき耳みみが聞きこえないんです。」

'Nishiyama has been deaf since birth.'

隣となりのセキくんはうるさすぎるわよ。彼かれが音楽おんがくを聴きくときいつもあたしのアパートに聞きこえるわ！

My neighbor Seki is too loud! Whenever he listens to music, I can hear it in my flat!

「教師きょうしの声こえが小ちいさすぎるから何なにも聞きこえないんだよ。」

'The teacher is too quiet, so I cannot hear anything.'

「外そとがうるさいからテレビの音おとがよく聞きこえない。」

'Since it is loud outside, I cannot hear the TV very well.'

「滝たきの音おとはすごく大おおきくてここからでも聞きこえます！」

'The sound of the waterfall is really loud, and we can hear it even from here!'

子牛こうしは生うまれた直後ちょくごから耳みみが聞きこえるが、子猫こねこは聞きこえない。

A calf can hear from birth, but kittens cannot (hear).

試験官しけんかん：「皆みなさん、録音ろくおんがハッキリ聞きこえますか？」

Examiner: 'Can everybody hear the recording well?'

彼女かのじょ：「最近さいきん、私わたし、パレオを買かったの。」
彼氏かれし：「パレオ？なんだ、それ？食たべ物ものの名前なまえのように聞きこえるぞ。」

Girlfriend: 'Recently, I bought pareo.'
Boyfriend: 'Pareo? What is that? It sounds like the name of a food.'

「この店みせの中なかだと何なにも聞きこえないけど、階段かいだんを上あがってロビーに行いけば、電話でんわがよく聞きこえるよ。」

'I can't hear anything inside this shop, but if I go upstairs to the lobby, I can hear you well on the phone.'

彼かれはミサカとシライだけに聞きこえるように小声こごえで言いった。

He spoke in a low voice so that only Misaka and Shirai could hear him.

「今いまの音おと、僕ぼくには小ちいさな足音あしおと(のよう)に聞きこえたよ。」

'The sound just now sounded like small footsteps to me.'
